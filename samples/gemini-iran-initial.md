# **Operation Epic Fury and the Strategic Calculus of United States Entanglement in Iran: A Comprehensive Assessment**

## **Executive Overview of the March 2026 Crisis**

The initiation of joint United States and Israeli military strikes against the Islamic Republic of Iran on February 28, 2026, marks a profound inflection point in the geopolitical and security architecture of the Middle East. Designated "Operation Epic Fury" by the United States Department of Defense and aligned with Israel's "Operation Roaring Lion," the campaign represents the most significant American combat operation in the region since the 2003 invasion of Iraq.1 The coordinated assault achieved a primary strategic objective via a decapitation strike that successfully eliminated Iranian Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, alongside dozens of senior military commanders, fundamentally destabilizing the command and control apparatus of the Iranian state.3

The immediate aftermath of these strikes has thrust the region into a state of severe, multidimensional conflict. The Iranian response has been swift, geographically expansive, and asymmetric, utilizing a combination of direct ballistic missile strikes on Israeli territory, attacks on regional Arab states hosting United States military installations, and the mobilization of proxy forces across the "Axis of Resistance".1 Concurrently, the global macroeconomic environment has experienced immediate shocks, characterized by severe disruptions in international aviation corridors and acute volatility in global energy markets due to Iranian threats to close the Strait of Hormuz.7

A central analytical dilemma now confronts strategic planners and policymakers: determining the trajectory and ultimate depth of United States military involvement. This report exhaustively evaluates the crisis across a spectrum of potential United States entanglement, ranging from a strictly sustained limited engagement utilizing offshore balancing and air superiority, to the perilous probability of a prolonged regional quagmire necessitating conventional "boots on the ground." The analysis indicates that while the current force posture and domestic political constraints heavily favor a limited, standoff engagement strategy, the structural fragility of the Iranian regime, the potential for state collapse, and the unpredictable escalation ladder of regional proxies introduce profound risks of involuntary mission creep.9

## **The Strategic Prelude: Diplomatic Collapse and the Accumulation of Combat Power**

The military intervention of February 2026 was not a spontaneous eruption of hostilities but the culmination of protracted diplomatic failures, escalating domestic Iranian unrest, and an unprecedented buildup of American military power in the United States Central Command (CENTCOM) area of responsibility.

### **The Breakdown of the Geneva and Oman Negotiations**

In the months preceding the strikes, the United States and Iran engaged in high-stakes nuclear negotiations in Geneva, mediated by Oman. The talks aimed to address Iran's accelerating nuclear enrichment program, which had continued unabated following the limited June 2025 "Twelve-Day War" and the United States' corresponding "Operation Midnight Hammer".11 However, the diplomatic channels collapsed on February 17, 2026, when Iranian negotiators rejected maximalist American demands. United States officials required Iran to completely dismantle its deeply buried nuclear facilities at Fordow, Natanz, and Esfahan, deliver all enriched uranium to the United States, and agree to a permanent, non-expiring treaty without sunset clauses.14 Iran refused to destroy its nuclear infrastructure or ship its uranium abroad, viewing the demands as a capitulation of its sovereign rights.14 Following this deadlock, President Donald Trump warned of "major combat operations" and a willingness to "raze their missile industry to the ground".11

### **The Domestic Catalyst: The 2025-2026 Iranian Protests**

The external diplomatic pressure was mirrored by profound internal instability within the Islamic Republic. Beginning in December 2025, Iran was engulfed in the largest popular uprising since the 1979 Islamic Revolution.16 Driven by extreme socio-economic stress—including 48.6% overall inflation, 64% food inflation, severe water shortages, and a collapsing currency—the protests quickly morphed into a systemic challenge to the theocracy.1 The regime's response, ordered directly by Supreme Leader Khamenei, was characterized by extreme brutality. Security forces utilized live ammunition, resulting in mass casualties. While the Iranian government confirmed 3,117 deaths, human rights organizations and activists estimated the death toll at between 16,500 and 36,500 civilians.1 This domestic massacre severely eroded the regime's internal legitimacy and provided a humanitarian pretext for the impending United States and Israeli intervention, which President Trump framed as an effort to prevent further killings and allow Iranians to "take over your government".3

### **The Architecture of the United States Military Buildup**

Anticipating the collapse of diplomacy and the necessity of military intervention, the United States executed its largest military buildup in the Middle East in over two decades.18 This deployment was specifically engineered to execute a massive, multi-day air campaign while minimizing the exposure of ground forces to Iranian retaliation.19

## **Phase I: Operation Epic Fury and the Execution of Regime Decapitation**

On the morning of February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched their preemptive war against the Islamic Republic. The operation was not a limited, punitive exchange; it was a comprehensive regime disruption campaign designed to systematically blind the Iranian military and decapitate its political leadership.1

### **Cybernetic Synchronization and Digital Suppression**

The kinetic strikes were inextricably linked to a massive, coordinated cyber warfare offensive. Before the first munitions impacted their targets, Iranian command and control networks experienced a catastrophic failure. Global internet monitoring organizations, including NetBlocks, reported that nationwide internet traffic in Iran plunged to merely four percent of normal levels.26 Official state news outlets, government digital services, and secure communications systems utilized by the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) were taken offline.26 Western intelligence sources indicated that this digital blackout was engineered to paralyze the IRGC's ability to coordinate air defenses, communicate with dispersed missile units, and organize an immediate counter-offensive.26

### **The Kinetic Bombardment: Munitions and Targets**

With Iranian early warning systems blinded, the kinetic phase of Operation Epic Fury commenced. The strikes utilized a combination of high-end stealth aviation and advanced standoff munitions. The United States military confirmed the inaugural combat deployment of the LUCAS (Low-Cost Unmanned Combat Attack System) kamikaze drones, manufactured by Spektreworks, which were utilized alongside sea-launched Tomahawk Land Attack Missiles.28 Israeli forces, participating under the banner of Operation Roaring Lion, deployed over 200 aircraft, dropping more than 550 munitions on over 500 targets.25

The target matrix prioritized the political and security nerve centers of the regime. The first wave struck the presidential palace, the Ministry of Defense, the Ministry of Intelligence, and the Atomic Energy Organization headquarters.1 Explosions were confirmed across Tehran, Isfahan, Qom, Karaj, Kermanshah, Tabriz, and Bandar Abbas.25 Preemptive strikes were heavily concentrated on nuclear facilities in Natanz, Fordow, Isfahan, and Arak, targeting subterranean infrastructure that satellite imagery indicated was being hardened against conventional bunker-buster munitions.1

### **The Assassination of the Theocratic Vanguard**

The strategic nucleus of Operation Epic Fury was the targeted assassination of the regime's upper echelon. The operation achieved its most significant objective with the confirmed death of the 86-year-old Supreme Leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei.3 Khamenei's death removes the singular, unifying figurehead who had wielded ultimate, unquestioned authority over Iran's military, nuclear program, judiciary, and proxy networks for thirty-seven years.31

The decapitation strike extended far beyond the Supreme Leader. The Israel Defense Forces confirmed that the opening minute of the operation eliminated approximately forty senior Iranian officials.3 Confirmed casualties include IRGC Commander-in-Chief Mohammad Pakpour, Khamenei's top security advisor Ali Shamkhani, the Iranian Defense Minister, and former President Mahmoud Ahmadinejad, whose residence in the Narmak district of Tehran was leveled.25 This catastrophic loss of leadership has induced a profound shock to Tehran's decision-making mechanisms, exploiting the inherent vulnerability of authoritarian regimes that rely on highly centralized power structures.25

### **Collateral Damage and Humanitarian Impact**

Despite the utilization of precision-guided munitions, the geographic integration of Iranian military installations within dense urban environments resulted in significant civilian casualties. The Human Rights Activists News Agency (HRANA) reported that at least 133 civilians were killed and over 200 injured during the initial Saturday strikes.35 The most devastating mass casualty event occurred in the southern city of Minab, where a suspected United States-Israeli missile strike targeted an IRGC barracks situated immediately adjacent to a girls' elementary school, resulting in the deaths of 148 people and wounding 95\.35 This extensive collateral damage complicates the strategic narrative, simultaneously terrifying the civilian populace and providing the regime with potent propaganda to fuel anti-Western sentiment among its loyalist base.

## **Phase II: Iranian Institutional Survival and Domestic Fracture**

The decapitation of the Islamic Republic's leadership has precipitated an acute constitutional and administrative crisis. The survival of the state apparatus now depends on its ability to manage a chaotic succession process while suppressing a highly mobilized domestic opposition.

### **The Emergency Leadership Council and Succession Friction**

In an effort to prevent immediate administrative dissolution, the Iranian government activated Article 111 of the Iranian Constitution, establishing a temporary, three-member Leadership Council to assume the duties of the Supreme Leader.36 This interim body comprises President Masoud Pezeshkian, Judiciary Chief Gholamhossein Mohseni Ejei, and Ayatollah Alireza Arafi, a senior cleric serving as the jurist member from the Guardian Council.38

However, this facade of constitutional continuity masks profound internal friction. Intelligence sources indicate that the surviving elements of the IRGC command structure, panicked by the ongoing airstrikes and fearful of domestic uprisings, are pressuring the political establishment to bypass legally prescribed procedures.40 With the Assembly of Experts unable to convene safely due to the continuous bombardment, the IRGC is pushing for an extra-constitutional, immediate appointment of a permanent successor to project military strength and deter internal fragmentation.40 The regime is acutely aware that the "talent shortage" created by the assassination of forty top officials leaves the state highly vulnerable to systemic collapse.37

### **The Mobilization of the Iranian Opposition**

The response of the Iranian populace to the strikes has been deeply polarized. While state media broadcast images of loyalists mourning the "martyrdom" of Khamenei, significant segments of the urban population engaged in impromptu celebrations.32 Despite renewed internet blackouts and the heavy deployment of security forces, footage emerged of citizens celebrating in Karaj, Qazvin, Shiraz, Isfahan, and Sanandaj, viewing the foreign military intervention as an opportunity for political liberation.30 In southern Iran, crowds toppled a monument dedicated to the Islamic Republic's founder, Ayatollah Ruhollah Khomeini.30

Exiled opposition groups have capitalized on the regime's weakness. Reza Pahlavi, the United States-based son of the former Shah and a central figure in the National Union for Democracy in Iran (NUFDI), characterized the joint United States-Israeli strikes as a "humanitarian intervention" and urged Iranians to prepare for a final uprising to reclaim the country.30 Pahlavi's organization claims to have established secure communication channels that have facilitated the defection of over 50,000 regime officials and insiders, suggesting a deeply compromised internal security apparatus.44 The United States strategy implicitly relies on this domestic opposition to organically overthrow the remaining elements of the theocracy, a massive strategic gamble absent the introduction of foreign ground troops.10

## **Phase III: Asymmetric Retaliation and the Expansion of the Theater**

Recognizing its inability to defeat the United States Air Force and Navy in a symmetrical conventional engagement, the Iranian Ministry of Defense and the IRGC rapidly activated "Operation Truth Promise 4," launching a theater-wide asymmetric retaliatory campaign.25 This response has utilized Iran's extensive arsenal of ballistic missiles and its deeply entrenched network of proxy militias to exact a toll on the United States and its regional allies.

### **Direct Ballistic Strikes on Israel and Gulf States**

The Iranian retaliation immediately escalated beyond tit-for-tat exchanges, targeting civilian and military infrastructure across the Middle East. Ballistic missiles penetrated Israeli airspace, causing significant damage. In Beit Shemesh, a city west of Jerusalem, an Iranian missile strike on a residential neighborhood killed at least nine Israelis and injured over two dozen.34

More consequentially for the broader geopolitical alignment, Iran launched missiles at sovereign Arab states hosting United States military installations. Facilities in the United Arab Emirates, Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait, and Jordan were targeted.6 The United Arab Emirates defense ministry reported three deaths and 58 injuries resulting from these strikes.35 In Bahrain, an Iranian strike hit the Crowne Plaza Hotel in Manama, forcing the emergency evacuation of United States Embassy personnel, while other projectiles landed within a few hundred yards of 300 British troops stationed in the country.35 This indiscriminate targeting of Gulf states has fundamentally altered regional security calculations, prompting Saudi Arabia, Bahrain, and the UAE to explicitly condemn Iran by name, effectively terminating the 2023 Chinese-brokered diplomatic rapprochement and forcing the Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) into a tighter defensive embrace with the United States.1

### **Activation of the Axis of Resistance**

The IRGC's Quds Force has mobilized its proxy network to initiate a protracted war of attrition against Western interests. This "Axis of Resistance" views the conflict not as an isolated national defense, but as a comprehensive regional struggle.25

* **Lebanon:** Hezbollah, despite suffering the loss of its senior leadership in late 2024 and 2025, has vowed to confront the "American-Zionist aggression" and has resumed firing on northern Israel.5  
* **Yemen:** Intelligence has detected preparations by the Houthi movement to resume high-intensity attacks on commercial shipping and United States naval vessels in the Red Sea and Gulf of Aden.1  
* **Iraq and Syria:** Iranian-backed Iraqi Shiite militias, operating under the umbrella of the Islamic Resistance in Iraq, have declared United States military sites as legitimate targets. Kataib Hezbollah explicitly threatened a "protracted war of attrition" and warned the Kurdistan Regional Government against supporting the United States, threatening to attack Erbil if Kurdish territory is utilized as a launchpad.14

### **First Blood: United States Military Casualties**

The assumption that a standoff aerial campaign guarantees total force protection for American personnel was tragically disproven. On March 1, 2026, the United States Central Command (CENTCOM) confirmed that three United States service members were killed in action and five others were seriously wounded during the initial waves of Iranian retaliatory combat operations.29 These represent the first confirmed American fatalities of Operation Epic Fury. While specific details regarding the exact location of their deaths were withheld pending next-of-kin notification, the casualties underscore the inherent risks of initiating major combat operations against a heavily armed regional power possessing advanced ballistic and drone capabilities.

## **Phase IV: Domestic Constraints, War Powers, and the Political Calculus**

The prosecution of Operation Epic Fury faces profound, structural opposition within the United States domestic political environment. President Donald Trump's decision to launch a massive military campaign aimed at regime change without formal congressional authorization has precipitated an immediate constitutional crisis.48

### **The Congressional War Powers Revolt**

Legislators from both sides of the aisle, recognizing the potential for a spiraling, open-ended conflict, have moved to restrict the executive branch. Representatives Ro Khanna and Thomas Massie, alongside Senator Tim Kaine, have drafted War Powers Act resolutions aimed at forcing a floor vote to halt the military engagement.48 Democratic leaders, including Senate Minority Leader Charles Schumer and House Minority Leader Hakeem Jeffries, demanded that Congress return immediately to Washington to assert its constitutional authority over declarations of war.50 Critics, including Senator Chris Van Hollen, have publicly denounced the strikes as an "illegal, regime-change war" that lacks a coherent post-conflict stabilization plan.48 While similar War Powers resolutions have historically failed to overcome presidential vetoes, the current effort reflects a deep, bipartisan anxiety regarding the lack of an exit strategy.48

### **Public Opinion and the "Forever War" Aversion**

The congressional revolt is heavily buttressed by a war-weary American electorate. Public opinion polling conducted by YouGov and the AP-NORC center immediately prior to the strikes revealed deep-seated national skepticism regarding military entanglement in Iran. Only 27% of Americans supported using military force against Iran, while nearly half (49%) strongly or somewhat opposed such actions.51 Furthermore, a significant plurality (48%) disapproved of the administration's specific handling of the Iranian crisis.51

Voters fear the human and economic toll of a prolonged Middle Eastern quagmire.52 Representative Jason Crow articulated this sentiment, noting that working-class Americans disproportionately bear the burden of foreign interventions.52 This intense, data-backed domestic opposition acts as an insurmountable political barrier to any strategic proposal involving the deployment of conventional ground troops, forcing military planners to rely entirely on standoff capabilities.

## **Phase V: Geoeconomic Shockwaves and the Global Energy Crisis**

The military confrontation has instantaneously transcended regional borders, inflicting severe shocks upon the global macroeconomic system. Acknowledging its inability to match American conventional military power, Iran has aggressively weaponized its geographical dominance over the Strait of Hormuz, the world's most critical energy transit chokepoint.

### **The Weaponization of the Strait of Hormuz**

Within hours of the initial United States and Israeli strikes, the IRGC Navy issued formal warnings to global maritime operators that transit through the Strait of Hormuz was strictly prohibited, effectively attempting to impose a naval blockade.7 Rear Admiral Alireza Tangsiri, commander of the IRGC Navy, declared that "full control over the Strait of Hormuz is in our hands," highlighting the deployment of fast-attack missile boats (such as the 110-knot Heydar-110), coastal defense cruise missiles, and drone carriers designed to swarm and destroy commercial shipping and opposing naval vessels.54

The immediate consequence of this threat has been catastrophic for global logistics. Over 150 commercial vessels, including massive crude oil and liquefied natural gas (LNG) tankers, dropped anchor in the Gulf of Oman, halting the transit of up to 15 million barrels of oil per day—representing roughly 20% of global daily supply.7 A tanker that attempted to ignore the blockade was subsequently attacked and set ablaze off the coast of Oman.7

This disruption has cascaded severely into the international aviation sector. Unprecedented airspace closures over Iran, Iraq, Syria, Kuwait, Bahrain, and Israel forced the abrupt cancellation of over 3,400 flights.8 Global transit hubs, including Dubai (the world's busiest international airport), Abu Dhabi, and Doha, were essentially paralyzed. Major carriers such as Emirates, Qatar Airways, and Etihad suspended operations, leaving hundreds of thousands of passengers stranded and severely disrupting global supply chains.8

### **The OPEC+ Intervention and Inflationary Pressures**

The threat to the Strait of Hormuz triggered immediate panic in the commodities markets. Brent crude, the global oil benchmark, surged by nearly 10% to $73 per barrel, a seven-month high.57 Economic analysts from Barclays and the Royal Bank of Canada (RBC) warned that a sustained closure of the strait or a prolonged conflict could rapidly drive crude prices past $100 per barrel.7 Capital Economics estimated that a $100-per-barrel scenario would add 0.6 to 0.7 percentage points to global inflation, threatening to induce a severe economic downturn in developed economies already struggling with cost-of-living pressures.7

In a desperate bid to stabilize the markets, the Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries and its allies (OPEC+), led by Saudi Arabia and Russia, convened an emergency virtual meeting on March 1, 2026\. The eight core nations (the "Voluntary Eight") agreed in principle to resume oil production increases, committing to add 206,000 barrels per day to the market beginning in April.58

The burden of securing these shipping lanes falls entirely upon the United States Navy, drastically increasing the probability of direct, sustained maritime combat in the Persian Gulf.64

## **The Spectrum of Entanglement: Assessing the Odds of US Ground Operations**

The core analytical mandate of this report is to evaluate the probability distribution across a spectrum of United States military entanglement, contrasting the likelihood of a limited, offshore engagement against the potential for a prolonged regional quagmire culminating in the deployment of ground troops. President Trump's explicit exhortation for the Iranian populace to "take over your government" firmly establishes regime change as the ultimate strategic intent of Operation Epic Fury.3 However, the methodology selected to achieve this objective dictates the depth of military commitment.

### **Pole 1: Sustained Limited Engagement via Offshore Balancing (High Probability: 60-70%)**

The most statistically probable scenario, supported by the current United States force posture and immense domestic political constraints, is a sustained campaign of limited engagement relying strictly on air, naval, and cyber superiority.9

Under this operational paradigm, the United States will persist with the methodology of Operation Epic Fury. The strategy seeks to systematically dismantle Iran's ballistic missile infrastructure, degrade its air defenses, neutralize its naval capacity in the Persian Gulf, and continuously eliminate emerging IRGC leadership nodes—all from standoff distances. The United States military will rely on its massive armada of carrier strike groups and over 330 forward-deployed aircraft, explicitly avoiding the insertion of conventional ground infantry onto Iranian soil.10

The strategic logic underpinning this approach relies heavily on proxy warfare and domestic Iranian insurrection. By applying maximum external kinetic pressure and shattering the state's monopoly on violence, Washington hopes to enable domestic opposition groups (such as the NUFDI and supporters of Reza Pahlavi) to organically overthrow the weakened theocracy.30

**Drivers of this Probability:**

1. **Domestic Political Veto:** As established, the profound domestic unpopularity of foreign wars and the immediate invocation of the War Powers Act heavily disincentivize any administration from deploying troops. The political cost of significant American casualties is considered prohibitive.50  
2. **Military Insufficiency for Invasion:** While massive in terms of strike capability, the current CENTCOM deployment is mathematically and logistically insufficient to execute a conventional ground invasion and subsequent occupation of a mountainous nation of 90 million people.1  
3. **Historical Template:** The operational doctrine mirrors the 1999 Kosovo campaign or the 2011 intervention in Libya, where Western airpower enabled local insurgencies to achieve regime change without a long-term occupying force.3

While the odds of the United States *attempting* to maintain this limited posture are overwhelmingly high, defense analysts caution that relying exclusively on airpower to achieve total government collapse has a historically poor track record, often resulting in protracted, bloody stalemates rather than swift, decisive victories.9

### **Pole 2: The Regional Quagmire and War of Attrition (Moderate Probability: 20-30%)**

If the limited air campaign fails to trigger rapid, systemic regime collapse, the conflict is highly likely to devolve into a medium-entanglement quagmire. This scenario emerges not from American strategic design, but from the grim necessity of managing Iranian asymmetric retaliation and protecting global economic lifelines.

In this scenario, the IRGC survives the initial decapitation strikes by dispersing its remaining command structures into deeply hardened, subterranean facilities. The regime then doubles down on its asymmetric warfare doctrine. The closure of the Strait of Hormuz transitions from a temporary disruption to a sustained operational reality. To prevent global economic collapse due to $100+ oil prices, the United States Navy is forced to implement a permanent, high-intensity convoy protection mission, engaging in daily maritime skirmishes against IRGC swarm boats, sea mines, and coastal defense cruise missiles.64

Concurrently, the "Axis of Resistance" inflicts a slow bleed on United States forward operating bases. Facilities in Iraq, Syria, and the Gulf States face continuous barrages from proxy militias. The United States is forced into a reactive cycle of attrition, conducting continuous airstrikes in Yemen to suppress the Houthis, in Lebanon to support Israeli operations, and in Iraq against groups like Kataib Hezbollah.14

The escalation dynamics in this scenario are largely out of Washington's control. If Iran or its proxies successfully sink a United States warship, or inflict mass casualties on an American base in the Gulf (building upon the initial three fatalities confirmed on March 1), the political pressure within the United States for a dramatic, sustained escalation will become irresistible, dragging the military into a multi-year regional policing action devoid of a clear exit strategy.65

### **Pole 3: State Collapse and "Boots on the Ground" (Low Probability: 5-10%, Catastrophic Impact)**

The extreme end of the entanglement spectrum involves the deployment of conventional United States ground forces—"boots on the ground"—to Iranian territory. A premeditated, 2003-style mechanized invasion of Iran is currently assessed as having a near-zero probability. However, a reactive, involuntary deployment of specialized ground troops remains a distinct, high-impact tail risk.10

This perilous scenario paradoxically emerges from the prospect of catastrophic success. If Operation Epic Fury actually achieves its stated goal and the Islamic Republic completely collapses, the subsequent power vacuum would be profoundly destabilizing. Iran is a complex tapestry of ethnic groups, including Persians, Kurds, Azeris, Balochs, and Ahwazis.1 The removal of the central theocratic authority, combined with the destruction of the IRGC's internal security apparatus, could rapidly precipitate a multi-factional civil war reminiscent of post-Gaddafi Libya, but on a vastly larger and more dangerous scale.

**Catalysts for Involuntary Ground Deployment:**

1. **Securing Weapons of Mass Destruction (WMD):** Iran possesses a highly advanced nuclear infrastructure and vast stockpiles of enriched uranium. If the state collapses and IRGC command dissolves, the risk of fissile material or advanced ballistic missile technology falling into the hands of rogue sectarian militias or transnational terrorist networks becomes an unacceptable global security threat. The United States military, utilizing rapid-deployment units such as the 82nd Airborne Division or Marine Expeditionary Units (MEUs), could be forced to conduct rapid, highly kinetic ground incursions to secure these strategic sites.18  
2. **Mitigating Humanitarian Catastrophe:** A collapse of the state distribution networks in a country already suffering from severe food inflation and water shortages would trigger a humanitarian crisis of historic proportions.1 The resulting creation of millions of refugees would severely destabilize neighboring borders (including Iraq, Turkey, and Pakistan). International pressure could mount for a United States-led stabilization force to secure humanitarian corridors.  
3. **Preventing Proxy State Fragmentation:** If neighboring adversaries—such as Russian intelligence networks or transnational jihadist organizations—attempt to carve out spheres of influence in a fractured Iran, the United States might feel compelled to insert special operations forces to back preferred local proxies, leading to classic, incremental mission creep.10

Expert consensus firmly advises against "owning the post-conflict Iranian landscape." There is no comprehensive inter-agency planning project for the stabilization of a post-Khamenei Iran.9 Therefore, any introduction of boots on the ground would be a desperate, reactive measure rather than a planned strategic occupation. While the overall probability remains low, the risk of specialized, limited-duration ground incursions rises proportionally with the likelihood of total regime collapse.

## **Strategic Outlook and Geopolitical Implications**

The joint United States and Israeli strategy relies heavily on the precarious assumption that extreme kinetic pressure and leadership decapitation will induce a rapid, manageable transition of power catalyzed by the Iranian populace. However, this assumption significantly underestimates the entrenched, heavily armed nature of the surviving IRGC factions and the historical resilience of the resistance axis.

The immediate outlook suggests a highly volatile, protracted air and naval campaign. The global economy must rapidly price in a sustained risk premium on energy commodities, as the Strait of Hormuz is guaranteed to remain a highly contested, militarized zone. Furthermore, the strategic alignment of Russia and China—both of whom immediately condemned the assassination of Khamenei as a "cynical murder" and a violation of international law—ensures that the surviving elements of the Iranian regime will receive diplomatic cover, intelligence sharing, and potentially clandestine material support to sustain their war effort.45

Ultimately, the United States finds itself navigating an exceptionally narrow and treacherous strategic corridor. It has initiated a war of regime disruption without the domestic political mandate, the economic resilience, or the appropriate military force structure to execute a conventional occupation.9 The administration must therefore rely entirely on the Iranian internal opposition to finalize the collapse of the theocracy. If the opposition fails to coalesce, or if the IRGC successfully suppresses the uprisings despite the catastrophic loss of its Supreme Leader, the United States faces the grim prospect of managing an open-ended, bloody war of attrition across the Middle East, perpetually teetering on the edge of deeper, catastrophic entanglement.
