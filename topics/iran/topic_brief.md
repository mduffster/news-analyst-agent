# Topic Brief: US-Israel-Iran Conflict

## Scope

This topic tracks the ongoing military and geopolitical confrontation involving the United States, Israel, and Iran, including but not limited to: direct military operations (strikes, assassinations, naval deployments), proxy dynamics (Hezbollah, Houthis, Iraqi militias), diplomatic channels, nuclear dimensions, economic/sanctions impacts, and regional realignment.

## Key Actors

- **United States** — military posture, political constraints, stated objectives, alliance management
- **Israel** — operational activity, war aims, political dynamics, intelligence operations
- **Iran** — regime stability, military capability, proxy network, nuclear program status
- **Hezbollah / Lebanese state** — operational status post-degradation, reconstruction, political positioning
- **Houthis (Ansar Allah)** — Red Sea operations, relationship with Iran
- **Iraqi militias** — activity level, relationship with Baghdad and Tehran
- **Gulf states (Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar)** — positioning, hedging, mediation efforts
- **Russia and China** — diplomatic support, material support, UN dynamics
- **Turkey** — regional positioning, NATO dynamics

## Source Hierarchy

Prefer in this order:
1. Official government/military statements and releases (tag as [OFFICIAL])
2. Wire services (AP, Reuters, AFP) and major newspapers of record
3. Domain-specific outlets: defense/intelligence (Janes, War on the Rocks, CTC Sentinel), regional (Al-Monitor, Al Jazeera English, Times of Israel, Tehran Times with appropriate skepticism)
4. Think tank analysis (CSIS, IISS, Crisis Group, ISW)
5. Social media / OSINT accounts — only when corroborated or when flagging as [UNCONFIRMED]

## Initial Outcome Questions

These were seeded at topic inception. The update process may add, modify, or resolve outcome questions over time.

**Q1: What is the likely escalation trajectory of US military involvement?**
- Limited engagement (strikes conclude, forces draw down)
- Extended air/naval campaign (weeks-to-months sustained operations)
- Significant ground presence (boots on the ground beyond existing deployments)

**Q2: Will Iran pursue overt nuclear breakout in response?**
- No change to nuclear posture
- Accelerated enrichment short of weapon assembly
- Full breakout attempt

**Q3: What is the trajectory of regional proxy conflict?**
- Proxy activity decreases (deterrence success or capability degradation)
- Proxy activity continues at current levels
- Significant proxy escalation (new fronts, major attacks on US/allied assets)

**Q4: What is the diplomatic trajectory?**
- Negotiated de-escalation within 3 months
- Frozen conflict / indefinite low-intensity standoff
- Further escalation with no diplomatic off-ramp in sight
