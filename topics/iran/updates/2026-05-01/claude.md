### What Changed Since Yesterday

#### Administration Claims War Has "Terminated" — Deadline Bypassed
**[OFFICIAL]** For War Powers Resolution purposes, US hostilities with Iran that began in February have now "terminated," a senior official in the US administration said. "Both parties agreed to a two-week ceasefire on Tuesday, April 7, that has since been extended," the official said. "There has been no exchange of fire between US Armed Forces and Iran since Tuesday, April 7."

**[VERIFIED]** Under that rationale, the administration has not yet met the requirement mandated by a 1973 law to seek formal approval from Congress for military action that extends beyond 60 days. Under the War Powers Resolution, President Donald Trump had until Friday to seek congressional authorization or cease fighting. The law also allows an administration to extend that deadline by 30 days.

**[REPORTED]** Richard Goldberg, who served as director for countering Iranian weapons of mass destruction for the National Security Council during Trump's first term, said he has recommended to administration officials that they simply transition to a new operation, which he suggested could be called "Epic Passage." That new mission, he said, "would inherently be a mission of self-defense focused on reopening the strait while reserving the right to offensive action in support of restoring freedom of navigation."

**Assessment:** The administration has chosen to argue the war has "terminated" rather than seek authorization or invoke the 30-day extension. This is a novel legal theory that will be challenged — the naval blockade is an ongoing act of war. Congress has adjourned without acting, effectively ceding the issue to the executive branch for now.

#### Senate Rejects 6th War Powers Resolution — Collins Breaks Ranks
**[VERIFIED]** The Senate on Thursday rejected a Democratic-led effort to force the removal of U.S. forces from Iran, turning back a War Powers Resolution just one day before a critical legal deadline. The procedural measure failed 47 to 50, with Sens. Susan Collins of Maine and Rand Paul of Kentucky the only Republicans breaking with their party.

**[VERIFIED]** Significantly, it was the first time Collins had voted for such a measure, making her the first Republican to do so since the war began in late February. Sen. John Fetterman of Pennsylvania continued to be the lone Democrat voting against the resolution.

**[OFFICIAL]** Collins stated: "The President's authority as Commander-in-Chief is not without limits. The Constitution gives Congress an essential role in decisions of war and peace, and the War Powers Act establishes a clear 60-day deadline for Congress to either authorize or end U.S. involvement in foreign hostilities. That deadline is not a suggestion; it is a requirement."

**[REPORTED]** Sen. Lisa Murkowski announced Thursday that she plans to introduce a measure on whether to formally authorize the war if she does not see a "credible plan" from the White House in the next week. "I do not accept that we should engage in open-ended military action without clear direction or accountability. Congress has a role." She said she will introduce her measure when the Senate returns from recess the week of May 11.

**Assessment:** Collins' defection is significant — she's the first Republican to vote against the war since it began. But with Congress now on recess until May 11, the administration has at minimum a 10-day window with no legislative pressure. Murkowski's threatened AUMF could be more impactful than the Democratic resolutions if it gains GOP support.

#### CENTCOM Briefed Trump on "Final Blow" Options — Dark Eagle Mentioned
**[VERIFIED]** The commander of US Central Command (CENTCOM) briefed US President Donald Trump on potential "final blow" strike options against Iran, Fox News reported on Thursday. Admiral Brad Cooper presented the possible options during a briefing with Trump in the Situation Room, outlining a "short and powerful wave of strikes" should the president decide to resume combat operations. The assessed targets reportedly include Iran's "remaining military assets, leadership and infrastructure."

**[REPORTED]** The Pentagon is also considering deploying advanced weapons systems, including a new hypersonic missile known as "Dark Eagle." The system is capable of striking targets up to 2,000 miles away, potentially targeting remaining ballistic missile launchers.

**[REPORTED]** The US CENTCOM has requested that the long-delayed Dark Eagle hypersonic missile be sent to the Middle East for potential use against Iran, marking the first time Washington would deploy the technology, Bloomberg reports. The request was made after Iran shifted its missile launchers out of range of the US Army's Precision Strike Missile, the current technology deployed.

**[REPORTED]** Israel is on heightened alert, ramping up preparations for a possible return to fighting with Iran. According to Channel 12, Israeli officials are bracing for the possibility that negotiations between the US and Iran could collapse as early as the start of next week. The report cites cabinet ministers as assessing that the US may need to "give a push" to its pressure campaign through military strikes on Iranian gas and energy facilities.

**Assessment:** The briefing has occurred. "Final blow" framing suggests options designed to decisively end the conflict rather than just break the negotiating logjam. The Dark Eagle request is significant — deploying untested hypersonic weapons would signal both military escalation and potential desperation. Israel is preparing for war resumption "as early as next week."

#### Iran Delivers New 10-Point Proposal via Pakistan
**[VERIFIED]** "The Islamic Republic of Iran delivered the text of its latest negotiating proposal to Pakistan, as the mediator in talks with the United States, on Thursday evening," the official IRNA news agency reported, without elaborating. It was not immediately clear what was included in the new proposal.

**[REPORTED]** Iranian state media said the 10-point plan was handed over through Pakistani mediators on May 1, showing Islamabad's continued role in facilitating sensitive regional dialogue.

**[REPORTED]** Tehran's revised proposal places strong emphasis on the strategic Strait of Hormuz and outlines steps aimed at ending the ongoing conflict. With Pakistan acting as an intermediary and both sides still exchanging amendments, the negotiations appear far from concluded, yet increasingly active on multiple diplomatic fronts.

**[VERIFIED]** Talks have since stalled as the US imposed a naval blockade on Iranian ports, while Iran has kept the key Strait of Hormuz largely closed, allowing only a trickle of ships through the waterway since the start of the war. On May 1, Iran's Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi held phone calls with counterparts from Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Turkey, Iraq and Azerbaijan on the latest "initiatives of the Islamic Republic to end the war."

**Assessment:** Iran has delivered its revised proposal. The emphasis on Hormuz suggests it follows the earlier framework of addressing the strait first, deferring nuclear issues. Whether the US will engage with it given Trump's rejection of that sequencing is unclear. Regional diplomatic outreach by Araghchi suggests Iran is building broader support.

#### Lebanon Violence Intensifies — 28+ Killed
**[VERIFIED]** Israeli attacks on southern Lebanon have killed at least 28 people, according to the country's National News Agency, despite a temporary United States-mediated "ceasefire." In the municipality of Jebchit, four people were killed and nine others wounded in an attack that destroyed a residential building. A separate attack killed four members of one family, including a child. In the municipality of Toul, four people were killed and six wounded.

**[VERIFIED]** Israel warns Iran: Israel's defence minister Israel Katz said his country may soon have to "act again" against Iran, to ensure the Islamic republic "does not once again become a threat to Israel." Deadly Lebanon strike: Israeli strikes on three south Lebanon villages killed nine people, among them two children and five women, according to Lebanon's Health Ministry.

**[VERIFIED]** Lebanese President Joseph Aoun denounced the "continuing Israeli violations" in southern Lebanon on Thursday, saying they were occurring "despite the ceasefire, as do demolitions of homes and places of worship, while the number of killed and wounded rises day after day."

**[VERIFIED]** An Israeli military official says that if Iran's stockpile of more than 400 kilograms of uranium enriched to 60% is not removed from the Islamic Republic, the entire recent war will be considered "one big failure." The senior officer says that if no agreement is reached to remove the uranium stockpile and halt enrichment, the achievements in the 40 days of fighting will have been for nothing.

**Assessment:** Lebanon violence has escalated sharply — 28+ killed in a single day despite the ceasefire. Israeli officials are now publicly stating the war is a "failure" without securing the uranium, which could be setting conditions for either resumed military action or pressure for a ground operation to seize the stockpile.
