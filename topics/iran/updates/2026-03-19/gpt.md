## Daily incremental update — US–Israel–Iran conflict

### What changed since the last report

1) **U.S. war-cost signal: Pentagon reportedly seeks ~$200B in additional funding**
- AP reports the Pentagon is seeking **$200 billion** in additional funds for the Iran war (Congress would need to approve). **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/972ec1bd956a2c3633e6ab7fff389791?utm_source=openai))  
**What it means:** This is a major **duration/escalation tell**. Even if it’s partly positioning for Congress, the size implies planning for **weeks-to-months** of high operational tempo (air/missile, naval, munitions burn, force protection, ISR, replenishment). It also raises the odds of **political friction** inside Washington becoming a binding constraint on strategy.

2) **Energy-war escalation looks more explicit and more regional: Iran intensifies attacks on Gulf energy sites after South Pars strike**
- AP says Iran **intensified attacks** on oil and gas facilities around the Gulf **in retaliation** for Israel hitting **South Pars** (shared with Qatar). **[VERIFIED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/52e94398f2432b3aba9b02b51fbe5000?utm_source=openai))  
- AP frames this as raising the risk of drawing **Arab Gulf neighbors** more directly into the conflict. **[VERIFIED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/52e94398f2432b3aba9b02b51fbe5000?utm_source=openai))  
**What it means:** This is a step from “pressure via Hormuz and selective strikes” toward a **tit-for-tat energy infrastructure campaign**. That dynamic tends to (a) internationalize the conflict by forcing Gulf states to respond, and (b) increase U.S. incentives to widen target sets for deterrence (even if Washington prefers not to).

3) **Kharg Island: U.S. confirms very large strike package (>90 targets), still framed as non-oil-infrastructure—for now**
- In AP’s live updates, Joint Chiefs Chair **Gen. Dan Caine** says U.S. forces hit **more than 90 targets** on **Kharg Island**, described as vital to Iran’s oil network. **[OFFICIAL]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/a7e51335d9ce790dbf6bc377ed2763a9?utm_source=openai))  
- Caine also repeats a conditional warning: if Iran interferes with shipping in Hormuz, he may reconsider the decision **not** to target oil infrastructure. **[OFFICIAL]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/a7e51335d9ce790dbf6bc377ed2763a9?utm_source=openai))  
**What it means:** The U.S. message is: **“We can reach your energy choke points, but we’re choosing restraint—conditional on Hormuz.”** This increases the probability of a **bargaining-through-escalation** phase where each side tries to force a climbdown without triggering total economic warfare—hard to sustain as strikes expand.

4) **South Pars importance + Qatar exposure are being emphasized; Ras Laffan remains a key tripwire**
- AP underscores South Pars as an Iranian “energy lifeline,” and notes that global prices moved largely on **retaliation fears** against Gulf energy infrastructure. It also notes Qatar’s **Ras Laffan** had shut March 2 after an Iranian attack and was hit again (per AP). **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/5ad45090d3b66444467cc255ee966a37?utm_source=openai))  
**What it means:** The conflict’s center of gravity is shifting toward **regional energy system fragility** (Qatar LNG, Gulf export hubs, alternative ports/routes). That generally **raises tail risks** (accidental mass-casualty strike, misattribution, or a “must-respond” event for a Gulf monarchy).

---

### Net assessment (incremental)
- The biggest new signal today is **institutional U.S. planning for a costly, extended operation** (the $200B request) plus **widening regional energy targeting**. Together, they push the outlook away from a near-term taper and toward **protracted regional entanglement**.
- The U.S. still appears to be holding a line (at least rhetorically) on **not** directly targeting oil infrastructure—**but** the conditionality tied to Hormuz means the restraint is **reversible** and could flip fast after a single major maritime incident.
