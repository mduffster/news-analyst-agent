## Daily Update: US-Israel-Iran Conflict — April 24, 2026

### Executive Summary

**KEY DEVELOPMENTS: Israel-Lebanon ceasefire extended three weeks; Iran FM heading to Pakistan as talks momentum builds; Oil spikes to $106; Hegseth attacks European allies for "free-riding"; May 1 War Powers deadline looms.**

The situation has evolved on both diplomatic and economic fronts over the past 24 hours. The most significant shift is the Iran-Pakistan diplomatic channel appearing to reactivate, alongside renewed tensions between the US and its European allies.

### What Changed Since Yesterday

#### Israel-Lebanon Ceasefire Extended Three Weeks

**[OFFICIAL]** A ceasefire in Lebanon has been extended by three weeks after Israeli and Lebanese representatives met at the White House, Trump announced. Trump said the ceasefire between Israel and Lebanon is expected to last three weeks, "and the leaders will most likely be coming here during that time."

**[OFFICIAL]** Secretary of State Marco Rubio said Trump's involvement in Israel-Lebanon mediation "made it possible" for the ceasefire to be extended.

**[OFFICIAL]** Trump told reporters that Iran needs to stop funding Hezbollah as part of any deal with the US. "That's a must," the president said during the Oval Office event.

**[VERIFIED]** Despite the extension, fighting continued in southern Lebanon as Israeli forces continued to pound Hezbollah targets following some of the deadliest days since the earlier deal. The Israeli military said it killed two armed individuals in southern Lebanon.

**[VERIFIED]** The Lebanese health ministry said an Israeli strike on south Lebanon killed three people – despite the ceasefire extension.

**Assessment:** The three-week extension buys time but ceasefire violations continue from both sides. The key obstacle remains Hezbollah's status and disarmament.

---

#### Iran FM Heading to Pakistan — Second Round of Talks Possible

**[VERIFIED]** Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi is expected to arrive in Pakistan today for talks with mediators, sources tell CNN. The Iranian delegation is not expected to speak with US representatives, according to a US source and Iranian state media, but the Pakistanis anticipate the meeting will lead to a second round of talks between the US and Iran.

**[VERIFIED]** Iran's state-run IRNA news agency confirmed Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi was on his way to Pakistan on a trip focused on "bilateral consultations" and regional developments. It said he would also travel to Oman and Russia.

**[VERIFIED]** In their call Friday, Araghchi, his Pakistani counterpart Ishaq Dar and Pakistan's army chief Field Marshal Asim Munir, talked over "regional developments and issues related to the ceasefire." Pakistan's Foreign Ministry said Dar "underscored the importance of sustained dialogue and engagement to address outstanding issues."

**[REPORTED]** One Pakistani official said there was now a "high likelihood of a breakthrough" between the US and Iran, after days of escalating brinkmanship and rising tensions.

**Assessment:** This represents the first significant diplomatic movement since Iran refused to show for talks earlier this week. While Iran insists this is bilateral with Pakistan, the intention to lay groundwork for US-Iran talks is clear. This is a notable shift.

#### Oil Spikes to $106/Barrel

**[VERIFIED]** At 9 a.m. Eastern Time today, oil was priced at $106.01 per barrel with Brent serving as the benchmark. That's a gain of $2.34 compared with yesterday morning and around $39 higher than the price one year ago.

**[VERIFIED]** Brent crude topped $106 per barrel early on Friday morning as Washington and Tehran stepped up their confrontation over the key maritime route. Brent stood at $106.80 as of 01:00 GMT, up nearly 5 percent from its closing price on Wednesday.

**[VERIFIED]** International benchmark Brent crude futures fell 0.42% to $104.63 per barrel by 7:54 a.m. ET, while West Texas Intermediate futures lost 1.15% to $94.75. Oil prices fell Friday on a report that Iranian Foreign Minister Seyed Abbas Araghchi will arrive in Pakistan for peace talks.

**Assessment:** Oil briefly breached $106 overnight — the highest since the war began — before retreating on news of Iran's diplomatic outreach. The market is extremely sensitive to any hint of talks resuming. The week overall will see ~14% gains.

#### Strait of Hormuz: Five Ships Transit in 24 Hours

**[VERIFIED]** Only five ships, including one Iranian oil products tanker, have passed through the Strait of Hormuz in the past 24 hours, Friday shipping data showed. Shipping traffic passing through the crucial waterway during an uneasy ceasefire between Washington and Tehran represents a fraction of the average 140 daily passages before the Iran war began.

**[VERIFIED]** Hundreds of ships and 20,000 seafarers remained stranded inside the Gulf with war risk insurers and oil companies watching for any sign that the risks may have eased so they can prepare to sail through.

**[VERIFIED]** Hegseth said the number of Iranian ships prevented from exiting the Strait of Hormuz by US forces had risen to 34, an increase of one since Thursday evening.

#### Hegseth Attacks European Allies for "Free-Riding"

**[OFFICIAL]** Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth said Friday that the "free ride" is over for American allies. "This should not be America's fight alone. We barely use the Strait of Hormuz as a country. Our energy doesn't flow through there, and we have plenty of energy," Hegseth told reporters. "Europe and Asia have benefited from our protection for decades, but the time for free riding is over."

**[OFFICIAL]** "We are not counting on Europe, but they need the Strait of Hormuz much more than we do, and might want to start doing less talking and having less fancy conferences in Europe and get in a boat. This is much more their fight than ours."

**[OFFICIAL]** The United States on Friday said its expanding naval blockade of Iran was tightening "by the hour", as Defence Secretary Pete Hegseth asserted that Tehran faced a stark choice between a nuclear deal and mounting economic collapse under sustained American pressure.

**[OFFICIAL]** "All they have to do is abandon a nuclear weapon — and in meaningful and verifiable ways. Or instead, they can watch their regime's fragile economic state collapse under the unrelenting pressure of American power. A blockade as long as it takes. Whatever President Trump decides."

**Assessment:** This is a significant rhetorical escalation against NATO allies, coming as the UK and France announced a 51-country defensive maritime mission. Hegseth's comments align with Trump's broader NATO skepticism but risk alienating partners whose cooperation may be needed for mine-clearing and convoy operations.

#### May 1 War Powers Deadline — One Week Away

**[VERIFIED]** Trump has until May 1 to obtain congressional approval under the War Powers Resolution. It states that he must limit deployments in any ongoing conflict after 60 days – unless he is granted specific authorisation to continue. To grant this, both the House and Senate must pass a joint resolution with a simple majority in each. That's not happened so far.

**[VERIFIED]** As things currently stand, it is far from certain that Congress will authorise continued military action against Iran because of deep divisions between Democrats and Republicans. On April 15, a fourth bipartisan bid in the US Senate to curb Trump's authority was defeated by 52-47.

**[OFFICIAL]** Some Republicans, such as Sen. Susan Collins, have indicated that they would join with Democrats to vote in favor of resolutions ordering an end to the fighting.

**[VERIFIED]** The deadline can be pushed to 90 days if the president certifies to Congress in writing that "unavoidable military necessity" related to the safety of U.S. armed forces requires an extension.

**Assessment:** This is emerging as a critical political constraint. While past presidents have ignored War Powers deadlines, the combination of $100+ oil, Republican unease, and no end in sight may shift the calculus. The Senate has rejected four Democratic resolutions so far, but the post-May 1 environment may look different.

#### Additional Developments

**Trump Rules Out Nuclear Weapons:**
**[OFFICIAL]** Trump ruled out using a nuclear weapon in the Iran war. Asked by a reporter, the president called it a "stupid question." "We don't need it," he said. "Why would I use a nuclear weapon when we've totally in a very conventional way decimated them without it? No, I wouldn't use it. A nuclear weapon should never be allowed to be used by anybody."

**Iran Leadership Denies Division:**
**[OFFICIAL]** President Masoud Pezeshkian, speaker of Iran's parliament, Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf, and the head of Iran's judiciary, Gholamhossein Mohseni Ejei, have denied Trump's assertions of internal rifts, with coordinated messaging underscoring unity within Iran's leadership.

**Jones Act Waiver Extended:**
**[OFFICIAL]** The White House said that Trump issued a 90-day extension to the Jones Act waiver, making it easier for non-American vessels to transport oil and natural gas in the wake of the war. Trump first announced a 60-day waiver in mid-March.

**Tehran Flights Resume:**
**[VERIFIED]** International flights from Tehran's Imam Khomeini Airport will resume Saturday, Iran's state-run ISNA news agency reported Friday. The first flights to resume will be to Istanbul and Muscat.

**Israel Says Ready to Resume War:**
**[OFFICIAL]** Defence minister Israel Katz said on Thursday that Israel was "prepared to resume the war against Iran", adding that his country was awaiting a green light from the United States to return Iran to "the Stone Age". "The IDF is ready both defensively and offensively, and the targets have been marked."

**Hezbollah Downs Israeli Drone:**
**[VERIFIED]** Hezbollah on Friday said it shot down an Israeli military drone. The Israel Defense Forces confirmed their "remotely piloted aircraft was downed" and that they're reviewing the incident.

### Key Emerging Issues

1. **May 1 War Powers deadline** — Now just 7 days away. Key Republicans are signaling this may be a real constraint, unlike previous invocations. The Senate's Armed Services Committee chair plans a hearing "sometime in May" — after the deadline. This is becoming a significant political factor.

2. **US-European alliance fracture** — Hegseth's attack on European "free-riding" and threat of punitive measures (reported Falklands review) represents a significant deterioration in transatlantic relations during wartime.

3. **Pakistan diplomatic channel reviving** — Araghchi's visit to Pakistan, with planned trips to Oman and Russia, suggests Iran is exploring a negotiating path while maintaining its insistence that the US lift the blockade first.

4. **Oil volatility** — $106 spike overnight followed by retreat on talks rumors shows market hypersensitivity to any diplomatic signals. This creates incentive for negotiation but also risk of manipulation.

5. **Lebanon ceasefire fragility** — Despite extension, violations continue daily. Hezbollah's drone shootdown and ongoing Israeli strikes suggest the ceasefire may not hold for three weeks.
