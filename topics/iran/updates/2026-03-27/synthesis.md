## What Changed Since Yesterday (March 26)

### Trump Extends Deadline to April 6 — But Operations Continue

**[OFFICIAL]** United States President Donald Trump has pushed back a self-imposed deadline for attacks on Iran's power grid to April 6, citing progress in negotiations to end the ongoing war in the country.

"They said to me very nicely, through my people, 'Could we have more time?' Because we're talking about tomorrow night, which is pretty quick, and if they don't do what they have to do, I will knock out their power plants," Trump said. "They asked for seven, and I said, 'I'm going to give you 10,' because they gave me ships," he added. Trump said that Iranian officials were "very thankful" about that.

The benchmark S&P 500 stock market index fell by 1.74% on Thursday, its biggest daily drop since the beginning of 2026, as oil prices rose. Brent crude oil futures rose by more than 5.6%, ending at $108.01 per barrel.

**Assessment:** This is the second deadline extension — Trump originally gave 48 hours on March 21, extended to March 28, now extended again to April 6. The pattern suggests the administration is seeking an off-ramp but hasn't found terms Iran will accept. However, the extension does not stop ongoing military operations.

### Vance Emerges as Lead Negotiator, Clashes with Netanyahu

**[REPORTED - Axios]** Vance has already had multiple calls with Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, met Gulf allies about the war and been involved in indirect communications with the Iranians. He's expected to be the top U.S. negotiator in potential peace talks. Vance was highly skeptical of Israel's rosy prewar assessment of how the war would unfold, and currently expects the war to continue for another few weeks, according to U.S. and Israeli sources.

Partly for those reasons, Witkoff recommended Vance as lead negotiator. "If the Iranians can't strike a deal with Vance, they don't get a deal. He's the best they're gonna get," a senior administration official said.

Vance advisers think some in Israel are trying to undermine the VP, possibly because they find him insufficiently hawkish. Israeli officials deny that.

**Assessment:** This is a significant new dynamic. Vance's skepticism about regime change and his emergence as lead negotiator suggests the US may be preparing to settle for less than Israel's maximalist objectives. This could create serious friction between Washington and Jerusalem.

### Israel Strikes Yazd Missile Facility — Naval Production Hub

**[VERIFIED - Israel]** Israel's air force struck Iran's "most central" missile and naval mine production facility in the city of Yazd overnight on Thursday, targeting weapons used for maritime attacks, the Israel Defense Forces (IDF) spokesperson said. The military said the site was crucial to the development and storage of missiles designed for launch from naval vessels, submarines, and helicopters against maritime targets. The mission was carried out based on intelligence from the Military Intelligence Directorate and the Naval Intelligence Division.

"This is a site where most of the naval missiles and mines are developed by the Iranian Navy's forces," the post added. The IDF said that this adds to the elimination of the Navy command of the Revolutionary Guards, and constitutes a severe blow to the production capabilities of the naval forces.

The Fars news agency reports that the strikes hit Khuzestan Steel near Ahvaz and Mobarakeh Steel in Isfahan. The strikes on the factories, which are partially owned by the IRGC, are expected to cause major damage to the Iranian economy.

**Assessment:** Israel is now explicitly targeting Iran's Hormuz blockade infrastructure — first killing IRGC Navy Commander Tangsiri (Thursday), now destroying the primary facility producing naval weapons. This demonstrates Israel's strategy of degrading Iran's maritime capabilities regardless of diplomatic talks.

### IDF Chief Issues "Collapse" Warning — Critical Sustainability Constraint

**[VERIFIED]** Israel's Chief of Staff, Lt. Gen. Eyal Zamir, has issued an unprecedented warning that the Israel Defense Forces (IDF) are "on the verge of collapse," citing severe manpower shortages as the military fights simultaneous campaigns against Iran, Hezbollah in southern Lebanon, and threats in the West Bank. In a closed-door briefing reported by multiple Israeli outlets, Zamir raised "10 red flags," noting that reserve units are in their sixth and seventh mobilization cycles, regular forces are in "complete collapse," and the army lacks sufficient personnel to sustain operations. He warned that urgent legislative action, including a new Draft Law, Reserve Service Law, and extended mandatory service, is needed to prevent the IDF from being unable to continue missions.

The IDF has said it urgently needs 12,000 recruits — mostly combat troops — due to the strain on standing and reserve forces caused by the war against Hamas in Gaza and other military challenges. In January, Zamir sent a letter to Netanyahu and other senior officials, warning that the shortage of soldiers could harm military readiness in the very near future. Zamir has urged the government to extend mandatory military service for men back to 36 months, after it was changed to 30 months in August 2024.

"At this pace, the IDF will not be prepared for its routine missions in the near future," Zamir said. "Reservists will not hold." Military officials said the reserve system is showing signs of strain and emphasized the need for additional personnel as operational demands continue to grow.

**Assessment:** This is a critical operational constraint that may force Israel to prioritize among its multiple fronts — Iran, Lebanon, West Bank — regardless of political preferences. The warning suggests the multi-front war cannot be sustained indefinitely with current force levels.

### UAE Commits to Hormuz Security Force

**[REPORTED - FT]** The United Arab Emirates has told the U.S. and other Western allies it would participate in a multinational maritime taskforce to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, the Financial Times reported on Friday, citing people familiar with the matter. The UAE is trying to push dozens of countries to create a "Hormuz Security Force" to defend the strait from Iranian attacks and escort shipping, the report added. The UAE has faced more Iranian attacks than any other country in the region, including Israel.

Officials said the UAE is also working with Bahrain on a United Nations Security Council resolution to provide any future force with a mandate, although opposition from Russia and China is possible. The initiative aims to create what has been described as a "Hormuz Security Force" to escort vessels and ensure safe passage. There is growing recognition among Gulf states and in Washington that reopening the strait may require coordinated naval escorts.

**Assessment:** The UAE's willingness to participate in a maritime force represents a significant hardening of Gulf positions toward Iran. This increases the probability of direct Gulf state military involvement in the conflict.

### Oil Prices Hit $111 as Chinese Ships Turned Away

**[VERIFIED]** International benchmark Brent crude futures with May delivery rose 2.82% to $111.06 per barrel, while U.S. West Texas Intermediate futures with May delivery also advanced 2.68% to $97.01.

Traders in the futures market shifted the probability the Federal Reserve will raise interest rates by the end of 2026 to 52%. It's the first time the reading has crossed the 50% threshold, according to the CME Group. The move comes with global benchmark crude prices topping $110, which combines with other developments this week that signal inflation is a growing problem.

### Iran Launches "Wave 83" of Retaliatory Strikes

**[VERIFIED - Iran state media]** On Thursday, Iran's Islamic Revolution Guards Corps (IRGC) announced the execution of wave 83 of Operation True Promise 4 targeting key American and Israeli military installations across the region with missiles and drones. According to a statement from the IRGC's Public Relations, the operation was dedicated to the "warm-hearted people of the southern strip of the country in the north of the Ever-Persian Gulf." The strikes targeted a range of strategic assets belonging to American and Israeli forces. Among the targets were storage tanks and the oil depot in Ashdod, a military personnel site in the Modi'in settlement, and a US military information exchange centre in the region.

Bahrain sounded its missile alert siren late Friday morning over an incoming Iranian attack. Qatar, which had not seen a major attack in days, also sounded its alerts Friday. Kuwait said Friday its Shuwaikh Port in Kuwait City came under attack and sustained "material damage."

### Mediation Architecture Expands

**[REPORTED]** On Thursday, Trump extended his deadline for negotiations with Iran, as Pakistani, Egyptian and Turkish mediators keep trying to organize in-person talks. Iranian officials told the mediators they're still waiting for a green light from "top leadership."

Witkoff claimed "Iran is looking for an off-ramp" from the war and noted that he communicated to an Iranian official that they shouldn't "miscalculate again." A source with knowledge of the mediation efforts said despite public statements rejecting the U.S. proposal, Iranian officials have made it clear they are interested in negotiations. At the same time, the Iranian officials still haven't given a final response to the proposal to hold a high-level meeting with the U.S. in the coming days. The source said the mediators are pressing Tehran to agree to a meeting, but the Iranian officials claim the country's top leadership has yet to make a decision.

## Critical Assessments

### The Vance-Netanyahu Fault Line

The most significant new development is the emerging tension between Vice President Vance and Prime Minister Netanyahu. Key implications:

- **Vance expects "another few weeks" of war** — not months, not regime change
- **Vance was "highly skeptical" of Israeli pre-war assessments** — suggesting fundamental strategic disagreement
- **White House suspects Israeli efforts to undermine Vance** — coalition friction is real
- **If negotiations materialize, Vance would lead US delegation** — his skepticism shapes US endgame

This creates a fundamental question: Can Israel achieve its maximalist objectives if the US is preparing to settle for less?

### IDF Sustainability Crisis — The Hidden Constraint

The IDF Chief's "collapse" warning reveals that Israel's multi-front war faces serious sustainability limits:

- Reserve units in 6th and 7th mobilization cycles
- Regular forces in "complete collapse"
- 12,000 additional troops urgently needed
- Ultra-Orthodox conscription remains politically blocked
- Government has not responded to warnings

**Implication:** Regardless of stated war aims, Israel may be forced to prioritize among fronts (Iran vs. Lebanon vs. West Bank) within weeks if the manpower crisis is not addressed. This could accelerate willingness to accept a ceasefire — or force more dramatic action to achieve objectives quickly.

Day 28 reveals a fundamental incoherence in the US-Israel approach:

| Track | Action | Implication |

These tracks point in different directions. Something has to give.

### UAE Entry Could Widen Conflict

The UAE's commitment to a "Hormuz Security Force" represents the clearest signal yet that Gulf states may transition from defensive to offensive postures. Key factors:

- The UAE has faced more Iranian attacks than any other country in the region, including Israel.
- Working with Bahrain on UN Security Council resolution
- France coordinating with 35 countries on post-ceasefire mission
- But Several U.S. allies have said they have no immediate plans to send ships to unblock the Strait of Hormuz, rebuffing a Trump request for military support. France said on Thursday it had held talks with around 35 countries seeking partners and proposals for a mission to reopen the strait, but only once the U.S.-Israeli war ends.
