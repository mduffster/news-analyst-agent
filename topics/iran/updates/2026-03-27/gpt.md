## Daily incremental update — US–Israel–Iran conflict  
**As of Fri, March 27, 2026 (ET)**  
**Window scanned:** new reporting since **Thu, March 26, 2026 (ET)**

### What changed since the last report (new since Mar 26)

1) **Trump formally “punts” again on the energy-plant ultimatum; new fixed deadline is April 6 (8 p.m. ET)**
- Trump said he is **pausing** threatened strikes on Iran’s energy plants and extending the deadline for Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz to **Monday, April 6, 2026 at 8 p.m. ET**, framing it as requested by Iran. **[OFFICIAL]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/bd5ad0e6999abf1cbdf1e7cfb8ea5109?utm_source=openai))  
- AP also links the move to **market stress (stocks down, oil up)**, implying the White House is managing a markets/war-risk feedback loop. **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/bd5ad0e6999abf1cbdf1e7cfb8ea5109?utm_source=openai))  

**What it means:** This is a *real* de-escalation signal in the narrow sense that it reduces the immediate probability of a near-term U.S. strike on Iran’s energy infrastructure. But it also entrenches a **countdown dynamic**: April 6 becomes the next focal point for coercion/retaliation and market volatility.

2) **Israel issues explicit warning: attacks “will escalate and expand”**
- AP reports Israel warning that its attacks on Iran **will “escalate and expand.”** **[OFFICIAL]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/195444c54cbb7545d0a77f8ffbc0e4c0?utm_source=openai))  

**What it means:** This partially offsets Trump’s delay. Net effect looks less like a unified glidepath to ceasefire and more like **dual-track pressure**: U.S. delays the biggest escalation lever (energy plants) while Israel signals continued/expanded operations—raising the odds of continued tit-for-tat even amid diplomacy.

3) **Diplomacy track: Pakistan’s mediator role reinforced; “backchannel” widening**
- AP published a deeper look at Pakistan’s role as a mediator; it reiterates that **indirect messaging is ongoing**, and adds that **Turkey and Egypt** are also working behind the scenes. **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/f4e809dd3f93b3d67b54f9d75d33d55c?utm_source=openai))  

**What it means:** The “talks” narrative is becoming **more multilateral and resilient** (more intermediaries), which marginally improves the odds of a **limited deconfliction arrangement** (especially maritime) even if a comprehensive ceasefire remains out of reach.

---

### Net assessment (incremental)
**Today’s delta is primarily political/diplomatic rather than battlefield-confirmation.** Trump’s deadline extension to **April 6** reduces immediate escalation risk from the U.S. energy-plant threat, but Israel’s explicit “expand” warning suggests the operational tempo may continue rising anyway. The mediation architecture is thickening (Pakistan + others), modestly improving the odds of a narrow off-ramp (e.g., Hormuz access rules) before April 6.

No new, independently confirmed resolution of the key uncertainty from yesterday (Iran confirmation/denial of senior IRGC-navy leadership losses) surfaced in the new AP items reviewed today.
