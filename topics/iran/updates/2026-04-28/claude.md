## US-Israel-Iran Conflict: Daily Update — April 28, 2026

### Executive Summary

**KEY DEVELOPMENTS: Trump signals rejection of Iran's Hormuz-for-blockade proposal; Oil surges past $111; UAE announces OPEC exit effective May 1; Israel expands strikes to Bekaa Valley; Democrats to force sixth War Powers vote; May 1 deadline now 3 days away.**

Trump held a Situation Room meeting on Iran's proposal Monday but emerged dissatisfied — the nuclear issue remains the core obstacle. Meanwhile, the UAE's shock departure from OPEC reshapes the regional oil landscape, and the Lebanon ceasefire continues to erode.

---

### What Changed Since Yesterday

#### Trump Rejects Iran's Hormuz-First Proposal
**[VERIFIED]** According to Reuters, an unnamed US official said President Trump was unhappy with the proposal because it did not include provisions for Iran's nuclear programme. The official noted that "he doesn't love the proposal."

**[OFFICIAL]** Trump will address the matter "very soon," White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt told reporters on Monday. "His red lines with respect to Iran have been made very, very clear," she said, adding they included preventing Tehran building a nuclear weapon.

**[REPORTED]** One of the people familiar with the matter said Trump was not likely to accept the plan. Reopening the strait without resolving questions over Iran's nuclear enrichment or stockpile of near-bomb-grade uranium could remove a key piece of American leverage in the talks, officials said.

**[OFFICIAL]** Secretary of State Marco Rubio told Fox News on Monday that the proposal was "better than what we thought they were going to submit," but questioned Tehran's intentions. "They're very good negotiators," he said. "We have to ensure that any deal that is made, any agreement that is made, is one that definitively prevents them from sprinting towards a nuclear weapon at any point."

**[REPORTED]** The officials said the White House would likely make a counterproposal in the coming days.

**Assessment:** Yesterday's expected outcome has materialized — Trump will not accept the Hormuz-first proposal. The administration's position is clear: nuclear issues must be addressed upfront, not deferred. However, the language about a counterproposal suggests negotiations aren't dead.

---

#### UAE Announces Shock Exit from OPEC — Effective May 1
**[OFFICIAL]** The United Arab Emirates has announced its decision to quit OPEC and OPEC+ to focus on "national interests", dealing a heavy blow to the oil-exporting groups at a time when the US-Israel war on Iran has caused a historic energy shock and rattled the global economy. The move, which will take effect on Friday, reflects "the UAE's long-term strategic and economic vision and evolving energy profile", a statement carried by state media said on Tuesday.

**[VERIFIED]** The UAE was the third-largest producer in OPEC behind Saudi Arabia and Iraq. The shock announcement comes after the UAE was the target of missile and drone attacks for weeks by fellow OPEC member Iran.

**[OFFICIAL]** Energy Minister Suhail Al Mazrouei said the UAE made the decision at a time when it would be the least disruptive. "Our exit at this time is the right time for it, because it will have a minimum impact on the price and it will have a minimum impact on our friends at OPEC and OPEC+," Al Mazrouei said.

**[REPORTED]** Rystad Energy's head of geopolitical analysis, Jorge Leon, writes: "Saudi Arabia is now left doing more of the heavy lifting on price stability, and the market loses one of the few shock absorbers it had left."

**[REPORTED]** UAE officials have expressed frustration with regional allies over their response to recent security threats. Anwar Gargash, diplomatic adviser to the UAE president, said: "The Gulf Cooperation Council countries supported each other logistically, but politically and militarily, I think their position has been the weakest historically."

**Assessment:** This is a major geopolitical development. The UAE's departure signals fractures in Gulf unity and reflects deep frustration with Iran's attacks during the war. It weakens OPEC's ability to coordinate on supply and could accelerate internal Gulf tensions with Saudi Arabia. The timing — effective the same day as the War Powers deadline — is notable.

#### Oil Surges Past $111 — Highest Since March
**[VERIFIED]** Brent rose to 111.16 USD/Bbl on April 28, 2026, up 2.71% from the previous day. Over the past month, Brent's price has risen 3.51%, and is up 75.67% compared to the same time last year.

**[VERIFIED]** The global oil price climbed above $110 a barrel for the first time in three weeks today. Brent crude, the global oil benchmark, was up 2.7% to above $111 a barrel at 7 a.m. ET. WTI, the US benchmark, gained 3.5% to nearly $100 a barrel.

**[REPORTED]** "Physical scarcity is driving prices rather than headlines over the war," Neil Wilson, a strategist at investment bank Saxo, wrote in a note. "Traders are increasingly watching what's happening on the water (i.e. nothing) rather than trading the diplomatic stuff," he added.

**Assessment:** Oil jumped ~3% from yesterday's $108 to $111, reflecting Trump's rejection of Iran's proposal and the UAE's OPEC exit. The "physical scarcity" framing is significant — markets are now pricing in prolonged closure of Hormuz rather than trading on diplomatic headlines.

#### Israel Expands Lebanon Strikes to Bekaa Valley
**[VERIFIED]** Israel struck Lebanon's Bekaa Valley for the first time since an April 16 ceasefire, while clashes with Hezbollah continued. The strikes on Lebanon's eastern Bekaa Valley marked the first time the area has been hit since a US-brokered ceasefire came into force.

**[VERIFIED]** Israel has continued to carry out strikes across southern Lebanon, and its troops are occupying a strip of the country's south, destroying homes they describe as infrastructure being used by Hezbollah. The Iran-backed group, meanwhile, has kept up its drone and rocket attacks against Israeli troops in Lebanon and on northern Israel.

**[VERIFIED]** At least 2,509 people have been killed in Israeli strikes across Lebanon since March 2, according to Lebanon's Health Ministry.

**Assessment:** Israel's expansion to the Bekaa Valley represents a significant escalation beyond the "yellow line" established in southern Lebanon. This is the clearest sign yet that Israel views the Lebanon ceasefire as providing cover for continued operations rather than a path to de-escalation.

#### Democrats Force Sixth War Powers Vote as May 1 Looms
**[OFFICIAL]** Senate Democratic leader Chuck Schumer said Democrats will force a sixth vote this week on a War Powers Resolution aimed at ending President Donald Trump's "war of choice" in Iran as the conflict reaches the 60-day mark. "As we reach 60 days of a reckless and unpopular war, will Republicans continue to back Trump and dig themselves deeper into this hole?" Schumer wrote on X on Monday.

**[VERIFIED]** "This war is flat-out illegal. Trump didn't have the authority to launch it in the first place, and nearly two months in, he still has not provided a reasonable justification for why we're in this mess," Senator Duckworth told Military.com in a statement Monday.

**[VERIFIED]** "The Trump-Vance Administration launched a war against Iran with no strategy, no congressional authorization, and little support from the American people," Senator Tim Kaine said. "The war hasn't made us or our allies safer, U.S. servicemembers have been killed and injured, gas prices are soaring, and our munitions stockpiles have been depleted."

**[VERIFIED]** The vote exposed divisions in both parties over Congress' role in authorizing military force. Sen. Rand Paul (R-KY) was the only Republican to support the effort, while Sen. John Fetterman (D-PA) broke with Democrats to oppose it.

**Assessment:** With 3 days until May 1, congressional pressure is intensifying. Previous five votes have failed along party lines, but the deadline's approach may shift dynamics. The key question is whether any additional Republicans will break ranks as the legal deadline arrives.

#### Behind-the-Scenes Diplomacy Continues
**[REPORTED]** The US and Iran may not have met for a second round of talks in Pakistan, but the two sides are not as far apart as they seem, according to sources familiar with the mediation process. Intense diplomacy continues behind the scenes, the sources say, and ongoing talks are centered around a staged process in which the first part of a potential deal would focus on returning to the status quo before the war and reopening the Strait of Hormuz without restrictions or tolls. The issue of Iran's nuclear program would be addressed later.

**[VERIFIED]** Pakistani officials told Reuters that negotiations were still taking place remotely, but there were no plans to convene a meeting in person until the sides were close enough to sign a memorandum.

**Assessment:** Despite Trump's public rejection, back-channel diplomacy appears active. The "staged process" framework described by sources essentially mirrors Iran's proposal — Hormuz first, nuclear later. This suggests a potential path forward despite official posturing.

#### German Chancellor Slams US Strategy
**[REPORTED]** German Chancellor Friedrich Merz blasted the US administration on Monday for lacking a strategy in its war with Iran. "The Americans clearly have no strategy. And the problem with conflicts like this is always that it's not enough just to get in - you also have to get out," Merz told a student forum. He said Iranians were "perhaps very skillfully refusing to negotiate" by having the U.S. delegation travel to Islamabad and concluding the talks with no outcome.

**Assessment:** Merz's public criticism signals growing European frustration with Washington's handling of the conflict. This adds to international pressure on the administration.

#### Iranian Army: "Still in War Situation"
**[REPORTED]** An Iranian army spokesperson says Iran is still in a "war situation" as the Strait of Hormuz remains a major sticking point in negotiations.

**[OFFICIAL]** Iran said that the United States was no longer able to "dictate" what other countries do. "The United States is no longer in a position to dictate its policy to independent nations," defence ministry spokesman Reza Talaei-Nik said.

#### First LNG Tanker Transits Since War Began
**[VERIFIED]** A fully laden LNG tanker passed through the Strait of Hormuz for the first time since the war in Iran began, shipping analytics firm Kpler said today. Overall traffic remains limited. It is not exactly clear when the tanker, Mubaraz, managed to cross the strait, Kpler said, since it had likely turned off its transponder and reappeared off the southern Indian coast last night.

**Assessment:** A single LNG tanker getting through underscores how effectively closed the strait remains. Before the war, between 125 and 140 ships passed through the strait each day. In the past 24 hours, only seven vessels have done so, according to ship-tracking data.

#### GCC Emergency Summit Called
**[REPORTED]** Sheikh Abdullah bin Zayed Al Nahyan, UAE Minister of Foreign Affairs, arrived in Jeddah to participate in the extraordinary GCC summit dedicated to discussing response to Iranian strikes.

**Assessment:** The timing of the GCC summit alongside the UAE's OPEC exit suggests Gulf states are actively reconsidering their positions and relationships amid the war.
