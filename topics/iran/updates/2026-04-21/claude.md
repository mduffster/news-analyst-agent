### Bottom Line

**The situation has improved marginally from yesterday's dire assessment, but remains extremely fragile.**

The key development is the confirmation from Pakistani mediators that both Vance and Qalibaf will arrive in Islamabad early Wednesday. This contradicts Iran's public denials and suggests the talks will proceed despite the rhetorical escalation.

However, the underlying dynamics have not changed:
- Trump is publicly insisting he won't extend the ceasefire
- Iran is publicly insisting it won't negotiate "under threats"
- The core issues (nuclear, Hormuz, blockade) remain unbridged
- Both sides are posturing to avoid appearing weak

**Critical next 36 hours:**

If talks proceed and show any progress, the most likely outcome is a short extension (days to weeks) while negotiations continue under an "Islamabad process" framework.

If Iran doesn't show up, or if talks collapse quickly, Trump has signaled he will resume military operations immediately.

**Ceasefire extension probability: ~45% (up from 35% yesterday)**

The improvement is driven by the apparent confirmation of talks, not by any narrowing of the substantive gaps between the parties.
