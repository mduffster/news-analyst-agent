## Daily Update: US-Israel-Iran Conflict — May 6, 2026 (Day 68)

### Executive Summary

**Major pivot: Trump pauses Project Freedom after just 24 hours, citing "great progress" toward a deal. The US and Iran are reportedly negotiating a one-page, 14-point memorandum of understanding that could end the war within 48 hours. Oil prices crashed 8-10% on the news. But Trump simultaneously threatened to restart bombing "at a much higher level" if Iran doesn't agree. France's Charles de Gaulle carrier group crossed the Suez Canal today, pre-positioning for a Franco-British Hormuz mission. Inside Iran, President Pezeshkian reportedly clashed with the IRGC over Monday's UAE strikes, calling them "madness."**

**Bottom line:** After 24 hours of military confrontation over Project Freedom, both sides appear to be backing toward diplomacy. The question is whether Iran's internal divisions — with Pezeshkian reportedly furious at IRGC escalation — will allow Tehran to reach consensus on a deal, or whether hardliners will sabotage the process.

### Critical New Developments (May 6)

#### Trump Pauses Project Freedom, Cites Deal Progress
**[OFFICIAL]** "Based on the request of Pakistan and other Countries, the tremendous Military Success that we have had during the Campaign against the Country of Iran and, additionally, the fact that Great Progress has been made toward a Complete and Final Agreement with Representatives of Iran, we have mutually agreed that, while the Blockade will remain in full force and effect, Project Freedom (The Movement of Ships through the Strait of Hormuz) will be paused for a short period of time to see whether or not the Agreement can be finalized and signed," Trump wrote on Truth Social.

**[OFFICIAL]** Secretary of State Marco Rubio said at a news conference Tuesday that Operation Epic Fury, the U.S. military's name for the war with Iran, is over and that the current military actions to help ships safely navigate the strait are not an "offensive operation." "The operation is over. Epic Fury is — the president notified Congress we're done with that stage of it."

**Assessment:** This represents a sharp reversal from Monday's aggressive posture. The administration is now explicitly stating that the February bombing campaign is "over" — a significant legal and political marker.

#### One-Page Deal Reportedly Close
**[REPORTED]** The one-page, 14-point memorandum of understanding (MOU) is being negotiated between Trump's envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner and several Iranian officials, both directly and through mediators.

**[REPORTED]** The document would declare an end to the war while triggering a 30-day negotiation period on resolving sticking points, including on nuclear issues, unfreezing Iranian assets and future security in the Strait of Hormuz. Precise details of the plan couldn't immediately be verified, but the source familiar said it would include discussion of a moratorium on uranium enrichment for a period of longer than 10 years. A previous US proposal had set it at 20 years. The plan also requires Iran to ship its stockpile of highly enriched uranium out of the country, but details were still being negotiated.

**[REPORTED]** The United States expects a response from Iran within the next 48 hours on key points of a proposed agreement to end the war. The report said the sides were discussing a one-page memorandum of understanding that would set a framework for more detailed nuclear negotiations. Nothing has been agreed, but sources said this was the closest the two sides had come to a deal since the war began.

**[UNCONFIRMED]** Many of the terms laid out in the memo would be contingent on a final agreement being reached, leaving the possibility of renewed war or an extended limbo in which the hot war has stopped but nothing is truly resolved. Reality check: The White House believes the Iranian leadership is divided and it may be hard to forge consensus across the different factions. Some U.S. officials remain skeptical that even an initial deal will be reached.

**Assessment:** If accurate, this would be the closest the two sides have been to a formal agreement. However, the terms remain conditional and Iran's response in the next 48 hours will be decisive.

#### Trump Threatens "Much Higher Level" Bombing
**[OFFICIAL]** President Donald Trump has said the US will start bombing Iran "at a much higher level and intensity" than before the ceasefire if Iran does not agree on a deal. The threat comes hours after Trump paused the US military's "Project Freedom" operation to open the Strait of Hormuz, due to "great progress" being made towards a "complete and final agreement with the representatives of Iran."

**[OFFICIAL]** "Assuming Iran agrees to give what has been agreed to, which is, perhaps, a big assumption, the already legendary Epic Fury will be at an end, and the highly effective Blockade will allow the Hormuz Strait to be OPEN TO ALL, including Iran," Trump wrote. "If they don't agree, the bombing starts, and it will be, sadly, at a much higher level and intensity than it was before," the president added.

**Assessment:** Classic Trump negotiating — extend an olive branch while threatening devastation. The phrase "much higher level" is ominous given his previous threats against civilian infrastructure.

#### Iran Internal Rift: Pezeshkian vs. IRGC
**[UNCONFIRMED]** Exclusive information obtained by Iran International points to a growing clash between Iran's President Masoud Pezeshkian and its military leadership over Monday's escalation in the Persian Gulf and attacks on the United Arab Emirates. According to sources familiar with Tehran's deliberations, Pezeshkian has expressed strong anger at actions by the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps, led by Ahmad Vahidi, describing missile and drone strikes on the UAE as "completely irresponsible" and carried out without the government's knowledge or coordination. Pezeshkian is said to have described the IRGC's approach to escalating tensions with regional countries as "madness," warning of potentially irreversible consequences. Amid a worsening situation and the risk of the country sliding back into war, Pezeshkian has requested an urgent meeting with Mojtaba Khamenei to press for an immediate halt to IRGC attacks on Gulf states and to prevent further escalation.

**[UNCONFIRMED]** Iran's president and parliament speaker are seeking Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi's removal, accusing him of following the Revolutionary Guard chief's instructions in nuclear talks without informing the president. President Masoud Pezeshkian and Speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf believe Araghchi has in recent weeks acted less as a cabinet minister tasked with implementing government policy and more as an aide to Ahmad Vahidi, the commander of the Revolutionary Guards. According to the sources, Araghchi has acted over the past two weeks without informing Pezeshkian, in full coordination with Vahidi and based on his directives. The situation has caused deep dissatisfaction for Pezeshkian, who has told people close to him that he will dismiss Araghchi if it continues.

**Assessment:** If true, this represents a major internal fracture in Tehran. Pezeshkian calling the IRGC actions "madness" and seeking Araghchi's removal suggests the civilian government may be losing control of military decisions. This is highly significant for any peace deal — if the IRGC can bypass the president, who is actually negotiating?

**Assessment:** This is the sharpest single-day decline since the war began — a ~$10-15 drop from yesterday's $116.55 level. Markets are pricing in significant probability of a deal. However, prices remain roughly 50% above pre-war levels.

#### France Deploys Charles de Gaulle to Red Sea
**[VERIFIED]** France's Charles de Gaulle aircraft carrier group is moving into the Red Sea and Gulf of Aden as part of efforts by France and Britain to prepare for a future mission to help freedom of navigation on the Strait of Hormuz, France's military said on Wednesday. The French Armed Forces ministry said in a statement that the aircraft carrier group had crossed the Suez Canal on Wednesday, en route to the south of the Red Sea. This French aircraft carrier strike group was deployed to the eastern Mediterranean shortly after the U.S and Israel launched air strikes on Iran, and it can stay at sea for between four to five months. France and Britain are spearheading efforts to lead an international mission to protect shipping in the Strait of Hormuz, when conditions permit.

**[VERIFIED]** The French operation is distinct from "Project Freedom," the U.S. escort mission launched Sunday, which has already drawn Iranian fire and threats to the April 8 ceasefire. "It's a mission that is distinct from the U.S. mission," Vernet said, calling the French-British plan defensive and consistent with international law.

**Assessment:** European powers are pre-positioning for a post-war scenario while maintaining clear separation from US operations. This dual-track approach gives them flexibility regardless of whether a deal is reached.

#### Iran IRGC Denies UAE Attacks
**[OFFICIAL]** The United Arab Emirates has come under attack from Iranian missiles and drones for the second day in a row, according to its Defence Ministry, while Iran denied being behind the aggression. Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guards Corps (IRGC) said late on Tuesday that the country's forces "have not carried out any missile or drone operations against the UAE in recent days." "If any action had been taken, we would have announced it firmly and clearly. Therefore, the report of that country's Ministry of Defense is absolutely denied and is devoid of any truth," the IRGC statement said.

**Assessment:** The IRGC's denial is notable given earlier reports of internal conflict with Pezeshkian. Either the attacks came from rogue elements, or this is strategic ambiguity to maintain deniability.

#### China Calls for "Comprehensive Ceasefire"
**[OFFICIAL]** Chinese Foreign Minister Wang Yi on Wednesday told his Iranian counterpart Abbas Araghchi that China was "deeply distressed" over the war that has lasted more than two months and said a "comprehensive ceasefire" was needed. China's top diplomat met with Araghchi in Beijing, the first time the Iranian foreign minister traveled to China since the war with the U.S. and Israel started Feb. 28. "We believe that a comprehensive ceasefire is urgently needed, that a resumption of hostilities is not acceptable, and that it is particularly important to remain committed to dialogue and negotiations," Wang said.

**[REPORTED]** Speaking in China following a meeting with Beijing's top diplomat, Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi added: "We will do our best to protect our legitimate rights and interests in the negotiations." Notably, Beijing is a key buyer of Iranian oil, and media reports have suggested that China could be looking to persuade Tehran not to escalate tensions with the U.S. ahead of a meeting between Chinese leader Xi Jinping and Trump next week.

**Assessment:** China is positioning itself as a moderating influence ahead of the Trump-Xi summit May 14-15. Beijing has leverage as Iran's largest oil customer.

#### 23,000 Seafarers Still Stranded
**[VERIFIED]** The administration noted that about 23,000 seafarers from 87 countries remain stranded in the Persian Gulf due to the disruption.

**[VERIFIED]** Rubio described the day-old U.S. push to reopen the strait to maritime traffic as a defensive operation, aimed at helping thousands of civilian sailors stranded there by the war. "They're sitting ducks, they're isolated, they're starving, they're vulnerable," Rubio said. "At least 10 sailors have already died as a result."

**Assessment:** The humanitarian crisis continues. The 10 confirmed deaths underscores the urgency of reopening the strait.

### Key Emerging Issues

1. **Iran's internal fracture as deal-breaker** — If Pezeshkian can't control IRGC, who is actually empowered to accept a deal? The reported clash over UAE strikes suggests military may be operating independently.

2. **48-hour window** — Washington reportedly expects Iranian response on MOU within 48 hours. This creates a hard decision point by Friday.

3. **Trump's dual messaging** — Simultaneously pausing military operations and threatening "much higher level" bombing. This keeps all options open but may also confuse Iran about US intentions.

4. **European pre-positioning** — France moving Charles de Gaulle south of Suez suggests European powers are preparing for multiple scenarios, including both a negotiated resolution and a prolonged crisis.

5. **Market verdict** — The ~10% oil price crash reflects markets pricing in significant deal probability. This creates political pressure on both sides not to let talks collapse.

6. **China summit looming** — Trump-Xi meeting May 14-15 creates diplomatic pressure point. China's call for "comprehensive ceasefire" and Araghchi's Beijing visit suggest coordinated pressure on Iran.
