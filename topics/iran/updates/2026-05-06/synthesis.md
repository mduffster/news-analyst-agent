## Critical Developments Since May 5

### 1. Trump Pauses Project Freedom, Declares Epic Fury "Over"

**[OFFICIAL]** "Based on the request of Pakistan and other Countries, the tremendous Military Success that we have had during the Campaign against the Country of Iran and, additionally, the fact that Great Progress has been made toward a Complete and Final Agreement with Representatives of Iran, we have mutually agreed that, while the Blockade will remain in full force and effect, Project Freedom (The Movement of Ships through the Strait of Hormuz) will be paused for a short period of time to see whether or not the Agreement can be finalized and signed," Trump wrote on Truth Social.

**[OFFICIAL]** In Washington, DC, Secretary of State Marco Rubio told reporters that the US has completed its offensive operations against Iran, dubbed "Operation Epic Fury", and "there's no shooting unless we're shot at first".

**[VERIFIED]** It also represented a surprising about-face from the Trump administration, which just hours earlier had framed Project Freedom as a matter of life or death for thousands of civilian sailors.

**Assessment:** This represents a sharp reversal from Monday's aggressive posture. The administration is now explicitly stating that the February bombing campaign is "over" — a significant legal and political marker that may help avoid War Powers complications.

### 2. One-Page, 14-Point MOU Reportedly Close

**[REPORTED]** The United States and Iran are moving closer to an agreement on a short memorandum ⁠to end the Iran war, a regional source familiar with the negotiations said, although Trump administration officials cautioned that talks had previously fallen apart at the last minute.

**[REPORTED]** The White House received positive feedback from Pakistani mediators on Tuesday that the Iranians were progressing toward a compromise, two administration officials told CNN while offering some skepticism about Pakistan's optimism.

**[REPORTED]** Washington's one-page memorandum of understanding will, if Iran accepts it, lead to the gradual reopening of the Strait of Hormuz and lifting of the American blockade on Iranian ports. Nothing has yet been agreed, the person said, and detailed negotiations on Iran's nuclear program will come later in the process.

**[REPORTED]** The plan also requires Iran to ship its stockpile of highly enriched uranium out of the country, but details were still being negotiated. Trump told PBS News on Wednesday that the terms of a potential deal would include Tehran shipping the material to the United States and pledging not to operate its underground facilities.

**[UNCONFIRMED]** Iran's Foreign Ministry spokesman says the country has not yet issued a formal response to the US's latest proposal but is continuing to exchange diplomatic messages via mediator Pakistan. President Donald Trump has said the US will start bombing Iran "at a much higher level and intensity" than before the ceasefire if Iran does not agree on a deal.

**Assessment:** If accurate, this would be the closest the two sides have been to a formal agreement. However, terms remain conditional and Iran has not formally responded. The 48-hour window for Iran's response creates a decision point by Friday.

### 3. Trump Threatens "Much Higher Level" Bombing

**[OFFICIAL]** President Donald Trump has said the US will start bombing Iran "at a much higher level and intensity" than before the ceasefire if Iran does not agree on a deal.

**Assessment:** Classic Trump negotiating — extending an olive branch while threatening devastation. The phrase "much higher level" is ominous given previous threats against civilian infrastructure. This keeps maximum pressure on Tehran while offering an off-ramp.

### 4. Iran Internal Rift: Pezeshkian vs. IRGC

**[UNCONFIRMED]** Exclusive information obtained by Iran International points to a growing clash between Iran's President Masoud Pezeshkian and its military leadership over Monday's escalation in the Persian Gulf and attacks on the United Arab Emirates. According to sources familiar with Tehran's deliberations, Pezeshkian has expressed strong anger at actions by the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps, led by Ahmad Vahidi, describing missile and drone strikes on the UAE as "completely irresponsible" and carried out without the government's knowledge or coordination.

**[UNCONFIRMED]** Pezeshkian is said to have described the IRGC's approach to escalating tensions with regional countries as "madness," warning of potentially irreversible consequences. Amid a worsening situation and the risk of the country sliding back into war, Pezeshkian has requested an urgent meeting with Mojtaba Khamenei to press for an immediate halt to IRGC attacks on Gulf states and to prevent further escalation.

**[UNCONFIRMED]** President Masoud Pezeshkian and Speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf believe Araghchi has in recent weeks acted less as a cabinet minister tasked with implementing government policy and more as an aide to Ahmad Vahidi, the commander of the Revolutionary Guards. According to the sources, Araghchi has acted over the past two weeks without informing Pezeshkian, in full coordination with Vahidi and based on his directives. The situation has caused deep dissatisfaction for Pezeshkian, who has told people close to him that he will dismiss Araghchi if it continues.

**[REPORTED]** Iran's escalation against the UAE also likely reflects internal Iranian regime dynamics and IRGC Commander Major General Ahmad Vahidi's efforts to constrain diplomacy. CTP-ISW previously assessed that Vahidi may seek to derail negotiations and may have accepted the risk of potential renewed conflict with the United States and Israel.

**Assessment:** If true, this represents a major internal fracture in Tehran. The civilian government may be losing control of military decisions. This is highly significant for any peace deal — if the IRGC can bypass the president, who is actually negotiating?

### 5. IRGC Denies UAE Attacks

**[OFFICIAL]** Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guards Corps (IRGC) said late on Tuesday that the country's forces "have not carried out any missile or drone operations against the UAE in recent days". "If any action had been taken, we would have announced it firmly and clearly."

**[VERIFIED]** According to the UAE, Tuesday's attack came a day after at least three people were injured in strikes the day before, and a drone sparked a fire at a key oil facility in the eastern emirate of Fujairah.

**Assessment:** The IRGC's denial is notable given reports of internal conflict with Pezeshkian. Either the attacks came from rogue elements, or this is strategic ambiguity to maintain deniability while creating an off-ramp.

### 6. Oil Prices Crash ~10-12% on Deal Hopes

**[VERIFIED]** Brent fell to 101.61 USD/Bbl on May 6, 2026, down 7.52% from the previous day. Over the past month, Brent's price has fallen 7.01%, but it is still 66.24% higher than a year ago.

**[VERIFIED]** At 8:30 a.m. Eastern Time today, the price of oil sits at $106.52 per barrel, using Brent as the benchmark. That's a decrease of $10.03 since yesterday morning.

**[VERIFIED]** Mr. Trump faces pressure at home in the form of higher gas prices. The national average price of a gallon of gas reached $4.54 on Wednesday, up from $4.23 a week ago and around $3 per gallon at the start of the war.

**Assessment:** This is the sharpest single-day decline since the war began. Markets are pricing in significant probability of a deal. However, prices remain roughly 50% above pre-war levels.

### 7. France Deploys Charles de Gaulle to Red Sea

**[VERIFIED]** France's Charles de Gaulle aircraft carrier group is moving into the Red Sea and Gulf of Aden as part of efforts by France and Britain to prepare for a future mission to help freedom of navigation on the Strait of Hormuz, France's military said on Wednesday. The French Armed Forces ministry said in a statement that the aircraft carrier group had crossed the Suez Canal on Wednesday, en route to the south of the Red Sea.

**[VERIFIED]** This French aircraft carrier strike group was deployed to the eastern Mediterranean shortly after the U.S and Israel launched air strikes on Iran, and it can stay at sea for between four to five months. France and Britain are spearheading efforts to lead an international mission to protect shipping in the Strait of Hormuz, when conditions permit.

**[REPORTED]** At the same time, the publication, citing the General Staff of the French Armed Forces, indicates that "the movement of the aircraft carrier strike group is not related to the US military operations launched in the region and is solely defensive in nature."

**Assessment:** European powers are pre-positioning for a post-war scenario while maintaining clear separation from US operations. This dual-track approach gives them flexibility regardless of whether a deal is reached.

### 8. China Calls for "Comprehensive Ceasefire" in Araghchi Meeting

**[OFFICIAL]** "We believe that a comprehensive ceasefire is urgently needed, that a resumption of hostilities is not acceptable, and that it is particularly important to remain committed to dialogue and negotiations," Wang said. The Chinese foreign minister said the conflict "has already lasted for more than two months. It has not only caused serious losses to the Iranian people, but also had a severe impact on regional and global peace. China is deeply distressed by this."

**[VERIFIED]** Araghchi's visit on Wednesday comes a week before US President Donald Trump visits Beijing for a summit with President Xi Jinping on May 14 and 15.

**[REPORTED]** In a meeting with his Iranian counterpart Abbas Araghchi, Chinese foreign minister Wang Yi called for "an immediate end to the hostilities" while urging warring powers to continue diplomatic negotiations. China also called for a "prompt resumption of shipping traffic through the Strait of Hormuz," though that point was absent in a statement posted by Iran's foreign ministry on Telegram.

**[REPORTED]** The Iranian envoy also emphasized that Iran is committed to finding a diplomatic solution to the conflict, adding that "facts have proven that the political crisis cannot be resolved through military means." Notably, Iran did not commit to reopening the Strait of Hormuz or to allowing the free flow of commercial shipping.

**Assessment:** China is positioning itself as a moderating influence ahead of the Trump-Xi summit May 14-15. Beijing has significant leverage as Iran's largest oil customer and appears to be urging Tehran toward a deal.

## Analysis: Binary Outcome Emerging

**May 6 reveals a potential pivot point: the situation is moving toward a binary outcome rather than prolonged ambiguity.**

The Project Freedom pause, Epic Fury termination, and MOU negotiations represent the most significant diplomatic movement since the war began. However, Trump's simultaneous "much higher level" bombing threat ensures that failure leads to escalation rather than status quo.

**Three critical variables will determine the outcome:**

1. **Iran's internal coherence** — Can Pezeshkian deliver a deal the IRGC will accept? The reported civil-military split suggests this is uncertain.

2. **Nuclear terms** — Iran wants to defer enrichment discussions; US wants HEU shipped out. The MOU apparently bridges this by creating a "framework for later negotiations," but hardliners on both sides may reject this ambiguity.

3. **China's leverage** — Beijing's call for "comprehensive ceasefire" and pressure on Iran to reopen Hormuz could be decisive, especially with Trump-Xi summit looming.

**The next 48 hours will determine whether this becomes a genuine off-ramp or another false start.** Oil markets have voted for optimism; the question is whether Iran's factional politics allow them to take the exit.
