#### Israel-Lebanon Talks: Historic but Fragile

**[OFFICIAL]** Beirut views the Washington talks strictly as a "preliminary meeting" to secure a pause in military activity, according to Lebanese Culture Minister Ghassan Salame.

**[OFFICIAL]** "These negotiations are futile," said Hezbollah's leader Naim Qassem in a speech on Monday. "No one has the right to take Lebanon down this path without internal consensus among its components."

**[REPORTED]** Sources say the talks will allow Netanyahu to buy time and continue Israel's strikes on Hezbollah in Lebanon while presenting goodwill toward Trump.

#### US Blockade: Day 2 — Limited Enforcement

**[VERIFIED]** The US military blockade of all of Iran's ports and coastal areas went into effect at 10 a.m. ET. According to the US military, it will allow ships to transit the Strait of Hormuz if traveling between non-Iranian ports.

**[OFFICIAL]** The US has now been blockading Iranian ports for more than 20 hours, with Vice President Vance calling the move a just response to Tehran effectively shutting the Strait. Vance accused Iran of "economic terrorism" and said "two can play at that game."

**[VERIFIED]** One oil tanker passed through the Strait—the Chinese-owned vessel Rich Starry, sanctioned by the US since 2023 due to Iran ties. Another sanctioned tanker, Elpis, also passed through overnight.

**[OFFICIAL]** China called the US blockade of Iranian ports a "dangerous and irresponsible act" that will further inflame tensions and risks undermining an "already fragile ceasefire situation."

**[OFFICIAL]** Hours after Trump claimed other countries would join the blockade, a senior NATO official told CBS News: "The U.K. is leading planning efforts of a coalition comprised of more than 40 nations to reopen the Strait of Hormuz and protect freedom of navigation."

**Assessment:** The blockade is targeting Iranian ports specifically rather than all strait traffic. However, sanctioned Chinese tankers are already defying the blockade, and NATO allies are pursuing a separate strategy rather than supporting the US approach.

#### UK-France: Independent Hormuz Initiative

**[OFFICIAL]** UK Prime Minister Starmer said Monday that Britain will not join Trump's blockade. "We're not supporting the blockade," he told BBC Radio 5 Live, adding that it was vital to get the Strait reopened.

**[OFFICIAL]** France's Macron announced: "In the coming days, together with the United Kingdom, we will organize a conference with those countries prepared to contribute alongside us to a peaceful multinational mission aimed at restoring freedom of navigation in the strait. This strictly defensive mission, separate from the warring parties to the conflict, is intended to be deployed as soon as circumstances permit."

**[REPORTED]** NATO Secretary-General Mark Rutte said the UK is leading a coalition effort to align military, political, and economic tools to ensure free passage through the strait.

**[REPORTED]** Turkish Foreign Minister Hakan Fidan said the current 14-day ceasefire could be extended by 45 to 60 days to allow further negotiations.

**Assessment:** A significant transatlantic split is emerging. The UK and France are explicitly distancing themselves from the US blockade while pursuing a parallel multinational mission. This could complicate US strategy or provide an alternative pathway to reopen the strait.
