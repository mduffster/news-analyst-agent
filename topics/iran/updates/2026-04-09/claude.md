## US-Israel-Iran Conflict: Daily Update — April 9, 2026

### Executive Summary

**The ceasefire is holding—barely—but Lebanon threatens to collapse it before talks even begin.**

The two-week US-Iran ceasefire has entered its second day with the core Iran-US pause appearing to hold, though the Strait of Hormuz remains effectively closed and Lebanon continues to be devastated by Israeli attacks. Key developments:

1. **Islamabad talks confirmed for Saturday** — The negotiations are set to begin this weekend in Islamabad after Pakistani Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif formally invited both sides. The White House has confirmed that formal discussions will start on Saturday morning local time.

2. **Iran reports over 3,000 killed during the war** — Iran's forensic chief has now revealed higher casualty figures as the country marks 40 days since Ayatollah Khamenei's assassination. Continuous Hezbollah rocket fire targets Israel's north while Iranian parliament speaker insists "Lebanon and the entire Resistance Axis form an inseparable part of the cease-fire."

3. **Hormuz remains largely blocked** — Vessel traffic through the Strait of Hormuz remains negligible more than a day after the ceasefire announcement. As of Thursday morning local time, MarineTraffic data showed large clusters of ships still anchored in the Persian Gulf, with over 400 tankers, 34 LPG tankers and 19 LNG vessels remaining in the region.

4. **Oil prices rebound** — Crude Oil rose to $101.28 USD/Bbl on April 9, 2026, up 7.28% from the previous day, as uncertainty over Hormuz traffic persists.

### Key Developments Since April 8

#### Iran's 40-Day Memorial for Khamenei

**[VERIFIED]** Commemoration ceremonies marking the 40th day after the martyrdom of Ayatollah Seyyed Ali Khamenei are being held Thursday with widespread public participation in Tehran and across the country. The event, held forty days after his death in a targeted attack attributed to the United States and Israel, has become one of the largest mourning gatherings in recent weeks. The ceremony symbolically began at 9:40 a.m.—coinciding with the exact time of the assassination.

**[OFFICIAL]** Iran's President Masoud Pezeshkian attended a significant commemorative ceremony in Tehran's Republic Square on Thursday. In a massive display of national mourning, vast crowds of Iranians took to the streets of the capital and across the nation to mark the 40th day following the killing.

**[OFFICIAL]** The Islamic Revolution Guards Corps (IRGC) issued a statement asserting that the death of Khamenei was "as effective as his lifetime presence in the promotion of Islamic Revolution and the Islamic Republic." The IRGC highlighted the resilience of the nation and the impact of the military's retaliatory strikes as part of the "blessings of the Leader's pure blood during the imposed war."

**Assessment:** The memorial serves dual purposes—consolidating domestic support behind Mojtaba Khamenei's leadership and projecting defiance ahead of negotiations. The regime appears to have survived the initial shock.

#### Lebanon Crisis Continues to Threaten Ceasefire

**[VERIFIED]** Israeli attacks on Lebanon raise questions about the future of a fragile Iran-United States ceasefire. Lebanon has declared a day of mourning after a wave of Israeli attacks killed at least 200 people and injured more than 1,000 in a single day on Wednesday.

**[VERIFIED]** As of April 8, at least 1,784 people have been killed and 5,977 wounded by Israeli strikes in Lebanon during the 2026 Lebanon war.

**[VERIFIED]** In the early hours of 9 April 2026, Hezbollah said it launched a missile attack against northern Israel. The attacks on April 8 were the largest since the start of the war, with Israel saying it hit over 100 targets within ten minutes, killing at least 254 people.

**[OFFICIAL]** Hezbollah, which had initially said it would pause attacks on Israel in line with the ceasefire, said it was resuming them on Thursday morning and had fired once across the border into Israel and twice at Israeli forces in southern Lebanon.

**[VERIFIED]** Two IDF soldiers were killed by Hezbollah attacks in Southern Lebanon. One Israeli civilian was killed by Hezbollah rocket fire in Nahariyah.

**[OFFICIAL]** According to the Israeli paper Yedioth Ahronoth, the IDF is preparing a plan to continue the operations and ground occupation in Lebanon even after the Iran war ends.

**[OFFICIAL]** Defense Minister Israel Katz provided an update on Operation "Eternal Darkness," saying more than 200 militants were killed yesterday, bringing the total number of Hezbollah fighters killed in the campaign to over 1,400 – more than double the toll of the Second Lebanon War. He described the strikes as a "strong blow to Hezbollah, leaving it stunned and confused."

**[REPORTED]** "What happened yesterday was grave violation," Iranian deputy foreign minister Saeed Khatibzadeh told BBC Radio of Israel's attacks on Lebanon.

**Assessment:** The Lebanon situation has become the most dangerous vector for ceasefire collapse. Israel's explicit exclusion of Lebanon and Hezbollah's resumption of attacks create a cycle that could pull Iran back into direct conflict.

#### Strait of Hormuz: Reopening Stalled

**[VERIFIED]** Vessel traffic through the Strait of Hormuz remains negligible more than a day after President Trump announced a two-week ceasefire. As of Thursday morning local time, MarineTraffic data showed large clusters of ships still anchored in the Persian Gulf. According to data from the tracker, over 400 tankers, 34 LPG tankers and 19 LNG vessels remain in the region.

**[REPORTED]** Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps claimed that shipping through the waterway had stopped following Israel's bombardment of Lebanon, which Tehran said was a violation of the ceasefire.

**[OFFICIAL]** Since the ceasefire was announced, a small number of ships have transited the strait, including the Greek-owned bulk carrier NJ Earth and Liberian-flagged bulk carrier Daytona Beach.

**[REPORTED]** "There's no indication that the strait is going to reopen, and it seems like a flimsy ceasefire, to say what's obvious," oil analyst Tom Kloza told CNN. He said there is just "a trickle" leaving the region. "It looks as though we're weeks away from any restoration of even 50% or 70% of the Strait of Hormuz traffic that we depend on," Kloza said.

**[OFFICIAL]** "The ceasefire is welcome news for the 20,000 seafarers who are awaiting evacuation on the ships which remain in the Persian Gulf," said Damien Chevallier, the Director of IMO's Maritime Safety Division. "The priority now is to ensure the safety of navigation to guarantee an evacuation."

**Assessment:** The Hormuz situation remains the critical test of ceasefire sincerity. Iran is conditioning full reopening on Israel's compliance in Lebanon—a stance that directly challenges the US-Israeli position that Lebanon is excluded.

#### Islamabad Talks — Stakes and Setup

**[VERIFIED]** The White House has confirmed that US Vice President JD Vance will lead the American delegation, joined by President Donald Trump's top envoy Steve Witkoff and son-in-law Jared Kushner. Iranian Parliament Speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf and Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi are expected to lead the Iranian delegation.

**[OFFICIAL]** Authorities have declared April 9 and April 10 public holidays in the federal capital Islamabad, exempting essential services such as police, hospitals and utilities. Security has been tightened across the city.

**[REPORTED]** Iran's ambassador to Pakistan, Reza Amiri Moghadam, briefly announced on X that the Iranian delegation would arrive on April 9 for "serious talks based on the 10 points proposed by Iran." He noted "skepticism of Iranian public opinion due to repeated ceasefire violations by the Israeli regime to sabotage the diplomatic initiative." But he deleted the post within hours.

**[OFFICIAL]** President Trump cautioned Iran late Wednesday night that the U.S. military is standing by with additional ammunition and weapons to take action against Iran if necessary. "All U.S. Ships, Aircraft, and Military Personnel... will remain in place in, and around, Iran, until such time as the REAL AGREEMENT reached is fully complied with," Trump said on Truth Social.

**[VERIFIED]** Public comments from officials indicate multiple areas where Iran and the U.S. don't appear to be aligned. While Ghalibaf's statement and the version of the proposal publicly released by Iran include an acceptance of Iran's right to enrichment, White House Press Secretary Leavitt called Iran's enriched uranium "a red line that the President is not going to back away from."

**Assessment:** The gap between positions remains enormous. Both sides enter talks with contradictory red lines on enrichment, Lebanon, and Hormuz control. The deleted Iranian ambassador post suggests internal debate about the wisdom of negotiations.

#### Updated Casualty Figures

**[VERIFIED]** As of 7 April, Human Rights Activists in Iran (HRANA) documented 3,636 deaths in Iran due to strikes, including 1,701 civilians, 1,221 military personnel, and 714 unclassified.

**[VERIFIED]** As of 7 April, 40 Israeli citizens have been killed, including 27 civilians, and 7,367 injured, including 416 military personnel, during the war.

**[VERIFIED]** As of 8 April, at least 1,784 people have been killed and 5,977 wounded by Israeli strikes in Lebanon. Internal Hezbollah sources said that over 400 Hezbollah fighters had been killed while the IDF said it had killed about 1,000 fighters.

#### Oil Markets Rebound on Uncertainty

**[VERIFIED]** Crude Oil rose to $101.28 USD/Bbl on April 9, 2026, up 7.28% from the previous day. Over the past month, Crude Oil's price has risen 21.36%, and is up 68.60% compared to the same time last year.

**[VERIFIED]** Oil prices rose on Thursday after Iran accused the United States of violating elements of a two-week ceasefire agreement. Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf, Iran's parliamentary speaker, said three elements of Iran's 10-point truce proposal had been violated, including Israel's ongoing strikes in Lebanon.

**[REPORTED]** Goldman Sachs trimmed its second-quarter 2026 forecasts for Brent and U.S. crude to $90 and $87 a barrel.

### Emerging Issues to Watch

1. **Islamabad talks (Saturday, April 12 local time)**: First direct US-Iran negotiations since war began. VP Vance vs. Speaker Ghalibaf. Key issues: enrichment, Lebanon, Hormuz.

2. **Lebanon-as-spoiler**: Hezbollah has resumed rocket fire. If Israeli strikes continue, Iran may exit talks or condition any agreement on Lebanon ceasefire.

3. **Hormuz reopening reality**: An Iranian official indicated no more than 15 vessels per day will pass through Strait of Hormuz—far below the 135 daily transits in normal times. This falls short of US demands for unconditional opening.

4. **IDF ground operations expanding**: The IDF said it was sending more troops from Division 162 to join two other army divisions in southern Lebanon to expand the "buffer zone" in the area.

5. **Conflicting 10-point proposals**: US and Iran appear to be working from different versions. In the version of its 10-point ceasefire plan released in Persian, Iran included the phrase "acceptance of enrichment" regarding uranium, but that phrase was missing from English versions shared by Iranian diplomats with journalists.

### Bottom Line

**Day 2 of the ceasefire: The US-Iran pause holds, but everything around it is on fire.**

The core ceasefire between Washington and Tehran appears to be holding—no direct strikes between the two principals since April 7. However, three critical problems are emerging:

1. **Lebanon is destroying the narrative**: Israel's massive attack on April 8 (254 killed) and Hezbollah's resumed rocket fire Thursday create a parallel conflict that Iran says must be part of any deal. The US and Israel disagree.

2. **Hormuz remains effectively closed**: Despite claims of reopening, fewer than a handful of ships have transited since the ceasefire. Over 800 vessels remain trapped.

3. **Saturday's talks face impossible gaps**: Iran demands enrichment rights and Lebanon inclusion. The US demands uranium surrender and treats Lebanon as separate. Neither position has moved.

The next 48 hours before Islamabad talks begin will determine whether this ceasefire is the beginning of a peace process or just a pause before the next escalation.
