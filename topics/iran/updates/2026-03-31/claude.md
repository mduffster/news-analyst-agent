## Key Developments

### Pentagon Briefing: "Decisive Days" Ahead

**[OFFICIAL]** Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth and Gen. Dan Caine, Joint Chiefs Chairman, declared at a news conference on Tuesday "the upcoming days will be decisive" in the war with Iran. "Iran knows that, and there's almost nothing they can militarily do about it," Hegseth claimed.

The Defense Secretary said that "American firepower is only increasing," while saying Iran's is "decreasing." "Just one month in — only one month — we set the terms," Hegseth said.

**[OFFICIAL]** Hegseth, citing intelligence, said the strikes were damaging the morale of the Iranian military. This, he said, was triggering widespread desertions, key personnel shortages and causing frustration amongst senior leaders.

"We would much prefer to get a deal. If Iran was willing to relinquish material they have and ambitions they have, open the strait — that's the goal," Hegseth said. "We don't want to do more militarily than we have to."

"Regime change has occurred" in Iran, Hegseth said, adding that the country's new regime "should be wiser than the last."

**Assessment:** The Pentagon is projecting confidence, but the "decisive days" framing suggests pressure for resolution. The claims of mass desertions, if accurate, would signal internal military collapse—but lack independent verification.

### Trump Signals Potential Off-Ramp Without Hormuz

**[REPORTED]** According to a report, President Donald Trump is prepared to end military operations in Iran without resolving the crisis surrounding the Strait of Hormuz, a vital shipping route through which one-fifth of the world's oil passes. Trump has been making increasingly aggressive threats to escalate the conflict unless Iran reopens the strait, but has now reportedly told aides he is willing to walk away from the war even if the passage remains closed.

The FTSE 100, European and US stocks pushed higher on Tuesday following a Wall Street Journal report that Donald Trump has told his aides he was willing to end the US military campaign against Iran, even if the Strait of Hormuz remained largely closed. The US president assessed that a mission to reopen the strait would push the war beyond his timeline of four to six weeks.

**[OFFICIAL]** Ensuring safe passage for oil tankers through the Strait of Hormuz is not one of the "core objectives" President Donald Trump has set for ending his military operation against Iran, White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt told reporters on Monday.

**Assessment:** This is a major shift from Trump's repeated ultimatums demanding Hormuz reopening. If confirmed, it suggests the administration is seeking an exit even without its stated goal—potentially leaving Iran with enhanced leverage over the strait long-term.

### Four Israeli Soldiers Killed in Lebanon

**[VERIFIED]** The Israeli military has said four soldiers were killed in combat in southern Lebanon, where its forces are clashing with Hezbollah fighters after launching a ground invasion of the country. An army statement on Tuesday named three soldiers from the same battalion who "fell during combat". In a separate statement, it said another soldier had been killed in the same incident and two others wounded.

The four soldiers were killed in a close-range fight with gunmen in southern Lebanon, according to the IDF. The Israeli military also said that gunmen fired an anti-tank missile at forces evacuating the wounded soldiers.

Ten Israeli soldiers have been reported killed since fighting between Israel and Hezbollah flared up on March 2. [Note: The four new deaths bring this total to 14.]

Israel's far-right ministers have urged Netanyahu to annex southern Lebanon, as the military destroys bridges and homes to cut the area off from the rest of the country. Al Jazeera's Lebanon correspondent Zeina Khodr said Monday night marked a new escalation as Israel opened a new front in Lebanon's Bekaa Valley, targeting roads that link towns known to be Hezbollah strongholds.

**Assessment:** This was the deadliest single day for the IDF in Lebanon since the March 2 escalation. Despite Netanyahu's expansion order, Hezbollah is successfully inflicting costs on the advancing forces. The close-quarters firefight suggests IDF is now operating in contested terrain.

### Three UN Peacekeepers Killed

**[VERIFIED]** The UN said on Monday that two members of its peacekeeping force were killed in an explosion in south Lebanon, with a third being killed in a separate blast the previous day.

At least three UN peacekeepers killed this week as violence surges amid Israel's expanding invasion of southern Lebanon. It said three other Indonesian peacekeepers with UNIFIL were also wounded in the attack, which it described as "indirect artillery fire".

"These are two separate incidents and we are investigating them as two separate incidents," said UNIFIL's spokesperson Kandice Ardiel. The UN Security Council will meet in an emergency session this morning following the killings.

"The incidents are being thoroughly reviewed in order to clarify the circumstances and determine whether they resulted from Hezbollah activity or from IDF activity," the Israeli military posted on Telegram. "It should be noted these incidents occurred in an active combat area."

**Assessment:** Three peacekeepers killed in 24 hours marks an unprecedented toll. The emergency Security Council meeting could lead to increased international pressure on Israel. With UNIFIL mandate set to end December 2026, the peacekeepers find themselves in an increasingly dangerous environment.

### Iran Casualties: Higher Than Previously Reported

**[REPORTED - Iran International]** On 31 March, Iran International reported that at least 4,700 security forces had been killed.

This is significantly higher than prior estimates:
- HRANA documented 3,114 deaths in Iran due to airstrikes by 17 March, including 1,354 civilians, 1,138 military personnel, and 622 unclassified. HRANA states that "military casualties are significantly higher than the figures reported in these reports."

**Assessment:** If accurate, the 4,700 security force deaths would represent a dramatic increase from earlier estimates. Combined with Hegseth's claims of mass desertions, this suggests the Iranian military is suffering severe attrition—though verification remains difficult.

### China-Pakistan Five-Point Peace Initiative

**[VERIFIED]** Pakistan and China on Tuesday issued a five-point initiative for restoring peace and stability in the Gulf and the Middle East amid the US-Israeli war on Iran, in which they called on the immediate cessation of hostilities and starting peace talks as soon as possible.

Pakistani Foreign Minister Ishaq Dar, who is also deputy prime minister, is visiting Beijing where he will meet with top Chinese diplomat Wang Yi for talks on international and bilateral issues. "The two foreign ministers will strengthen strategic communication and coordination on the Iran situation and... make new efforts toward advocating for peace," Beijing's foreign ministry spokeswoman said.

"The principle of protecting civilians in military conflict should be observed. China and Pakistan call on parties to the conflict to immediately stop attacks on civilians and nonmilitary targets, and fully adhere to International Humanitarian Law."

**[UNCONFIRMED]** "Iran has asked for guarantees in any deal with the US," Nasr posted on X on Monday. "Word is that Pakistan Foreign Minister is going to Beijing to get a guarantor for the potential deal. Likely that is Iran's condition for talks with US."

**Assessment:** China's formal entry into the diplomatic process is significant. If Iran requires Chinese guarantees for any deal—similar to how the JCPOA had P5+1 backing—this could complicate US objectives but also provide a face-saving framework for de-escalation.

### Oil Prices: Volatile but Retreating

**[VERIFIED]** As of 8:30 a.m. Eastern Time today, oil is trading at $110.69 per barrel, based on the Brent benchmark. That's 41 cents below yesterday morning's level—but about $35 higher than where it stood a year ago.

Brent crude falls 1.56% to $105.71 per barrel and WTI drops 1% to $101.85 as trading opens lower on March 31, 2026, with traders eyeing potential de-escalation in Middle East tensions after month's surge.

The average US gas price has hit $4 a gallon, the highest since 2022. The war in Iran has essentially shut off oil tanker traffic through the Strait of Hormuz.

Trump's war with Iran has pushed U.S. gas prices above $4 per gallon for the first time since 2022, squeezing household budgets. For the first time since 2022, following Russia's invasion of Ukraine, the national average retail price of gasoline crossed $4 per gallon.

**Assessment:** Oil prices have retreated from their ~$115-116 highs to the $105-110 range amid hopes of de-escalation. The WSJ report on Trump's willingness to end without Hormuz reopening gave markets some relief. March still ends with Brent's largest monthly gain on record (~55%).

### Iranian Attacks Continue on Gulf and Turkey

**[VERIFIED]** NATO intercepts Turkiye-bound missile: NATO forces intercepted a missile fired from Iran towards Turkiye – the fourth since the start of the Middle East war. Attack on Kuwaiti oil tanker: Iran launched a "direct and criminal" attack on the Al-Salmi, a fully loaded Kuwaiti oil tanker stationed at Dubai port. Missile interceptions in Saudi Arabia: Saudi Arabia says it intercepted at least eight ballistic missiles targeting Riyadh and the energy-rich Eastern province.

Iran's latest wave of regional attacks — the 87th in a month — was launched by the country's navy.

The Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) confirmed the death of its naval commander, Admiral Alireza Tangsiri, who was killed in an Israeli strike recently.

**Assessment:** Despite claims of Iranian military degradation, Iran continues launching coordinated attacks across multiple countries. The fourth missile fired toward Turkey underscores regional spillover risks.

### Houthis: Iran Pressuring for Red Sea Escalation

**[REPORTED]** According to a report by Bloomberg, Iran is pressuring the Houthis to resume attacks against ships in the Red Sea in the event of further escalation in the war. Leaders of the Yemen-based Houthis, a militant group backed by Iran, are weighing options for more aggressive action after launching ballistic missiles at Israel.

**Assessment:** This is a critical watch item. If Houthis resume Red Sea shipping attacks, it would create a "dual chokepoint" crisis. Their restraint appears conditional on Iran's direction and the war's trajectory.

## Bottom Line

Day 32 shows signs of potential de-escalation pathways opening alongside continued military pressure. Trump's reported willingness to end the war without reopening Hormuz represents the most significant policy shift since the conflict began—potentially signaling an exit strategy even without achieving stated objectives. The "decisive days" framing from the Pentagon suggests an imminent decision point.

The next 6 days until the April 6 deadline will be critical. Markets are pricing in hope for resolution, but the gap between diplomatic rhetoric and battlefield reality remains vast.
