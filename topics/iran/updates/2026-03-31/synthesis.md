## What Changed Since March 30 (Day 31)

### Trump Signals Potential Exit Without Hormuz

**[REPORTED]** The Wall Street Journal reported that President Trump told aides he's willing to end his military campaign against Iran while leaving the Strait of Hormuz largely closed. Trump and his aides assessed that a mission to reopen the strait would push the conflict beyond his timeline of four to six weeks. Trump decided that the U.S. would wind down current hostilities with Iran after achieving its main goals of hobbling Iran's navy and missile stocks.

**[OFFICIAL]** In a phone call with CBS News Tuesday morning, Trump said he is not ready "quite yet" to remove U.S. assets, but reiterated his frustration that other countries had not sent military assets to join the war. "At some point I will, not quite yet. But countries have to come in and take care of it. Iran has been decimated, but they're going to have to come in and do their own work."

**[OFFICIAL]** "We've done our job," Trump said. "It'll take them 10 years to rebuild. We've had total regime change. These are different people than anyone has ever heard of before, and frankly they've been more reasonable. So, we've had total regime change beyond what anyone thought possible."

**[OFFICIAL]** White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt told reporters that ensuring safe passage for oil tankers through the Strait of Hormuz is not one of the "core objectives" Trump has set for ending his military operation against Iran. Leavitt was asked whether Trump was willing to declare victory even if Iran continued to block most vessels from passing through the strait.

**Assessment:** This represents the most significant policy shift since the conflict began. Trump is clearly seeking an exit, even if it means leaving Hormuz partially closed. The contradiction between his public threats and private calculations suggests the administration is looking for a face-saving way to end the war.

### Pentagon: "Decisive Days" — Hegseth Projects Confidence

**[OFFICIAL]** Defense Secretary Hegseth declared "the upcoming days will be decisive" in the war with Iran. He said "American firepower is only increasing" while Iran's is "decreasing." "Just one month in — only one month — we set the terms."

**[OFFICIAL]** Hegseth, citing intelligence, said the strikes were damaging the morale of the Iranian military. This, he said, was triggering widespread desertions, key personnel shortages and causing frustration amongst senior leaders.

**[OFFICIAL]** "We would much prefer to get a deal. If Iran was willing to relinquish material they have and ambitions they have, open the strait — that's the goal," Hegseth said. "We don't want to do more militarily than we have to. But I didn't mean it flippantly when I said that in the meantime, we'll negotiate with bombs."

**[OFFICIAL]** Asked about whether Trump will send U.S. ground troops to Iran, Hegseth said, "We're not going to forgo any options." Hegseth added that the Pentagon aims "to be unpredictable."

**Assessment:** The Pentagon is projecting confidence while acknowledging uncertainty. The "decisive days" framing suggests pressure to conclude operations soon. Claims of mass desertions lack independent verification but, if accurate, would signal severe stress on Iran's military.

### Three UNIFIL Peacekeepers Killed — UN Security Council Emergency Session

**[VERIFIED]** The United Nations Interim Force In Lebanon (UNIFIL) said three of its peacekeepers were killed in south Lebanon in less than 24 hours in separate incidents. All three deceased peacekeepers were from the Indonesian army. The first died when a shell hit the base.

**[VERIFIED]** In a statement, UNIFIL said two peacekeepers were killed on Monday "when an explosion of unknown origin destroyed their vehicle" near Bani Hayyan. The UN force said a third peacekeeper was severely injured while a fourth was also wounded.

**[VERIFIED]** "The incidents are being thoroughly reviewed in order to clarify the circumstances and determine whether they resulted from Hezbollah activity or from IDF activity," the Israeli military posted on Telegram. "It should be noted these incidents occurred in an active combat area," where it was operating against Iran-backed group Hezbollah. "Therefore, it should not be assumed that incidents in which UNIFIL soldiers were harmed were caused by the IDF."

**[VERIFIED]** The UN Security Council convened an emergency session at France's request. UNIFIL said two of its personnel were killed Monday in an explosion, and another died late Sunday when a projectile hit their position.

**[VERIFIED]** The three deaths bring to 97 the number of UNIFIL peacekeepers killed by malicious acts since the mission began in March 1978.

**Assessment:** Three peacekeepers killed in 24 hours marks an unprecedented toll in the current conflict. The emergency Security Council meeting could generate increased international pressure on Israel and demonstrates how dangerous the southern Lebanon environment has become.

### Four Israeli Soldiers Killed — Deadliest Day in Lebanon

**[VERIFIED]** The Israeli military said four soldiers were killed in combat in southern Lebanon, where forces are clashing with Hezbollah fighters after launching a ground invasion of the country. An army statement on Tuesday named three soldiers from the same battalion who "fell during combat". In a separate statement, it said another soldier had been killed in the same incident and two others wounded.

**[VERIFIED]** Hezbollah attempts to make Lebanon ground invasion "costly" for Israeli army as it continues its advance.

**[VERIFIED]** Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu on Monday ordered the military to expand its invasion in southern Lebanon, pushing deeper to extend what he calls a "buffer zone" reaching the Litani River. Israel's far-right ministers have urged Netanyahu to annex southern Lebanon, as the military destroys bridges and homes to cut the area off from the rest of the country.

**[OFFICIAL]** Israeli Defense Minister Israel Katz said the military would continue occupying a significant portion of southern Lebanon even after the war against Hezbollah. "At the end of the operation, the IDF will establish itself in a security zone inside Lebanon, on a defensive line against anti-tank missiles, and will maintain security control over the entire area up to the Litani."

**Assessment:** This was the deadliest single day for the IDF in Lebanon since the March 2 escalation. The close-quarters combat suggests the IDF is now operating in heavily contested terrain. Katz's announcement of permanent occupation plans signals Israel has no intention of withdrawing, raising the stakes for Hezbollah and international pressure.

### China-Pakistan Five-Point Peace Initiative

**[VERIFIED]** China and Pakistan called on Tuesday for an immediate end to the war in the Middle East, and for peace talks as soon as possible, as they agreed to boost their cooperation on Iran. The two countries outlined a joint initiative "for restoring peace and stability in the Gulf and Middle East region" after Pakistani Foreign Minister Ishaq Dar's visit to Beijing. Both countries have sought to mediate in the Middle East to prevent the conflict from escalating, with Islamabad saying it is ready to host "meaningful talks" between the United States and Iran.

**[VERIFIED]** Dar's ministry said the two sides agreed on a five-point plan, starting with "immediate cessation of hostilities" and the "start of peace talks as soon as possible." The governments said dialogue and diplomacy were "the only viable option to resolve conflicts." "China and Pakistan support the relevant parties in initiating talks, with all parties committing to peaceful resolution of disputes."

**[VERIFIED]** The plan also calls for an end to attacks on civilians and non-military targets, such as energy infrastructure and desalination plants. Shipping lanes should be secured, allowing "the early and safe passage of civilian and commercial ships" through the strategic Strait of Hormuz. Both countries said a lasting peace should be based on the UN charter and international law.

**[UNCONFIRMED]** "Iran has asked for guarantees in any deal with the US," according to Vali Nasr, a former US Department of State official. "Word is that Pakistan Foreign Minister is going to Beijing to get a guarantor for the potential deal. Likely that is Iran's condition for talks with US."

**[VERIFIED]** After coordination with relevant parties, three Chinese ships recently transited the Strait of Hormuz, suggesting some arrangement is being worked out for select commercial traffic.

**Assessment:** China's formal entry into the diplomatic process through the five-point initiative is significant. If Iran requires Chinese guarantees for any deal — similar to the JCPOA P5+1 framework — this could complicate US objectives but also provide a face-saving framework for de-escalation. The selective transit of Chinese ships suggests Iran may be willing to negotiate partial reopening.

### Iran Continues Regional Attacks — Tanker Hit Off Dubai

**[VERIFIED]** NATO forces intercepted a missile fired from Iran towards Turkey — the fourth since the start of the Middle East war. Iran launched a "direct and criminal" attack on the Al-Salmi, a fully loaded Kuwaiti oil tanker stationed at Dubai port. Saudi Arabia says it intercepted at least eight ballistic missiles targeting Riyadh and the energy-rich Eastern province.

**[VERIFIED]** The Kuwait-flagged Al-Salmi crude oil tanker was hit following a reported Iranian drone strike off the coast of Dubai. According to KUNA, the tanker was fully laden at the time of the attack, which had caused "material damage to the ship's hull" and "the possibility of an oil spill in the surrounding waters." Kuwait's military also said its air defenses were responding to "hostile missile and drone attacks."

**[VERIFIED]** Iran's latest wave of regional attacks — the 87th in a month — was launched by the country's navy. Al Jazeera said it serves as a "very strong message to the US", proving Washington's claims of obliterating Iran's naval forces to be untrue. The Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) confirmed the death of its naval commander, Admiral Alireza Tangsiri, who was killed in an Israeli strike recently.

**[REPORTED]** A Kuwaiti oil tanker burst into flames near Dubai following what the ship's owner said was an Iranian attack. Meanwhile, Iran's parliament has approved an early plan to charge a toll for passage through the Strait of Hormuz.

**Assessment:** Despite claims of Iranian military degradation, Tehran continues launching coordinated attacks across multiple countries. The tanker attack near Dubai raises the stakes significantly — targeting a fully laden oil tanker near a major commercial port could cause environmental disaster and demonstrates Iran retains significant offensive capability.

### Oil Prices Retreat But Remain Elevated

**[VERIFIED]** As of 8:30 a.m. Eastern Time on March 31, oil is trading at $110.69 per barrel, based on the Brent benchmark. That's 41 cents below yesterday morning's level — but about $35 higher than where it stood a year ago.

**[VERIFIED]** Brent crude fell 1.56% to $105.71 per barrel and WTI dropped 1% to $101.85 as trading opened lower on March 31, 2026, with traders eyeing potential de-escalation in Middle East tensions after month's surge.

**[VERIFIED]** March 2026 saw oil prices explode higher primarily due to escalating conflicts involving Iran, the United States, and Israel, pushing Brent up roughly 55% — its largest monthly gain since inception in 1988.

**[VERIFIED]** Trump's war with Iran has pushed U.S. gas prices above $4 per gallon for the first time since 2022. For the first time since 2022, following Russia's invasion of Ukraine, the national average retail price of gasoline crossed $4 per gallon, according to GasBuddy and the American Automobile Association. This marks an increase of more than $1 from last month's prices before the war began on Feb. 28.

**[VERIFIED]** Prices of diesel, the primary fuel across many critical sectors like transportation, shipping, construction, and agriculture, hit $5.45 on Tuesday, according to AAA, up 45% since the start of the war.

**Oil Price Trajectory (Brent):**
- March 20: ~$107
- March 27: ~$108
- March 30: ~$115-116
- **March 31: ~$105-111** (volatile, retreating on de-escalation hopes)

**Assessment:** Markets are reacting to de-escalation signals with some relief. The WSJ report on Trump's willingness to end without Hormuz reopening drove oil lower. However, prices remain dramatically elevated, and the record 55% monthly surge demonstrates profound market stress. March ends as the worst month for oil price increases in history.

## Critical Assessments

### The Dual-Track Dilemma

The administration is simultaneously pursuing two contradictory paths:

1. **Exit track**: Trump's private signaling about ending without Hormuz resolution, the "decisive days" framing, and emphasis on "deal as primary effort" all point toward seeking an exit.

2. **Escalation track**: Threats against desalination plants, ground invasion planning, continued strikes, and rejection of timeline constraints keep maximalist pressure active.

**Assessment:** This creates dangerous uncertainty. Iran cannot determine whether to negotiate seriously or prepare for prolonged conflict. Markets swing on each contradictory signal.

### The Lebanon Expansion Problem

Israel's announced permanent occupation plans create a separate crisis:
- More than a quarter-century has passed since Israel pulled out of the security zone, but it is fighting the same enemy, operating with much the same logic, and risks achieving the same result.
- Four IDF deaths in one day demonstrate Hezbollah is inflicting real costs
- Three UNIFIL peacekeepers dead raises international pressure
- 1.2 million Lebanese displaced creates humanitarian emergency

### China's Emerging Role

The five-point initiative and Chinese ship transits signal Beijing is becoming a serious diplomatic actor:
- Could serve as guarantor for any Iran deal
- Has economic leverage with both sides
- Provides face-saving framework for US and Iran
- Complicates simple US-Iran bilateral resolution

## Bottom Line

**Net assessment: 90% probability of continued conflict over the next 60 days — down from 94% on March 30.**

Day 32 reveals **the first genuine de-escalation signals since the war began**. Three dynamics are shifting:

1. **Trump is actively seeking an exit**. His willingness to end without Hormuz reopening, combined with the "decisive days" framing from the Pentagon, suggests the administration wants out of this war sooner rather than later.

2. **A diplomatic framework is emerging**. The China-Pakistan five-point initiative provides structure that didn't exist before. Chinese ships transiting Hormuz demonstrates some arrangement is possible.

3. **Economic pressure is biting**. $4 gas, 55% monthly oil surge, and market volatility are creating domestic political costs Trump wants to avoid.

**However, significant escalation risks remain:**

1. **Lebanon is intensifying, not de-escalating**. Four IDF deaths, three UNIFIL deaths, and permanent occupation announcements point toward prolonged conflict.

2. **Iran retains offensive capability**. The tanker attack off Dubai, 87th wave of regional attacks, and continued missile strikes demonstrate Iran is far from defeated.

3. **The gap between rhetoric and reality remains vast**. Trump claims "total regime change" and "Iran decimated" while Iran continues launching attacks across the region.

**Most likely trajectory (next 7 days to April 6):**
- Military operations continue at reduced tempo
- Diplomatic contacts intensify through Pakistan-China channel
- Trump seeks face-saving declaration of victory
- Iran tests whether US is genuinely exiting or bluffing
- Oil stabilizes in $105-115 range
- Lebanon conflict continues independently of Iran track

**Critical inflection point:** The next 72 hours will reveal whether Trump's exit signals translate into actual negotiations or were merely market-calming rhetoric. If direct US-Iran contact occurs through Pakistani mediation before April 6, a rapid de-escalation becomes possible. If not, the deadline becomes another forcing function for either escalation or credibility-eroding extension.
