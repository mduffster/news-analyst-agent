### What Changed Since Yesterday

#### Trump Issues "No More Mr Nice Guy" Warning
**[OFFICIAL]** Trump posted a warning on Truth Social Wednesday morning: "Iran can't get their act together. They don't know how to sign a non-nuclear deal," alongside an AI-generated image of himself holding an assault rifle with a caption reading "no more Mr nice guy."

**[OFFICIAL]** "They better get smart soon!" the president added.

**[REPORTED]** Trump has told US national security officials to prepare for a long blockade of Iran's ports in order to compel Tehran to give up its nuclear programme, according to the Wall Street Journal, citing unnamed American officials.

**[OFFICIAL]** Trump claimed on Tuesday that Iran "has just informed us that they are in a 'State of Collapse.' They want us to 'Open the Hormuz Strait,' as soon as possible, as they try to figure out their leadership situation."

**Assessment:** Trump's rhetoric has escalated significantly from yesterday's more patient messaging about Iran "figuring out their leadership." The "no more Mr Nice Guy" post signals growing frustration with the stalled talks. However, the simultaneous report about preparing for an extended blockade suggests the administration may be settling in for a war of economic attrition rather than resuming kinetic operations.

#### Hegseth Testifies Before Congress — First Time Since War Began
**[VERIFIED]** Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth is on Capitol Hill Wednesday for what is formally billed as a routine hearing on the Pentagon's budget request. But the appearance — the first before Congress for Hegseth since the war in Iran began in February — lands just two days before a 60-day deadline to wind down hostilities.

**[VERIFIED]** US Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth criticized opposition from lawmakers to the ongoing war with Iran during the congressional hearing today. The White House is seeking roughly $1.5 trillion for defense as part of a fiscal 2027 budget request.

**[VERIFIED]** Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth and Gen. Dan Caine, the chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, are testifying before the House Armed Services Committee about the Pentagon's record $1.5 trillion budget request. Members of Congress are expected to grill the two men about the costs of the Iran war, its economic fallout and the Trump administration's strategy. Democrats and some Republicans have shown interest in invoking the War Powers Resolution of 1973.

**[REPORTED]** In the months since the war began, 13 U.S. servicemembers have been killed and hundreds have been injured. The price of gas domestically has skyrocketed as Iran cut off the Strait of Hormuz. And while there is a fragile ceasefire in place, it is not clear what a potential offramp to the overall conflict looks like.

**[REPORTED]** Senator Tillis, who was a crucial vote to confirming the defense secretary, added that Hegseth's management of the Pentagon had caused him to have second thoughts on his support. "He may be able to clean it up, but on its face, you don't go through the number of highly reputable, senior-level officials, admirals and generals," Tillis added.

**Assessment:** This is the first real accountability moment for the administration since the war began. The combination of Hegseth's testimony, questions about Pentagon leadership shake-ups, and the imminent War Powers deadline creates significant political pressure. The hearing exposes Republican divisions over Hegseth's management.

#### Oil Surges Past $115 — Highest Since 2022
**[VERIFIED]** Brent rose to 116.53 USD/Bbl on April 29, 2026, up 4.74% from the previous day. Over the past month, Brent's price has risen 8.52%, and is up 90.85% compared to the same time last year.

**[VERIFIED]** Brent crude traded at $115.29 per barrel on Wednesday, up 3.62% on the session and rising for the seventh consecutive day, the longest winning streak of the year and the highest closing level for the European benchmark since June 2022. The June 2026 contract has now gained more than 88% over the past 12 months.

**[VERIFIED]** At 9 a.m. Eastern Time today, the price of oil sits at $113.99 per barrel. That's an increase of $4.03 since yesterday morning and roughly $50 more than at this time last year.

**[REPORTED]** Goldman Sachs upgraded its Q4 2026 Brent forecast to $90 from $80 on April 26, the fourth upward revision since the war began. JPMorgan continues to flag a $150 overshoot if Hormuz disruption extends.

**[REPORTED]** The shutdown of the strait, which normally handles about 20% of global oil trade, has triggered what the International Energy Agency described as the largest supply shock on record.

**Assessment:** Oil has jumped ~$5 in 24 hours, from $111 to $115-116. This 5% single-day surge reflects the market's assessment that Trump's hardened rhetoric reduces near-term deal prospects. The seventh consecutive day of gains signals sustained bearish sentiment on diplomatic resolution. Goldman's fourth upward revision underscores how consistently analysts have underestimated disruption duration.

#### Iran Expected to Submit Revised Proposal — Possibly by Friday
**[REPORTED]** Iran is expected to submit a revised proposal on ending the war to mediators in Pakistan in the coming days. The development comes after Trump indicated he would not accept an earlier version.

**[REPORTED]** Sources familiar with the efforts say an answer could come as soon as today, or slide to Friday. The same sources say these delays hint at difficulties reaching the Supreme Leader, Ayatollah Mojtaba Khamenei.

**[REPORTED]** Pakistan's Prime Minister, Shehbaz Sharif, told his cabinet that Iran's foreign minister "assured me that he would give me an answer." No timeline was given but the public nudge to the Iranians is notable.

**Assessment:** The fact that Iran is preparing a revised proposal — rather than walking away — suggests Tehran still sees diplomatic options. However, the delays attributed to difficulty communicating with the hidden Supreme Leader highlight ongoing Iranian leadership dysfunction. The timing is significant: a proposal by Friday would arrive the day after the War Powers deadline.

#### May 1 War Powers Deadline — 2 Days Away
**[VERIFIED]** The War Powers Act does not give presidents an indefinite time to continue their military ambitions. On May 1, a 60-day deadline will be met, at which point Trump must take further action if he wants the war in Iran to continue. Per the law, hostilities must come to a close if Congress doesn't authorize the war.

**[REPORTED]** Multiple Democrats tell TIME they may take Trump to court if the war in Iran continues past Friday's 60-day deadline. The internal discussions were described by lawmakers as still in the early stages but could intensify in the coming weeks if the President presses ahead with military operations.

**[VERIFIED]** At least two Republicans, Senators Thom Tillis and Susan Collins, have suggested they would not vote to approve further US military action following May 1. Senator Lisa Murkowski has said she is working on an authorization of use of military force (AUMF) which would allow US military operations to continue without a full declaration of war.

**[VERIFIED]** A resolution to limit Trump's power in Venezuela was defeated in the Senate only because of Vice President JD Vance's tie-breaking vote. But Vance said in January that "The War Powers Act is fundamentally a fake and unconstitutional law. It's not going to change anything about how we conduct foreign policy."

**Assessment:** The deadline arrives in 2 days with no indication Trump will seek authorization. Democrats exploring litigation is a new development, though historically courts have avoided ruling on War Powers disputes. The key question remains whether any additional Republicans break ranks. Collins, Tillis, and Murkowski remain the most likely crossover votes.

#### IRGC Issues Counter-Warning
**[REPORTED]** In Iran, the Islamic Revolutionary Guards Corps Navy's deputy for Political Affairs says that the force's "surprise tactics" await the US in case of any "fresh miscalculation." Mohammad Akbarzadeh said the navy would "employ its new cards, including in the field of smart targeting, and will set the massive vessels of the criminal regime ablaze."

**Assessment:** The IRGC's rhetoric matches Trump's escalation. "New cards" and "smart targeting" language suggests Iran may be signaling undisclosed asymmetric capabilities. This mutual escalation of rhetoric increases risks of miscalculation during the tense deadline period.

#### Iranian Economy Under Severe Pressure
**[VERIFIED]** The rial plummeted to a record low against the dollar. On the black market, the rial was trading at around 1.80 million against the US dollar. When the war started, one US dollar was worth about 1.70 million rials.

**[REPORTED]** Trump's Treasury Secretary, Scott Bessent, said Washington's "maximum pressure campaign" had caused Iran's inflation to accelerate and that the country was running out of oil storage. It would soon have to start cutting production, Bessent said.

**[VERIFIED]** Iran has banned the export of steel products, effective from April 26, after airstrikes targeted its steel industry. Steel is a strategically important material, vital for a range of industries as well as military production, including missiles, drones and ships.

**Assessment:** Economic indicators suggest the US blockade is having significant effect. The rial's 6% depreciation since the war began, steel export ban, and reports of oil storage constraints all point to mounting pressure. This may explain Iran's willingness to submit a revised proposal.

#### UN Documents Iran's Internal Crackdown
**[VERIFIED]** At least nine people were executed in connection with the January 2026 protests, ten for alleged membership in opposition groups, and two on espionage charges, UN Human Rights said. Since 28 February, "more than 4,000 individuals are estimated to have been arrested on national security related charges in Iran."

**[OFFICIAL]** UN Human Rights Chief Volker Türk said: "I am appalled that – on top of the already severe impacts of the conflict – the rights of the Iranian people continue to be stripped from them by the authorities, in harsh and brutal ways."

**Assessment:** This UN documentation of 21 executions and 4,000 arrests since the war began provides independent verification of Iran's domestic fragility. The regime is simultaneously fighting an external war and suppressing internal dissent — a potentially destabilizing combination.

#### Lebanon Fighting Continues Despite Ceasefire
**[VERIFIED]** In Lebanon, Israeli strikes killed at least ten people throughout southern Lebanon on Tuesday. Despite a declared ceasefire, strikes continued into early Wednesday morning.

**[VERIFIED]** "The IDF has now destroyed an underground terror infrastructure in Qantara, Lebanon, in a huge explosion, inside the new security zone," Defense Minister Katz said. A ceasefire has been in place since April 16 but strikes against Hezbollah in Lebanon have continued.

**Assessment:** The Lebanon front continues deteriorating regardless of the declared ceasefire. Yesterday's Bekaa Valley expansion and today's continued strikes demonstrate Israel's treatment of the ceasefire as cover for operations rather than de-escalation.
