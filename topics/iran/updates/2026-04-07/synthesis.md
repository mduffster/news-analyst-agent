## What Changed Since April 6

### US Kharg Island Strikes — Escalation Short of Oil Targets

**[VERIFIED]** Vice President JD Vance appeared to confirm the strikes during a press conference with Hungarian Prime Minister Viktor Orbán in Budapest. The strike was launched around 12 hours before President Donald Trump's self-imposed deadline to reopen the Strait of Hormuz oil trade channel.

**[VERIFIED]** The US attacked military targets across Iran's Kharg Island as President Donald Trump reiterated his threat to unleash a massive new bombing campaign if Tehran doesn't strike a deal to end the war. US forces early Tuesday struck sites similar to those that were hit in a round of attacks last month, but did not target energy infrastructure on the island, which is a key oil export hub.

**[REPORTED]** Kharg Island, which is situated in the Persian Gulf and 20 miles off the Iranian mainland's coast, is a strategic port and oil export hub, which handles up to 90 percent of Iran's oil exports. The island contains large oil storage facilities which are connected by pipelines to Iran's largest oil and gas fields. The island hosts three main energy infrastructure sites, including Falat Iran Oil Company—which produces 500,000 barrels of crude oil per day.

**Assessment:** The second US attack on Kharg Island maintains escalatory pressure while preserving oil infrastructure targeting as a future option. The deliberate restraint signals that the 8 PM deadline represents the true decision point for infrastructure strikes.

### Israel's Infrastructure Campaign — Bridges and Railways Hit

**[VERIFIED]** At least two people have been killed in an Israeli strike on the Yahya Abad railway bridge in central Iran's Kashan city, according Isfahan's deputy governor. The news came on Tuesday, hours after Israel threatened to target trains and railways before US President Donald Trump's deadline to open the Strait of Hormuz expires.

**[VERIFIED]** Iranian officials reported damage to at least two bridges, railway infrastructure and a key highway on Tuesday as part of a wave of deadly US-Israeli airstrikes on infrastructure targets. A bridge near the holy city of Qom and another carrying a railway line in the central city of Kashan were struck. Two people were killed and three were injured in Kashan. A key highway in northern Iran connecting the main northern city of Tabriz with Tehran via Zanjan was also closed after a hit around 90 kilometres outside of Tabriz.

**[VERIFIED]** The Iranian Red Crescent Society said that a residential area in Tehran was targeted by an airstrike early today, while two people were also killed after an attack on a railway bridge in the city of Kashan. As Trump's deadline ticks down, Israel's military issued a warning to Iranians saying "your presence on trains and near railway lines endangers your life."

**[REPORTED]** All trains were cancelled to and from Iran's second city of Mashhad on Tuesday following a warning from Israel against using the railways. According to the ISNA news agency, there was a power outage in parts of the cities of Karaj and Fardis outside Tehran after power transmission lines and a power substation were knocked out of service by airstrikes.

**Assessment:** Israel is already executing infrastructure strikes that Trump has threatened but not yet ordered against power plants. This demonstrates Israeli autonomy and complicates any diplomatic effort to distinguish between military and civilian targeting.

### Iran's 10-Point Response — Maximalist Position

**[VERIFIED]** Iran rejected a proposal for a temporary ceasefire, arguing it would give the US and Israel time to regroup and launch further attacks and citing past ceasefire violations in Gaza and Lebanon. Instead, Iranian officials put forward a 10-point proposal calling for a comprehensive and permanent end to the war. Tehran also demanded the lifting of longstanding sanctions, a compromise on uranium enrichment and the establishment of a new order in the Strait of Hormuz.

**[REPORTED]** "We won't merely accept a ceasefire," Mojtaba Ferdousi Pour, the head of Iran's diplomatic mission in Cairo, told The Associated Press news agency. "We only accept an end of the war with guarantees that we won't be attacked again."

**[OFFICIAL]** Trump said he was "highly unlikely" to postpone the deadline from 8pm Washington time on Tuesday. "They've had plenty of time," he said.

**Assessment:** The diplomatic gap remains unbridgeable in the hours before the deadline. Iran demands permanent guarantees; the US demands immediate Hormuz action without preconditions.

### UN Security Council Vote — Diluted Resolution

**[VERIFIED]** The vote is expected at 11:00 am (0300 GMT), though the outcome is not certain. The text in question has been diluted in recent days in the hope of avoiding rejection. A draft seen by AFP on Monday no longer mentions authorization to use force -- even defensively.

**[REPORTED]** The Security Council is expected to vote on a draft resolution which strongly encourages states interested in the use of commercial maritime routes in the Strait of Hormuz to coordinate efforts of a defensive nature to contribute to ensuring the safety and security of navigation, including through the escort of merchant and commercial vessels. It demands that Iran immediately cease all attacks against merchant and commercial vessels and any attempt to impede transit passage or freedom of navigation in the Strait.

**[REPORTED]** The vote requires nine affirmative votes and no veto from any permanent member to pass. China and Russia have warned the resolution could implicitly legitimise force, while France and the UK have pushed for a strictly defensive mandate.

**Assessment:** The watered-down resolution may pass but will have limited operational impact. It represents diplomatic signaling rather than a mechanism to reopen Hormuz.

**[VERIFIED]** Iran's regime calls on youth to form human shields around power plants as Trump's deadline nears. The Iranian regime called on young people to form human chains around the country's power plants as President Trump warns "a whole civilization will die" in Iran.

**[REPORTED]** A senior adviser to Iran's supreme leader warned Monday that if President Trump makes good on his threat to order strikes on the country's civilian power plants, Tehran would retaliate by targeting energy infrastructure in U.S.-allied Persian Gulf states. "The foolish President of the United_States has threatened to strike Iran's electrical infrastructure! The rulers of Arab_countries should, in order to prevent the region from going dark, make Trump understand that the Persian_Gulf is not a place for gambling," said Aliakbar Velayati.

**Assessment:** The human shields call creates potential for mass civilian casualties if power plant strikes proceed, with severe international legal and PR implications. Combined with Iran's threat to target Gulf energy infrastructure in retaliation, the stakes for tonight's deadline are extraordinarily high.

## Bottom Line

**Net assessment: 95% probability of continued conflict over the next 60 days — up from 93% on April 6.**

**The war reaches its most dangerous inflection point tonight.** The failed 45-day ceasefire proposal, Iran's rejection of temporary measures, Trump's "highly unlikely" extension statement, and the pre-deadline military strikes on Kharg Island and Iranian bridges all point toward major escalation.

**Three scenarios dominate the next 12 hours:**

1. **Most likely (50%)**: Trump orders infrastructure strikes after 8 PM ET. Power plants and bridges targeted. Iran retaliates against Gulf energy infrastructure. Catastrophic regional escalation risk materializes.

2. **Possible (25%)**: Another deadline extension as last-minute diplomacy continues. "Positive" talks language suggests some channel remains open. Pattern of prior extensions creates precedent.

3. **Less likely (25%)**: Some form of partial agreement — perhaps limited Hormuz passage for "friendly" vessels in exchange for pause on civilian infrastructure targeting.

- **Trump** believes escalatory pressure will force Iran to concede Hormuz

**Tonight's strikes, if they occur, will test both assumptions.** Iran has explicitly warned that crossing "red lines" will result in regional energy infrastructure being "deprived" for years. The human shields at power plants create potential for mass casualties and war crimes allegations.

Trump said the world would "find out tonight" in what he described as "one of the most important moments in the long and complex history of the World."

Whether that proves to be hyperbole or prophecy will be determined in the next several hours.
