## Daily Update: US-Israel-Iran Conflict — April 7, 2026 (Day 39)

### Executive Summary

The conflict reaches its most critical inflection point tonight. **Three major developments since yesterday:**

1. **US strikes Kharg Island ahead of deadline**: The United States launched airstrikes on Iran's Kharg Island early Tuesday morning. The U.S. struck more than 50 military targets on the island, which houses Iran's major oil export hub. US forces did not target energy infrastructure on the island, a key oil export hub. Wikipedia reports that in response, Iran said its restraint in targeting oil infrastructure in the region would no longer apply.

2. **Iran rejects ceasefire, issues 10-point counterproposal**: Iran sent a 10-point response to the proposals under discussion with the U.S. for ending the war. The chances for a ceasefire deal before Trump's deadline of 8pm ET on Tuesday currently appear slim. A U.S. official who saw the Iranian response called it "maximalist."

3. **Trump issues final apocalyptic warning**: "A whole civilization will die tonight, never to be brought back again. I don't want that to happen, but it probably will," Trump said in a social-media post on Tuesday.

### Key Developments

#### US Strikes on Kharg Island — Escalation Before Deadline

**[VERIFIED]** Even before Trump's deadline, an intense wave of strikes was reported on bridges across Iran and on Kharg Island, the country's key oil export hub. The U.S. military struck dozens of military targets on the island overnight, a U.S. official told NBC News.

**[OFFICIAL]** Vice President JD Vance appeared to confirm the strikes during a press conference with Hungarian Prime Minister Viktor Orbán in Budapest. The strike was launched around 12 hours before President Donald Trump's self-imposed deadline to reopen the Strait of Hormuz oil trade channel.

**[VERIFIED]** A U.S. official told CBS News on Tuesday that American forces had conducted new strikes on military targets on Iran's Kharg Island. The official said that, as with similar attacks launched in mid-March, oil infrastructure was not targeted in the overnight attacks. Some of the strikes hit targets that were hit during those previous attacks in March.

**[REPORTED]** Semi-official Iranian agency Mehr News said multiple strikes had been reported on the island, to which Iran pumps almost all of its crude production through underwater pipelines for export.

**Assessment:** The Kharg Island strikes represent a significant military escalation 12 hours before the deadline, but the deliberate avoidance of oil infrastructure suggests the US is preserving escalatory options. This is the second major US attack on the island since March.

#### Iran's Response: Defiance and Threat of Regional Oil War

**[VERIFIED]** On 7 April, US forces began strikes on Kharg Island. In response, Iran said its restraint in targeting oil infrastructure in the region would no longer apply.

**[OFFICIAL]** Iran's Revolutionary Guard warned it would "deprive the U.S and its allies of the region's oil and gas for years" if Trump follows through with his threats.

**[REPORTED]** Ebrahim Zolfaqari, spokesperson for the IRGC's Khatam al-Anbiya Central Headquarters, said Trump's rhetoric comes from "the deadlock he faces" and dismissed his threats as "baseless." "If attacks on non-civilian targets are repeated, our retaliatory response will be carried out far more forcefully and on a much wider scale," he said.

**[REPORTED]** Iran retaliated by firing missiles in central Israel, Saudi Arabia and Bahrain, forcing the closure of the key King Fahd bridge between the two Gulf countries. Saudi Arabia's ministry of defense said it was assessing damage on its energy facility caused by falling debris from dozens of ballistic missiles and drones intercepted by its air defense systems on Tuesday.

**Assessment:** Iran's declaration that its restraint on targeting regional oil infrastructure "no longer applies" represents a major escalation threat. If implemented, this could cause catastrophic damage to Gulf state energy facilities.

#### Iran's 10-Point Counterproposal — "Maximalist" Demands

**[VERIFIED]** Iran reportedly rejected the proposed ceasefire, putting forward instead a call for a permanent end to the hostilities. The Iranian proposal consisted of 10 clauses, including an end to conflicts in the region, a protocol for safe passage through the Strait of Hormuz, the lifting of sanctions and reconstruction, IRNA reported.

**[REPORTED]** Iran's 10-point proposal included a guarantee that Iran would not be attacked again, an end to Israeli strikes against the Iranian-backed Hezbollah in Lebanon and removal of sanctions in exchange for the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, with Iran imposing a $2 million fee for every ship moving through the key waterway, according to the New York Times.

**[OFFICIAL]** Trump earlier Monday called a proposal drafted by countries working to implement 45-day ceasefire a "significant step" but "not good enough." Iran rejected the proposal and called for a permanent end to the war, according to Iranian state-run media.

**[REPORTED]** Trump told reporters Iran's response was "significant" but "not good enough." He said it was "highly unlikely" he'd extend his deadline again. "I gave them a chance, and they haven't taken it," he said.

**Assessment:** The gap between positions appears unbridgeable before tonight's deadline. Iran demands permanent guarantees and sanctions relief; the US demands immediate Hormuz reopening without preconditions.

#### Trump's Deadline: Tonight at 8 PM ET

**[OFFICIAL]** Trump's deadline looms: US President Donald Trump said Iran could be "taken out in one night," which "might" be Tuesday — a deadline he set for Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz. He wouldn't say whether the war is winding down, calling it a "critical period" that depends on Iran's actions.

**[OFFICIAL]** Trump stated: "We have a plan, because of the power of our military, where every bridge in Iran will be decimated by 12 o'clock tomorrow night, where every power plant in Iran will be out of business, burning, exploding, and never to be used again."

**[REPORTED]** Human rights expert Kenneth Roth, the former executive-director of Human Rights Watch, said "Trump is openly threatening collective punishment, targeting not the Iranian military but the Iranian people. Attacking civilians is a war crime. So is making threats with the aim of terrorizing the civilian population."

#### Israel Prepares Additional Target Lists

**[REPORTED]** Israel has approved an updated target list of energy and infrastructure sites in Iran in preparation for a contingency scenario in which US diplomatic talks fail, two Israeli sources told CNN. "Israel is awaiting Trump's decision on the next steps, but we have additional plans for the upcoming weeks pending an American green light," one of the sources said.

**[REPORTED]** Israel is highly skeptical a deal is achievable. Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu has conveyed his concerns about possible ceasefire agreements in recent discussions with Trump, stressing that Israel believes any ceasefire must require Iran to hand over all of its enriched uranium and commit to a complete halt of its enrichment activities.

**[VERIFIED]** Israel's military said it attacked one of the "few remaining" petrochemical facilities producing ballistic missile and explosive material in Shiraz.

**[VERIFIED]** The Israeli military warned Iranians on Tuesday to avoid using trains and railways across the country until 9:00 p.m. local time, saying their presence could put their lives at risk.

#### Overnight Strikes and Casualties

**[VERIFIED]** At least 18 civilians, including two children, were killed in a Israeli-US attack on Iran's Alborz Province, the semi-official Mehr News Agency reported. The Iranian Red Crescent Society said that a residential area in Tehran was targeted by an airstrike early today, while two people were also killed after an attack on a railway bridge in the city of Kashan.

**[VERIFIED]** Iran's Ministry of Health claimed Monday that hundreds of women and children have been killed in US-Israeli strikes across the country since the war started. The ministry said 220 children under the age of 18 were killed and 1,959 others were injured. It said 18 of the dead were under 5 years old. At least 254 women were killed and 4,830 were injured.

**[VERIFIED]** Fifteen Americans were wounded in an overnight Iranian drone strike on Ali Al Salem Air Base in Kuwait.

#### "Human Chains" Called to Protect Power Plants

**[VERIFIED]** Iran's deputy minister of youth and sports called on young people to form a "human chain" around the country's power plants, following Trump's threats that the US will bomb Iran's public infrastructure if it doesn't reopen the Strait of Hormuz.

**[VERIFIED]** "I invite all youth, cultural and artistic figures, athletes, and champions to the national campaign 'Iranian Youth's Human Chain for a Bright Tomorrow,'" Alireza Rahimi wrote on X. "Tomorrow, Tuesday at 14:00, beside power plants across the country, with every belief and taste, we will stand hand in hand to say: Attacking public infrastructure is a war crime."

**Assessment:** The call for human shields is both a propaganda move and creates potential for catastrophic civilian casualties if strikes proceed.

#### Additional Regional Developments

**[REPORTED]** A shooting outside the Israeli consulate in Istanbul has resulted in several casualties. Police said three people had been "neutralized" and two police officers were injured in the incident.

**[REPORTED]** France and Iran have reached an agreement that will secure the release of French citizens held in Iran, French President Emmanuel Macron announced. According to Macron, Cécile Kohler and Jacques Paris are free and on their way back to France after three and a half years of detention.

**[REPORTED]** The United Nations Security Council is expected to table a resolution demanding the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz on Tuesday at 11 am ET. The resolution is sponsored by Bahrain.

#### Oil Prices — Volatile Ahead of Deadline

**[VERIFIED]** Oil prices today: Brent crude $110.05, WTI $113.42 per barrel.

**[VERIFIED]** Oil prices are rising today, as investors grapple with threats from US President Donald Trump to intensify attacks against Iran if the Strait of Hormuz is not reopened by Tuesday night. Brent crude traded 0.3% higher, above $110 a barrel. WTI traded at $113 a barrel.

**[VERIFIED]** Crude oil prices settled higher on Monday, with crude posting a 4-week high. Crude prices rallied after President Trump signaled an escalation of the conflict in Iran by Tuesday if no deal is reached to reopen the Strait of Hormuz.

### Bottom Line

**The war reaches its most dangerous moment tonight.**

Trump stated: "Maybe something revolutionarily wonderful can happen, WHO KNOWS? We will find out tonight." But the diplomatic window has effectively closed. Both sides rejected each other's proposals. Trump called Iran's 10-point plan "not good enough." Iran called Trump's demands "delusional."

With the clock ticking, Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian wrote on social media: "More than 14 million brave Iranians have so far declared their readiness to sacrifice their lives to defend Iran. I too have been, am, and will be sacrificing my life for Iran."
