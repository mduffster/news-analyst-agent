## Executive Summary

Day 19 marks a decisive escalation into **energy warfare**. Israel's strike on Iran's South Pars gas field was coordinated with and approved by the US Trump administration, marking the first time Israel attacked natural gas facilities in Iran during the ongoing military operation. Iranian media reported the strikes halted production at two refineries with a combined capacity of approximately 100 million cubic meters per day. In response, Iran warned countries around the Persian Gulf that a number of energy assets are now "legitimate targets," with facilities in Qatar, Saudi Arabia and the United Arab Emirates on a list of assets at risk of missile strikes. 

Iran has **confirmed the deaths of Ali Larijani and Intelligence Minister Esmail Khatib**—three senior regime figures killed in 48 hours, validating Israel's accelerating decapitation campaign. The war's death toll now exceeds 4,000, with more than three quarters of the fatalities in Iran. Russian military assistance to Iran is deepening, with Russia expanding intelligence sharing and providing satellite imagery and improved drone technology to aid Tehran's targeting of U.S. forces.

## What Changed in the Last 24 Hours

### Iran Confirms Larijani's Death — Decapitation Campaign Validated

**[VERIFIED]** Tehran has confirmed that its top security official Ali Larijani, secretary of Iran's Supreme National Security Council, has been killed. In the early morning of 17 March, several senior Iranian officials, including Ali Larijani and Basij chief Gholamreza Soleimani were targeted by Israeli airstrikes. Both their deaths were later confirmed by Iranian state media.

**[VERIFIED]** Israel's Defence Minister Israel Katz claims that Iran's Intelligence Minister Esmail Khatib and senior ministry figures were killed in an overnight attack. Iran confirmed the death through President Pezeshkian on social media.

**Significance:** Three senior regime figures killed in 48 hours represents an unprecedented tempo in the decapitation campaign. Iran's willingness to publicly confirm these deaths marks a shift from the ambiguity of previous days.

### South Pars Gas Field Strike — Energy War Escalates

**[VERIFIED]** Four gas treatment facilities in southern Iran were damaged in US and Israeli drone strikes. US and Israeli drones targeted gas treatment plants in Assaluyeh that process sour gas from phases 3, 4, 5 and 6 of the offshore South Pars gas field. The governor of Assaluyeh said the facilities have "been taken offline" to control and prevent the spread of fire.

**[VERIFIED]** Iranian gas flows to Iraq were halted following the attack. Tehran supplies between a third and 40 percent of Iraq's gas and power needs. Iran diverted its gas domestically following the attack.

**[VERIFIED]** Israel Hayom cites a source saying the strike marks "an escalation" that harms around a fifth of Iran's gas processing capacity.

**[OFFICIAL]** Trump and his administration opposed the previous strikes on oil facilities due to the images of fires and the resulting surge in prices. However, in the case of gas, this appears to be less of a concern.

**Qatar's reaction:** Qatar's Foreign Ministry spokesman directly blamed Israel for the attack. "Israel's targeting of facilities linked to Iran's South Pars gas field, which represents an extension of Qatar's North Field, is a dangerous and irresponsible step amid the current military escalation in the region."

### Iran's Threat to Gulf Energy Infrastructure

**[OFFICIAL — Iran]** The IRGC issued an "urgent warning" to citizens and residents near several facilities in Saudi Arabia, the UAE and Qatar, describing them as "direct and legitimate targets" and warning they could be struck "within the coming hours."

**[OFFICIAL — Iran]** Iran issued an evacuation warning for several oil facilities across Saudi Arabia, the UAE, and Qatar, saying they would be targeted by strikes "in the coming hours." The warning was directed at Saudi Arabia's Samref Refinery and Jubail Petrochemical Complex, the United Arab Emirates' Al Hosn Gas Field, and Qatar's Mesaieed Petrochemical Complex, Mesaieed Holding Company and Ras Laffan Refinery.

**[OFFICIAL — Iran]** The governor of Asaluyeh condemned the South Pars attack as "political suicide" by the U.S. and Israel and said it marks "a new phase of war equations." "The pendulum of war has swung to a full-scale economic war," he said. "Energy security in the region has reached the point of zero."

### Market and Economic Impact

**[VERIFIED]** Oil and European natural gas prices surged as Iran listed energy assets in the region that it would target. Brent oil climbed as much as 6.1% to a high of $109.75 a barrel, while Europe's gas benchmark jumped as much as 9.1%.

**[VERIFIED]** Oil prices remain approximately 40% higher than before the conflict began, with US gas prices rising 29% in 18 days.

### Iran's Retaliatory Strikes Continue

**[VERIFIED]** Two people were killed in central Israel earlier on Wednesday morning, after another Iranian missile strike. In response to Israeli attacks, Iran launched a missile barrage that killed two Israeli civilians in Ramat Gan.

**[VERIFIED]** Three people are lightly injured by Iran's latest ballistic missile attack on Israel, including a 44-year-old man and two children aged 13 and 12 who were hurt by a blast. Several cluster munition impact sites were reported in central Israel, one of which caused damage to a home.

### Russia Deepening Support for Iran

**[REPORTED]** Russia is now sharing satellite imagery, upgraded drone technology, and tactical guidance with Iran—likely improving Tehran's ability to strike US and allied targets in the Middle East. The new details suggest battlefield lessons from Ukraine are now helping Iran improve drone targeting in the Middle East.

**[REPORTED]** Iran has a small number of military satellites but Russia can draw on a large network to provide information about U.S. forces faster and with more precision. The types of targets Iran appears to be aiming for suggest Tehran is receiving enhanced intelligence. The Iranian aerial attacks appear to be more precise than in the 12-day war between Iran and Israel in June, and more focused on radar sites and communication posts in particular. "They appear to be going after command and control" for U.S. forces.

### Lebanon: Ground Operations Continue

**[VERIFIED]** The number of people killed in Lebanon since Israel renewed widespread attacks on the country now stands at 912, according to Lebanon's health ministry. Among those killed are at least 100 children. More than 2,200 people have also been wounded.

**[VERIFIED]** A wave of displacement unfolds from the city of Tyre toward the north following evacuation orders. At least 17 people were killed and dozens injured by Israeli strikes across Lebanon Tuesday night. In Beirut, six people were killed and 24 injured in two Israeli strikes.

**[VERIFIED]** Israel began ground operations in southern Lebanon on 16 March 2026. In a briefing on Monday, an Israeli military spokesman said Israel expected operations against Hezbollah to continue for at least three more weeks.

However, independent sources report significantly higher figures: Hengaw confirms that at least 5,300 people have been killed during the first 18 days of the war. Among them, 511 civilians account for 9.6% of total casualties, while 4,789 are Iranian military personnel.

### Witkoff Senate Briefing

**[VERIFIED]** Steve Witkoff, President Trump's chief negotiator with Iran, briefed senators Tuesday on a war that critics say was launched on disputed intelligence and without adequate planning. Witkoff's pre-war claims about Iran's nuclear program are being challenged by Iranian officials and independent weapons experts.

**[REPORTED]** The Trump administration has not said how it intends to secure the enriched uranium stockpiles that Iran is believed to have buried deep underground. The existence of those stockpiles—and the understanding that U.S. military ground troops would be required to take the material—has rankled the White House.

## Critical Dynamics

### Energy Infrastructure Now Primary Battlefield

The South Pars strike and Iran's explicit threat list represent a dangerous new phase. Both sides are now targeting upstream energy production—a major escalation from the earlier pattern of avoiding such targets. South Pars, which Iran shares with Qatar, is the world's largest gas field with 24 development phases. Qatar's public condemnation of Israel's strike reflects the growing risk to regional states being drawn into the conflict through energy infrastructure interdependencies.

### Decapitation Campaign: Systematic Dismantlement

With Larijani, Khatib, and Soleimani confirmed dead, and standing authorization for the IDF to eliminate other senior officials without case-by-case approval, Israel has shifted from targeted strikes to systematic dismantlement of Iran's command structure. The question is whether Iran's institutional structure can absorb these losses or whether a breaking point approaches.

### Russia's Deepening Role

Russia's decision to provide Iran with data from its satellites and other intelligence underscores the widening geopolitical fallout from the war and could carry potential risks for American ships, aircraft and bases in the region. This represents the first indication that another major U.S. adversary is participating—even indirectly—in the war.

### Coalition Dynamics: Gulf States Under Pressure

UAE adviser Anwar Gargash said Iran miscalculated by firing upon Arab states in the Gulf. The attacks will drive them closer to Israel and the US, while demonstrating why the region can't accept Iranian nuclear and missile programs. However, Iran's explicit threat to target their energy infrastructure creates a dilemma: being drawn deeper into conflict risks devastating economic damage.
