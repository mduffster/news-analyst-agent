## Summary of Key Developments

Day 19 marks a dramatic escalation on multiple fronts. Iran has confirmed Ali Larijani's death—Israel's most significant decapitation strike since Khamenei—while Israel claims to have killed a third senior leader, Intelligence Minister Esmail Khatib, within 24 hours. The conflict has taken an energy-war turn: Israel struck facilities associated with Iran's South Pars natural gas field, what would mark the first time Iran's upstream oil and gas facilities have been targeted since the war began. Iran's state television responded by publishing a threat saying the Islamic Republic would attack oil and gas infrastructure in Qatar, Saudi Arabia and the United Arab Emirates in the next few hours, specifically threatening Saudi Arabia's Samref Refinery and Jubail Petrochemical Complex, the UAE's Al Hasan Gas Field, and petrochemical plants and a refinery in Qatar. The US deployed bunker-buster bombs on Iranian coastal missile sites near Hormuz, while Iran continued retaliatory strikes that killed two civilians near Tel Aviv.

**Note on yesterday's report:** The prior report referenced Iran announcing it would "resume 90% uranium enrichment" as a major development. I have found no verification of this claim in current reporting. The nuclear dimension remains important, but there is no confirmed announcement of weapons-grade enrichment. I am striking this from the record until it can be verified.

## Major Developments Since Last Report

### Iran Confirms Larijani's Death — Most Significant Strike Since Day 1

**[VERIFIED]** Top Iranian security official Ali Larijani and the commander of Iran's internal Basij militia, Gholamreza Soleimani, have been killed, Iran's state media confirmed hours after Israel claimed to have killed the two. A statement released by Iran's Supreme National Security Council confirmed Larijani was killed along with his son Morteza Larijani and the head of his office, Alireza Bayat, as well as several guards.

Larijani is the most senior leader killed in the country since Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei late last month, and his death is a significant blow to the ruling clerical regime. Larijani was officially the secretary of Iran's Supreme National Security Council, but was considered by many to be the country's de facto leader after Khamenei's death.

Operationally, the impact of his death is likely limited in the short term. Politically, it could harden attitudes and reinforce the narrative inside Tehran that this is an existential fight aimed at dismantling the leadership itself.

### Israel Claims Third Senior Kill: Intelligence Minister Esmail Khatib

**[OFFICIAL - Israel; Iran confirmed]** Israel said it "eliminated" Iranian Intelligence Minister Esmail Khatib last night, a day after top national security official Ali Larijani and former Basij militia chief Gen. Gholam Reza Soleimani were killed.

Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian confirmed Khatib's death in a post on X. "The cowardly assassination of my dear colleagues Ismail Khatib, Ali Larijani, and Aziz Nasirzadeh, alongside some of their family members and accompanying team, has left us in deep mourning," Pezeshkian wrote.

Israeli Defense Minister Israel Katz announced that he and Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu had given the Israeli military standing authorization to eliminate other senior Iranian officials in their sights without case-by-case approval.

**Significance:** Three senior regime figures killed in 48 hours represents an acceleration of the decapitation campaign to an unprecedented tempo.

### US Deploys Bunker Busters on Hormuz Missile Sites

**[VERIFIED]** U.S. forces "successfully employed multiple 5,000-pound deep penetrator munitions on hardened Iranian missile sites" along the Strait of Hormuz, CENTCOM said.

U.S. Central Command said the 5,000 pound "deep penetrator munitions" were used because the anti-ship missiles at the sites "posed a risk to international shipping in the strait."

A U.S. official informed CNN that the munitions used were the GBU-72 Advanced 5K Penetrator, a type of explosive first deployed by U.S. aircraft in 2021.

### South Pars Gas Field Attacked — Energy War Escalates

**[VERIFIED]** Iranian state media reported that gas storage tanks at South Pars were struck, halting output at two refineries with a combined capacity of approximately 100 million cubic meters per day.

An Israeli official told The Jerusalem Post that the attack was coordinated with the United States, adding that the target was Iran's largest gas facility in Bushehr.

The attack on South Pars field involved Phases 3, 4, 5 and 6, Iranian state television reported, blaming Israel and the United States for the attack. It says it has shut down those phases of the field to stop the spreading fire.

Israel Hayom cites a source saying the strike marks "an escalation" that harms around a fifth of Iran's gas processing capacity.

The field supplies around 70 percent of Iran's domestic natural gas.

**Qatar's reaction:** The spokesman for Qatar's foreign ministry lambasted Israel for apparently targeting an Iranian oil field as "dangerous and irresponsible" and a "threat to global energy security." Israeli "targeting of facilities linked to Iran's South Pars field, an extension of Qatar's North Field, is a dangerous & irresponsible step amid the current military escalation in the region."

### Iran Threatens Gulf Energy Infrastructure Retaliation

**[OFFICIAL - Iran]** Iran warned countries around the Persian Gulf that a number of energy assets are now "legitimate targets" after Israel attacked its giant South Pars gas field. Facilities in Qatar, Saudi Arabia and the United Arab Emirates are on a list of assets now at risk of missile strikes, Iran's semi-official Tasnim news agency reported.

"Iran has calibrated its strikes on Gulf energy infrastructure to signal capability without triggering extended outages. That restraint has been deliberate. The question now is whether Tehran shifts from signaling to targeting critical components that could take months, if not years, to repair," stated Fernando Ferreira, Director of Geopolitical Risk at Rapidan Energy.

### Iran's Retaliatory Strikes Kill Two Near Tel Aviv

**[VERIFIED]** A man and woman died from "serious shrapnel injuries" in Ramat Gan, near Tel Aviv. In Israel, 14 people have been killed by Iranian missile fire.

Responding to the killing of Larijani, the paramilitary Revolutionary Guard said it had targeted central Israel with multiple-warhead missiles, which have an increased chance of evading missile defense systems and can overwhelm radar tracking systems. Footage filmed by The Associated Press showed at least one missile releasing cluster munitions over Israel.

### Iran's Foreign Minister Defiant

**[OFFICIAL - Iran]** Israel's killing of Ali Larijani will not deal a fatal blow to Iran's leadership, the country's foreign minister said. Iranian Minister of Foreign Affairs Abbas Araghchi said the United States and Israel had yet to realize that Iran's government does not rely on a single individual.

"I do not know why the Americans and the Israelis still have not understood this point: The Islamic Republic of Iran has a strong political structure with established political, economic, and social institutions. The presence or absence of a single individual does not affect this structure."

### Intelligence Assessment: Regime "Largely Degraded"

**[OFFICIAL - US]** The Iranian regime appears to remain intact but "largely degraded" following US military operations, Director of National Intelligence Tulsi Gabbard said. She said Iran's nuclear enrichment program was "obliterated" in June strikes, but the full extent of the damage remains unclear. Gabbard added the United States has seen no effort so far by Iran to rebuild its enrichment capability.

### Updated Casualty and Economic Figures

**[OFFICIAL]** More than 2,000 people have been killed across the Middle East. In Iran, Israeli and American strikes have killed more than 1,200 people, according to the Iranian Red Crescent Society. At least 850 people have been killed in Lebanon, and 13 have died in Israel. Thirteen U.S. service members have been killed, and two more died of noncombat causes.

**Oil prices:** Brent oil climbed as much as 6.1% to a high of $109.75 a barrel, while Europe's gas benchmark jumped as much as 9.1%.

**US gas prices:** The price of a gallon of regular gas rose another 5 cents, on average, in the United States today, now standing at $3.84 a gallon, the highest price since September 25, 2023. The nationwide average price has risen by 86 cents in just 18 days – a 29% jump – one of the fastest spikes on record.

### Nuclear Dimension: Mystery Facility at Isfahan

**[VERIFIED]** IAEA Director General Rafael Grossi said inspectors had to cancel a visit to Iran's new underground enrichment facility at Isfahan, so do not know whether it is "simply an empty hall" or whether centrifuges have begun to be installed. "There are many questions that we will only elucidate when we are able to go back."

### Israeli Drones Provide "Air Umbrella" for Iranian Celebrations

**[REPORTED]** "An unprecedented event took place last night, as Israeli drones targeted Basij and police patrols attempting to approach celebration sites," an Israeli official said. "For the first time, the people of Iran benefited from active support that paralyzed the repression apparatus and effectively provided an 'air umbrella' for the crowds."

This occurred during Chaharshanbe Suri celebrations, where Iranians across many cities took to the streets late Tuesday to celebrate, defying warnings and attempted crackdowns by security forces.

## Emerging Issues to Watch

1. **Iran's threatened strikes on Gulf energy:** Tasnim listed specific facilities. If these attacks materialize in the coming hours, it could trigger a broader response from Gulf states.

2. **Ahmad Vahidi as next target:** According to Jerusalem Post, another figure who could be running much of the regime behind the scenes is Ahmad Vahidi, the IRGC deputy chief on the eve of the war who was announced as its new leader on March 1. Given the Guard Corps' centrality to running the regime's security forces, his position could make him the regime's reigning authority.
