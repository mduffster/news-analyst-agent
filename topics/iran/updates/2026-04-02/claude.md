### Key Developments

#### Trump's Address: Familiar Rhetoric, No Exit Strategy

**[OFFICIAL]** President Trump predicted in a prime-time address Wednesday that the U.S. will complete its military mission in Iran "very shortly." The president, in his roughly 19-minute speech from the White House, said the U.S. will hit Iran "extremely hard" over the next two to three weeks. He also threatened to obliterate all of Iran's electric generating plants and target its oil sites if the country's leaders don't make a deal.

**[VERIFIED]** Trump's speech made no mention of two main potential routes to ending the war: conducting negotiations or sending ground troops. The president has repeatedly said his staff is in discussions with Iranian officials, and this morning said Iran's "new regime president" had asked for a ceasefire. Iran denies it is negotiating.

**[OFFICIAL]** On Hormuz, Trump reiterated plans to leave Iran in 2 to 3 weeks. "We will be helpful, but they should take the lead," Trump said of the strait, adding that other nations "must take care of that passage, they must cherish it, they must grab it," claiming that it can be done easily.

**[REPORTED]** A CNN poll released Wednesday ahead of the speech showed just 33% of respondents approve of the job Trump is doing as commander in chief, and 31% saying the same of his handling of the economy. A recent YouGov poll suggested that only 28 percent of respondents, including 61 percent of Republicans, support the war. In a previous YouGov survey released on March 2, 76 percent of Republican respondents said they support the war.

**Assessment:** The speech offered no strategic clarity. Analysts across the political spectrum criticized its lack of substance. The disconnect between Trump's claim of imminent victory and his threat of massive infrastructure destruction if no deal suggests uncertainty about how to end the conflict.

#### Oil Surges Back Above $111; Markets Sell Off

**[VERIFIED]** By 10:15 a.m. Eastern Time today, oil had reached $111.69 per barrel, measured using the Brent benchmark. That's $6.83 more than it cost yesterday morning and about $41.42 above its price a year earlier.

**[VERIFIED]** Dow futures fell by 670 points, or 1.4%, in premarket trading. S&P futures dropped 1.6%, and those tied to the tech-heavy Nasdaq 100 declined by 2.1%. Stocks had rallied earlier this week, posting their best day since May on Tuesday as optimism rose that the United States might make an effort to end the war with Iran. But the sell-off came after Trump, in an April 1 evening address to the nation, revealed no clear exit strategy and no solution to the effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz.

**[VERIFIED]** Asian stocks traded downward on Thursday, as US President Donald Trump repeated vows to hit Iran hard for another few weeks. Japan's benchmark Nikkei 225 index fell 2.1%, while South Korea's Kospi slid 3.9%. Hong Kong's Hang Seng index was down 1%.

**Assessment:** The market's Tuesday optimism was premature. Trump's speech provided no path to Hormuz reopening and promised continued escalation, driving oil back toward highs.

#### UK-Led Hormuz Summit (40 Countries) — US Absent

**[VERIFIED]** The United Kingdom is gathering foreign ministers from 40 countries to discuss options to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, a vital shipping route that has been choked off by the United States and Israel's war against Iran. British Prime Minister Keir Starmer said the virtual summit on Thursday, chaired by UK Foreign Secretary Yvette Cooper, "will assess all viable diplomatic and political measures" to restore the waterway. The US is not among the countries attending the meeting.

**[VERIFIED]** The countries participating in Thursday's summit, including the UK, France, Germany, Italy, Canada, Japan, and the United Arab Emirates, have signed a statement demanding that Iran stop its attempts to block the strait.

**[OFFICIAL]** French President Emmanuel Macron says it would be unrealistic to launch a military operation to force open the Strait of Hormuz, after US President Donald Trump challenged US allies to work towards reopening it.

**[VERIFIED]** A British official said it was expected that any first phase would focus on mine-hunting, followed by a second phase to protect tankers crossing the area.

**Assessment:** This represents a significant diplomatic realignment — US allies organizing without Washington to address a crisis caused in part by US policy. The summit acknowledges military action is unrealistic during active conflict, focusing on post-war planning.

#### Iran's "Tehran Toll Booth" — New Leverage Mechanism

**[REPORTED]** Before the war, 110 ships were passing through every day. Today, something like five to 10 ships are passing through every day, and those ships are no longer going unmolested through the middle of the strait. They are being forced into Iranian waters between a series of islands. This has been nicknamed the Tehran toll booth. The Iranians are inspecting these ships as they come by. They are charging some of them $2 million to pass through, and every indication is that the Iranians want to make this into a long-term arrangement, even after the war continues. The idea that the U.S. is going to step away and basically leave the Iranians in control of the Strait of Hormuz, in the hope that they are just going to naturally give up this level of control that they have asserted, is going to be very worrying for people here in the Middle East.

**Assessment:** Iran appears to be institutionalizing control over the strait rather than treating closure as a temporary wartime measure. This significantly complicates any post-conflict normalization and suggests Iran will demand major concessions to restore free passage.

#### Lebanon: Continued Escalation, Displacement Crisis

**[VERIFIED]** At least seven people have been killed in Israeli strikes on southern Lebanon today. An Israeli air raid on the town of Ramadiyeh, in the Tyre district, killed four people and wounded three others. Since Israel launched its offensive on March 2, 1,318 people have been killed in Lebanon and 3,935 injured.

**[VERIFIED]** The Israeli military on Thursday claimed to have killed 40 Hezbollah fighters in 24 hours, as it targeted weapons depots and launch sites.

**[VERIFIED]** Iran fires 4 missile salvos at Israel since midnight, some with cluster warheads; 4 wounded in Bnei Brak, including 2 babies.

**[OFFICIAL]** Lebanese Prime Minister Nawaf Salam said the situation in his country is "very critical", as Israel's invasion worsens Lebanon's displacement crisis.

**[VERIFIED]** Until Iranian attacks and threats all but shut the Strait of Hormuz, Iraq exported the majority of its oil through the strategic waterway. In a statement, the Iraqi oil ministry says it "has begun exporting oil by tanker truck through neighbouring Syria."

#### Houthis: Third Wave Complete, Red Sea Still Hanging

**[VERIFIED]** On 28 March, the Houthis resumed their attacks on Israel amidst the 2026 Iran war. The group has now launched three barrages at Israel but has not yet resumed Red Sea shipping attacks.

**[REPORTED]** Europe's Red Sea naval task force says it is prepared for the resumption of Houthi attacks on shipping in the region. Now there is growing concern that the Houthis could effectively shut down the Bab el-Mandeb (BAM) strait, a narrow stretch of water between Yemen and Djibouti. Doing so would choke off a flow of oil exports from Saudi Arabia, especially to east, exacerbating a huge spike in oil prices after Iran closed off the Strait of Hormuz. Having both straits closed at once is something of a 'sum of all fears' scenario for the global energy marketplace.

**[REPORTED]** While the Strait of Hormuz has been all but closed to shipping, the Houthis have so far remained on the sidelines of the conflict, allowing vessels to access Saudi Arabia's Red Sea ports unhindered. This may be about to change. The ports of Yanbu, King Abdullah and Jeddah have become critical import and export gateways for the Gulf, experiencing a surge in shipping volumes.

**Assessment:** The Houthis are conducting a calculated escalation — attacking Israel to demonstrate solidarity while holding back Red Sea shipping attacks as leverage. A Bab al-Mandeb closure would create a catastrophic "dual chokepoint" scenario.

#### Global Humanitarian Warning

**[VERIFIED]** The ongoing disruption to the Strait of Hormuz threatens not only global energy supplies but also impacts shipping lanes that carry medicine and food into crisis zones and other humanitarian missions, the IRC said. According to the organization, the current crisis looks set to surpass the global food shock in 2022 caused by Russia's war in Ukraine. World leaders and humanitarian groups are now faced with a quickly narrowing window to prevent a sharp rise in world hunger by June 2026, the IRC warned. IRC's CEO David Miliband said: "Now millions of people—including those I met today in Lebanon—are living its consequences on the ground."
