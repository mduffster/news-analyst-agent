### What changed since the last report (new since Apr 1)

1) **A “postwar” Hormuz reopening effort is being organized by the UK — notably without the U.S.**
- The UK convened a **virtual meeting of 40+ countries** to discuss **diplomatic/political measures** to reopen the Strait of Hormuz and address safety for **~20,000 seafarers on ~2,000 ships** affected/trapped by disruption (per AP summary of the meeting’s framing). **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/ca2c6af551df98c81a39f2137e417856))  
- AP reports the **U.S. did not attend**, in the context of Trump messaging that securing Hormuz “is not America’s job,” and urging others to “take it.” **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/ca2c6af551df98c81a39f2137e417856))  

**What it means:** This is a concrete shift toward a **Europe-/coalition-led planning track** for reopening—implicitly accepting that **military forcing functions are off the table while active fighting continues** (mines/anti-ship threats remain). It also increases the chance of a **two-track reality**: (a) U.S./Israel prosecute military pressure, while (b) others build the “day-after” maritime framework. That can reduce immediate escalation risk, but it may also **slow near-term normalization** if insurers/shippers interpret it as “reopening is contingent on end of fighting.” **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/ca2c6af551df98c81a39f2137e417856?utm_source=openai))

2) **Iran demonstrated continued strike capacity against Israel *and* Gulf neighbors, explicitly rebutting U.S. ‘mission accomplished’-style claims**
- AP reports **new Iranian missile fire** toward **Israel and Gulf Arab states** on Thursday, emphasizing Tehran’s ability to keep striking even as Trump claimed the threat was “nearly eliminated.” **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/c41dbdb8148d02ce6561ea6bd4aa0da1))  
- AP also highlighted Iranian military messaging that it retains **hidden stockpiles/production** and that prior U.S. strikes hit “insignificant” facilities. **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/c41dbdb8148d02ce6561ea6bd4aa0da1))  

**What it means:** The “capability signaling” contest is intensifying: Washington is messaging “decisive degradation,” while Tehran is messaging “resilient capacity.” This dynamic tends to **prolong air/sea campaigns** because both sides become incentivized to “prove” their narrative with continued operations.

3) **Trump threat profile broadened further: explicit talk of power-plant (and possibly oil-field) strikes + a 2–3 week coercive horizon**
- Axios reports Trump saying that absent a deal, the U.S. would bomb **Iran’s power plants** and perhaps **oil fields**, and framed the coming period as **2–3 weeks** of heavy pressure. **[REPORTED]** ([axios.com](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/02/trump-bomb-iran-stone-ages-power-plants))  

**What it means:** Threatening **civilian-critical infrastructure** is a major escalation lever even if not executed. It raises (a) incentives for Iran to **retaliate against regional partners** preemptively or asymmetrically, and (b) allied anxiety about being pulled into a widening target set. It also suggests the U.S. is leaning toward a **coercion-through-escalation** model rather than a clean diplomatic offramp in the immediate term.

### Bottom line (incremental)
The big new movement today is **institutional** (UK-led multinational planning for Hormuz reopening) and **signaling** (Iran’s continued strike demonstration + Trump’s broadened threats). Net: this **nudges near-term expectations toward a sustained air/sea + maritime-security problem** rather than quick stabilization.
