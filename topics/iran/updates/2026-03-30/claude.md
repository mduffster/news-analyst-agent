## Key Developments

### Trump's Kharg Island Ultimatum

**[OFFICIAL]** President Donald Trump said Monday that the US will "completely" obliterate Iran's electric generating plants, oil wells and Kharg Island if the strategically vital Strait of Hormuz is not "immediately" reopened and a peace deal is not reached "shortly." "The United States of America is in serious discussions with A NEW, AND MORE REASONABLE, REGIME to end our Military Operations in Iran," Trump said in a post on Truth Social.

Trump told the Financial Times in an interview published Sunday that he wants to "take the oil in Iran" and could seize Kharg Island, which handles about 90% of the country's oil exports. He compared the potential move to US operations in Venezuela, where the United States intends to control the oil industry "indefinitely" following its capture of authoritarian leader Nicolás Maduro in January.

A takeover of the island would require ground troops, and would likely dramatically escalate the war, as Iran has previously warned that any invasion of its islands would "shatter all restraint" and result in bloodshed.

Davis also warned the US may carry out their anticipated ground invasion within the coming week, further undermining prospects of diplomatic progress.

**Assessment:** Trump is escalating maximum pressure rhetoric while simultaneously claiming "great progress" in talks. The explicit threat to desalination plants marks a new level of targeting civilian infrastructure. The Kharg Island seizure option represents a potential shift from air campaign to ground operations.

### Oil Prices Hit Record Monthly Surge

**[VERIFIED]** As of Monday, March 30, 2026, Brent crude is at $115.35 per barrel (+2.47%) and WTI crude at $102.85 per barrel (+3.22%) — continuing to trade near the highest levels since July 2022.

Crude prices have exploded to levels not seen in years, with Brent crude recording a 51 percent monthly gain, placing it on track for its largest monthly surge on record. As of today, Monday, March 30, 2026, Brent crude is trading around $112.57 per barrel, up dramatically from $72.48 at the end of February. At one point during the month, Brent surged as high as $119.50, its highest level since June 2022.

According to market data, this move has now surpassed the previous historic monthly surge of 46 percent recorded in September 1990 during the Gulf War.

Macquarie analysts, in a Friday note, flagged a 40% chance of $200 Brent by June if the war persists, citing cascading effects on shipping fuel, natural gas, fertilizers, plastics, and aluminum.

**Assessment:** Oil prices have increased significantly from the $107 level reported on March 27. The conflict is now creating the worst oil shock since 1990 and is approaching territory that could trigger global recession. The pattern of deadline extensions has not calmed markets.

### Houthis Enter the War

**[VERIFIED]** Yemen's Houthi rebels have attacked Israel with a barrage of ballistic missiles – their first such strikes since the United States-Israeli war on Iran began. Brigadier-General Yahya Saree, a military spokesperson for the Houthis, announced the attack on Saturday on the rebels' Al Masirah satellite television.

While the Houthis started attacking Israel, they have yet to use their "nuclear option" of closing the strait of Bab al-Mandeb in the Red Sea. Doing that would dramatically increase the global economic crisis that has been created due to the war with Iran.

"We have seen over the past two and a half years that Houthis have significant power," Elmasry told Al Jazeera. "If they decided to move to shut down Bab al-Mandeb strait, the Red Sea and, ultimately, the Suez Canal, then we would have two major choke points [closed] along with the Strait of Hormuz."

**Assessment:** This is a significant escalation. The Houthis' entry raises the specter of a "dual chokepoint" crisis if they resume Red Sea attacks. So far they have limited action to missile strikes on Israel rather than shipping interdiction—but this restraint may not last.

### Netanyahu Expands Lebanon Invasion

**[VERIFIED]** Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu has instructed his country's military to further expand invasions of southern Lebanon, as regional tensions spike amid the United States-Israeli war on Iran. "I have just instructed to further expand the existing security buffer zone. We are determined to fundamentally change the situation in the north [of Israel]," Netanyahu said in a video statement from the Northern Command, pushing forward his country's stated bid to replicate the "Gaza model" of occupation.

At least 1,238 people have been killed since Lebanon was dragged into the war on March 2, according to Lebanon's Health Ministry. The toll includes 124 children, while more than 3,500 people have been wounded, the ministry said in a statement. On Saturday and Sunday alone, 49 people were killed, it said, including 10 rescue workers and three journalists.

The United Nations says that more than 1.2 million people have been displaced.

**Assessment:** Despite the IDF manpower crisis flagged in the last report, Netanyahu is doubling down on Lebanon expansion. The explicit "Gaza model" framing and push toward the Litani River suggests Israel intends a prolonged occupation regardless of sustainability concerns.

### Diplomatic Track Advances

**[VERIFIED]** The foreign ministers from four regional powers — Pakistan, Saudi Arabia, Turkey and Egypt — met in Islamabad today and will meet again Monday for a push towards diplomacy to end the war.

Iran has agreed to allow 20 ships under Pakistani flag to pass through the Strait of Hormuz, Pakistan's Foreign Minister Ishaq Dar said in a post on X. Under the agreement, two ships will cross the strait daily, he added. "This is a welcome and constructive gesture by Iran and deserves appreciation," Dar wrote.

**[REPORTED - Iran]** President Donald Trump said Sunday that Iran has agreed to "most of" the 15-point list of demands that the US conveyed, via Pakistan, to end the war. The president's comments come as Pakistan has said it is prepared to host talks between the US and Iran "in coming days."

**[UNCONFIRMED]** Iranian parliament speaker Mohammad-Bagher Ghalibaf struck an aggressive tone, writing: "the enemy publicly signals negotiations while secretly planning a ground invasion." "Our forces are ready for any US ground troops, and our response is clear: We'll never accept humiliation," Ghalibaf said.

**Assessment:** The diplomatic track shows mixed signals. Pakistan's success in securing passage for 20 tankers suggests Iran is willing to make limited gestures. However, Iranian leadership continues to publicly reject negotiations while Trump claims progress. The gap between public posturing and private communications remains wide.

### US Troops Wounded in Saudi Arabia

**[VERIFIED]** On the night between Friday and Saturday (March 28), Iran attacked Prince Sultan Air Base in Saudi Arabia, marking the second attack on the base that week. In the two attacks combined, at least 29 American soldiers were injured, five of them seriously. In the second attack, several refueling aircraft were damaged.

According to a report in The Wall Street Journal, one of the aircraft hit was an E-3 Sentry, used for airborne control and early warning against missile and UAV launches at ranges of hundreds of kilometers.

**Assessment:** Iranian strikes on Saudi Arabia are becoming more effective. The damage to an E-3 Sentry early warning aircraft is operationally significant and could affect US ability to detect incoming threats.

### UK Sends Minesweeper to Hormuz

**[REPORTED]** According to a report by the British newspaper The Times, the United Kingdom is expected to send the amphibious landing ship RFA Lyme Bay in order to assist in reopening the Strait of Hormuz. The vessel is equipped with a fleet of drones intended to assist in clearing naval mines.

**Assessment:** First concrete European military contribution to Hormuz operations, though limited in scope. Germany and other allies remain skeptical of deeper involvement.

### IRGC Threatens US University Campuses

**[VERIFIED - Iran]** Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) said it will target American and Israeli-affiliated universities in the region in retaliation for recent attacks on Iranian higher education centers. In a statement on Sunday, the IRGC said the institutions are now considered "legitimate targets until two universities are struck." The group warned students, staff and nearby residents to stay at least one kilometer away from American university campuses in the region. The IRGC also demanded that the US government condemn the reported strikes on Iranian universities via an official statement by March 30.

US universities that have campuses in the Gulf include Texas A&M and Northwestern universities in Qatar, and New York University in the United Arab Emirates.

**Assessment:** The March 30 deadline for US condemnation has now passed. Watch for potential IRGC retaliation against Gulf-based American university campuses.
