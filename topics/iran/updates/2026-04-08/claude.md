### Key Developments

#### Ceasefire Agreement — A Dramatic Reversal

**[OFFICIAL]** On 7 April at 6:32 p.m. ET (8 April, 2:02 a.m. IRST), President Trump announced via Truth Social that the United States and Iran had reached an agreement. The United States, Israel, and Iran agreed to a two-week ceasefire, under which Iran would also re-open the Strait of Hormuz.

**[OFFICIAL]** Trump, in his post announcing the two-week delay, claimed the U.S. had agreed to halt its planned attacks because "we have already met and exceeded all Military objectives, and are very far along with a definitive Agreement concerning Longterm PEACE with Iran, and PEACE in the Middle East." The move was "subject to the Islamic Republic of Iran agreeing to the COMPLETE, IMMEDIATE, and SAFE OPENING of the Strait of Hormuz."

**[OFFICIAL]** Iran's Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi wrote: "For a period of two weeks, safe passage through the Strait of Hormuz will be possible via coordination with Iran's Armed Forces and with due consideration of technical limitations." Araghchi also said Iran will stop military attacks as long as it is not attacked.

**[VERIFIED]** Moments later, Iranian officials confirmed that Khamenei had approved the ceasefire after a last-minute nudge by China.

**Assessment:** The ceasefire represents a significant de-escalation from Trump's apocalyptic rhetoric just hours earlier. Both sides are claiming victory. Iran's caveats about "technical limitations" and "coordination" for Hormuz transit provide room for interpretation. The ceasefire clock expires on April 21-22.

#### Lebanon Crisis Intensifies Despite Regional Ceasefire

**[VERIFIED]** Hours after the ceasefire announcement, Israel expanded its attacks on Lebanon. A wave of powerful blasts was felt across Beirut in what the Israeli military called its biggest strike of the war against more than 100 targets.

**[OFFICIAL]** The Israel Defense Forces (IDF) said today that it completed the largest coordinated strike in Lebanon since the war began, claiming it targeted more than 100 command centers and military sites of Hezbollah. "Most of the infrastructure that was struck was located within the heart of the civilian population," the statement said.

**[OFFICIAL]** Netanyahu's office stated the two-week ceasefire "does not include Lebanon."

**[REPORTED]** Lebanon's health ministry said dozens were killed and hundreds wounded in an early estimate from the Beirut strikes.

**[REPORTED]** Before the wave of new strikes, a Hezbollah official told The Associated Press that the group was giving a chance for mediators to secure a ceasefire in Lebanon, but "we have not announced our adherence to the ceasefire since the Israelis are not adhering to it."

**[VERIFIED]** According to Lebanese authorities, Israeli attacks on Lebanon have killed more than 1,500 people since March 2 and displaced more than 1.2 million. The Israeli military has also launched an invasion of southern Lebanon.

**Assessment:** The Lebanon carve-out is the ceasefire's most significant weakness. Israel is using the pause in Iran operations to intensify its campaign against Hezbollah. This creates potential for the conflict to re-escalate through the Lebanese front.

#### Ceasefire Violations and Continued Attacks

**[VERIFIED]** The United Arab Emirates (UAE) on Wednesday said its air defences "are actively engaging" incoming missiles and drones from Iran. The UAE's Defence Ministry said the sounds of explosions heard across the country were caused by air defence systems intercepting ballistic missiles, cruise missiles, and unmanned aerial vehicles.

**[VERIFIED]** Kuwait said its air defences were intercepting a wave of Iranian drones launched since 8am. The Kuwaiti army said some drones targeted vital oil facilities, power stations and water desalination plants, causing serious infrastructure damage. Kuwait's Defence Ministry said a total of 28 drones were dealt with.

**[VERIFIED]** Iranian state television reported that an oil refinery on the country's Lavan Island came under attack on Wednesday. Explosions were also reported on Iran's Sirri island.

**[REPORTED]** The drone attacks on Gulf states "took place hours after the targeting of oil facilities on Lavan Island in Iran," Iranian state broadcaster IRIB reported. The official student news agency, SNN, said the attacks were carried out in response to the "clear violation of the ceasefire."

**[REPORTED]** Despite continued attacks, there is hope in the Gulf that the ceasefire will hold. Iran has decentralized their military operations, and the violations could just be "a loose chain" in communication of military command, Al Jazeera reported.

**Assessment:** The "fog of ceasefire" is creating mutual accusations of violations. Iran claims it was struck first (Lavan Island), while Gulf states claim Iranian attacks continued after the ceasefire. This mutual recrimination could undermine the truce.

#### Islamabad Talks — Direct Negotiations Set for Friday

**[OFFICIAL]** Pakistan's Prime Minister Sharif invited delegations to Islamabad on Friday, 10th April 2026. Iran has agreed to a two-week ceasefire with the United States, with its National Security Council saying talks with Washington will begin in Islamabad on Friday, based on Tehran's 10-point proposal.

**[REPORTED]** According to Iranian state media, the Iranian delegation will be led by Speaker of Parliament Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf. The United States delegation will be headed by Vice President James David Vance. The Islamabad talks represent the first in-person negotiations between the two adversaries since the war began.

**[OFFICIAL]** White House press secretary Karoline Leavitt told CBS News that "there are discussions about in person talks, but nothing is final until announced by the President or the White House."

**[OFFICIAL]** According to Iran's National Security Council, its 10-point proposal calls for Iranian dominance and oversight of the Strait of Hormuz. The proposal also calls for the withdrawal of all "US combat forces" from bases in the Middle East, the lifting of all sanctions, and release of frozen Iranian assets.

**[VERIFIED]** The council said that while Tehran has agreed to talks, it does so "with complete distrust of the American side." The council added that Iran stood ready to respond with "full force" as soon as "the slightest mistake by the enemy is made."

#### Pentagon Briefing and Military Assessment

**[OFFICIAL]** Gen. Dan Caine, the chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, welcomed the ceasefire agreement but said the U.S. remains ready to continue combat operations "if called upon." Caine honored the lives of the 13 service members killed in the operation.

**[OFFICIAL]** Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth said: "We know exactly what they have, and they know that, and they will either give it to us voluntarily...or if we have to do something else ourselves, like we did in Midnight Hammer, we reserve that opportunity. What the new Iranian regime knows is they'll never have a nuclear weapon or the capability to get a path to one."

**[OFFICIAL]** Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth and Gen. Dan Caine told reporters that the Strait of Hormuz has reopened. Hegseth said ships "will be sailing" through Hormuz. "It's time for the rest of the world to step up and ensure that stays open."

**[VERIFIED]** Shipping data showed little movement in the waterway more than six hours after the ceasefire was announced, with one analyst warning vessels and insurers would need to see further positive signs before resuming traffic through the vital waterway.

#### Oil Prices and Market Reaction

**[VERIFIED]** Oil prices are tumbling and stocks surging today, after Trump agreed to a two-week ceasefire with Iran. WTI, the US crude benchmark, dropped 16% to just below $95 a barrel – still well above the $67 level where it traded before the war began. Brent crude futures slipped 13.5% to $94 a barrel.

**[VERIFIED]** Crude Oil fell to 93.13 USD/Bbl on April 8, 2026, down 17.55% from the previous day.

**[VERIFIED]** S&P 500 futures were up 2.3% on the day. Asian markets spiked in morning trading, with Tokyo's Nikkei 225 up 5.1% and Hong Kong's Hang Seng up 2.4%.

**[VERIFIED]** It will take months for jet fuel supplies and prices to normalize even if the Strait of Hormuz remains open, the director general of the International Air Transport Association (IATA) said. "It will still take a period of months to get back to where supply needs to be," Willie Walsh said.

#### US Military Casualties Update

**[VERIFIED]** Thirteen American service members have died in the war in Iran since February 28. About 373 more U.S. service members have been wounded, U.S. Central Command confirmed. The "vast majority" of those have been minor injuries; 330 of the wounded service members have returned to duty. Five service members are "seriously wounded."

**[REPORTED]** The U.S. and Israel have launched more than 3,000 strikes on Iran since the conflict erupted on Feb. 28, and Iran has retaliated with a total of 1,511 strikes against targets in Israel and Gulf countries, according to ACLED.

#### Iraqi Militias Suspend Operations

**[OFFICIAL]** Pro-Iran armed factions in Iraq announced on Wednesday a two-week halt to their attacks on "enemy bases" in the region. "The Islamic Resistance in Iraq announces the suspension of its operations in Iraq and the region for two weeks," the alliance said. Iraq later reopened its airspace.

#### American Journalist Released

**[OFFICIAL]** Freelance journalist Shelly Kittleson has been released a week after she was kidnapped by Kataib Hezbollah, the Iranian-backed Iraqi militant group. "We are relieved that this American is now free," Secretary of State Marco Rubio wrote on X.

### Emerging Issues to Watch

1. **Lebanon as the new flashpoint**: Israel's "biggest strike" hours into ceasefire and Netanyahu's explicit exclusion of Lebanon creates a potential vector for conflict resumption. Hezbollah's response will be critical.

2. **Islamabad talks (Friday, April 10)**: VP Vance vs. Speaker Ghalibaf in first direct US-Iran negotiations since war began. Key issues: nuclear material, sanctions, Hormuz control, US troop withdrawal.

3. **Ceasefire verification**: Who struck Lavan Island? Were Iranian attacks on Gulf states pre-ordered? The mutual accusations of violations could provide pretexts for resumption.

4. **Hormuz reopening reality**: Pentagon claims strait is "open" but shipping data showed little movement in the waterway more than six hours after the ceasefire was announced. Actual traffic resumption will test Iran's commitment.

5. **Iran's 10-point demands**: Iran's maximalist position (Hormuz oversight, US troop withdrawal, sanctions relief, frozen asset release) is far from US positions. Bridging this gap in 2 weeks appears unlikely.

6. **Gulf interceptor depletion**: With 75-87% of Patriot stocks depleted, another Iranian attack wave could overwhelm defenses. This creates pressure on all parties.

### Bottom Line

**The immediate crisis has passed, but the war is not over.**

A two-week ceasefire between the United States and Iran has halted 40 days of US-Israeli attacks on Iran that had pushed the region to the brink of a wider war. The truce, brokered by Pakistan, follows fierce exchanges of air strikes, missile attacks and threats.

However, three critical risks remain:

1. **Lebanon**: Israel's explicit exclusion and immediate escalation means one theater of the war continues unabated.

2. **Violations**: Both sides report attacks after the ceasefire announcement. Iran enters talks with "complete distrust" and its armed forces have "hands on the trigger."

3. **Fundamental gaps**: Iran demands Hormuz oversight, US troop withdrawal, and full sanctions relief. The US demands uranium handover and no enrichment. These positions are incompatible without major concessions.

The Islamabad talks Friday will be the first real test of whether this ceasefire is a prelude to peace or merely a pause before escalation resumes. The clock is ticking toward April 21-22.
