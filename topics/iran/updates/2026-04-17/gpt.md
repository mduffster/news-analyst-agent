### 1) Strait of Hormuz: Iran says “fully open,” but the U.S. blockade stays
- **Iran’s foreign minister** said the Strait of Hormuz is now **fully open to commercial vessels**. **[OFFICIAL]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/4bd5a29af608ecbd72356559b3c55d67))  
- **Trump** publicly welcomed the reopening message—but then clarified the **U.S. naval blockade on Iranian ships and ports “will remain in full force”** until a broader deal with Iran is completed (explicitly including the nuclear file). **[OFFICIAL]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/4bd5a29af608ecbd72356559b3c55d67))  

**What it means:** This is a meaningful shift in the coercion/diplomacy balance: Washington is signaling it will **decouple “waterway open” from “pressure removed.”** That lowers immediate global-shipping panic risk (if traffic truly resumes) while **keeping day-to-day incident risk elevated** because blockade enforcement still requires intercepts/diversions—and the first serious miscalculation remains a key escalation trigger. **[VERIFIED]** (Iran reopening + Trump continuing blockade are both directly documented) ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/4bd5a29af608ecbd72356559b3c55d67))

### 2) Ceasefire-extension track: reporting of an “in-principle” extension understanding

**What it means:** Compared with yesterday’s “mediation is more overt,” today adds a clearer (though not finalized) signal that an extension is being **socialized as viable**. The key caveat is that “in-principle” + unnamed sources still leaves room for last-minute failure—especially if a blockade encounter turns kinetic or if Israel/Iran dispute Lebanon-linked conditions.
