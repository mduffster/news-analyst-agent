## Major Developments Since Last Report

### Israel Claims Killing of Ali Larijani and Basij Commander

**[OFFICIAL - Israel, UNCONFIRMED by Iran]** Israel's Defence Minister Katz says Iran's security chief Ali Larijani has been killed. The Israeli army also said it killed Basij force commander Gholamreza Soleimani in an attack last night. Iran has not commented on the claims yet.

Israel says it killed Ali Larijani, Iran's top security official and one of its most powerful decision-makers. The head of the Basij, Iran's feared paramilitary force, has also been killed, Israel said.

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu responded Tuesday to the targeted killing, stating Israel is "undermining this regime in the hope of giving the Iranian people a chance to remove it." In pre-recorded video remarks, Netanyahu described Larijani as "the boss of the Revolutionary Guards – that gang of thugs who actually run Iran."

This is a significant escalation in the decapitation strategy. If confirmed, Larijani's death removes one of Iran's most experienced security officials at a critical moment.

### Joe Kent Resigns as Counterterrorism Chief Over Iran War

**[VERIFIED]** Joe Kent, who led the National Counterterrorism Center and was a top aide to intelligence director Tulsi Gabbard, became the first senior Trump administration official to resign over the war in Iran. Kent's stinging rebuke — that Trump launched the war under pressure from Israel despite Iran posing "no imminent threat" to the U.S. — underscores the discomfort many in the "America First" camp feel about the war.

"I cannot in good conscience support the ongoing war in Iran," Kent wrote. "Iran posed no imminent threat to our nation, and it is clear that we started this war due to pressure from Israel and its powerful American lobby."

Kent, a former Army Ranger and CIA paramilitary officer, announced that Iran was not an imminent threat to the United States, which the president and his administration have claimed in order to justify attacking the nation. "As a veteran who deployed to combat 11 times and as a Gold Star husband who lost my beloved wife Shannon in a war manufactured by Israel, I cannot support sending the next generation" to fight.

Kent's departure leaves the United States without a director of the National Counterterrorism Center during a war.

### European Allies Firmly Reject Hormuz Coalition

**[VERIFIED]** Germany sees no role for NATO in policing the strait. "As long as this war continues, there will be no involvement, not even in an option to keep the Strait of Hormuz open by military means," Stefan Kornelius, a spokesperson for German Chancellor Friedrich Merz, said. "I would also like to remind you that the U.S. and Israel did not consult us before the war, and that Washington explicitly stated at the start of the war that European assistance was neither necessary nor desired."

U.K. Prime Minister Keir Starmer rebuffed Trump's demands, telling reporters he is working with allies on a plan to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, but it won't be a NATO mission. "While taking the necessary action to defend ourselves and our allies, we will not be drawn into the wider war."

To date, no country has confirmed its involvement. In contrast, several countries have already doused the prospect, with Australia, Japan, Poland and Sweden and Spain saying they had no intentions of sending military ships. On Monday, several more European leaders joined the chorus, with German Defence Minister Boris Pistorius saying that there would be "no military participation."

### US Embassy Baghdad Targeted

**[VERIFIED]** A blast was heard close to the US Embassy in Baghdad, Iraq, as videos geolocated by CNN appeared to show air defense systems engaging a projectile close to the compound.

An Iran-aligned militia group fired a combination of drones and rockets at the U.S. Embassy.

### Marine Expeditionary Unit En Route

**[VERIFIED]** A US Navy warship believed to be carrying Marine ground troops to the Middle East has been tracked off Singapore.

The deployment will add thousands of Marines, several warships, and F-35 fighter jets to support those already in the region. The U.S. official said U.S. Central Command asked for the new force in order to have more options for military operations against Iran. The Marine expeditionary unit will be able to conduct ground operations if ordered.

### UAE Continues Taking Heavy Fire

**[VERIFIED]** The United Arab Emirates' air defenses have "engaged" more than 300 ballistic missiles, 15 cruise missiles and over 1,600 drones since the Iran war started, the defense ministry said. The attacks have resulted in the deaths of two members of its armed forces as well as six fatalities of Pakistani, Nepali, Bangladeshi, and Palestinian nationalities. On Tuesday, the UAE's defenses engaged 10 ballistic missiles and 45 drones from Iran.

The government of the UAE's Fujairah says a fire broke out at its oil industry zone after being attacked by drones. Falling debris from a missile intercepted by air defences has killed a person in the Bani Yas area of Abu Dhabi. The UAE also announced a temporary closure of its airspace.

### Military Casualty and Damage Update

**[VERIFIED]** On 14 March, the Hengaw Organization for Human Rights estimated that 4,400+ members of Iranian military forces had been killed in the attacks.

Trump said that more than 100 Iranian naval vessels have been "sunk or destroyed" since the US and Israel launched the war on February 28, including more than 30 "mine laying ships." He further claimed a 90 percent reduction in Iran's ballistic missile launches and a 95 percent reduction in drone attacks. All told, he said, the US and Israel have struck more than 7,000 targets across Iran.

Since the beginning of the campaign, the IDF has carried out more than 7,600 strikes across Iran as part of nearly 5,000 aerial sorties.

### Civilian Toll Continues to Mount

**[VERIFIED]** An Amnesty International investigation confirmed that a US attack on an Iranian primary school killed at least 170 people, most of whom were schoolgirls.

Government spokesperson, Fatemeh Mohajerani, says US-Israeli strikes have damaged more than 42,000 civilian sites across Iran, including homes, schools and hospitals.

### Congressional Standoff Intensifies

**[VERIFIED]** A group of six Democrats has said that unless hearings are scheduled with Hegseth, Secretary of State Marco Rubio and other Cabinet officials, they will call up daily votes on a series of war powers resolutions. The votes would eat up valuable time on the Senate floor.

The United States' war with Iran is entering its third week, but Congress has yet to publicly test the Trump administration's case for the conflict. Sen. Cory Booker said, "We've had no oversight whatsoever over what the executive is doing as we're spending a billion dollars a day."

"Let me be clear: I will not support a blank check for this war of choice," Sen. Jack Reed, the senior Democrat on the Senate Armed Services Committee, said Wednesday on the chamber floor.
