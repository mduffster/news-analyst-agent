### Israel Claims Killing of Ali Larijani — Still Unconfirmed by Iran

**[OFFICIAL - Israel; UNCONFIRMED by Iran]** The IDF said Larijani, considered the Iranian regime's de facto wartime leader, was targeted in a safe house in Tehran overnight. It added that it had also killed the commander of Iran's internal security force, the Basij, along with other senior officials.

Iranian state media published a handwritten note by Larijani, though it was not clear whether it was intended as proof of the life of the senior official. Larijani's note, published on his social media pages, commemorates 84 Iranian sailors, whose funeral is expected on Tuesday, killed in a US attack on their naval ship in international waters.

After Ayatollah Ali Khamenei was killed in U.S.-Israeli strikes on Feb. 28, Larijani "functioned" as the de facto leader of the Iranian regime, the IDF said. According to the statement, Larijani was the target of a "precise strike" while he was "located near Tehran."

**Significance:** Ali Larijani was more than a symbolic figurehead or a frontline commander for the Iranian regime. The 67-year-old is a vital coordinator operating in the regime's power center, a man who kept Iran's sprawling, factionalized system functioning under extreme stress. As secretary of the Supreme National Security Council, Larijani sat at the intersection of military planning, internal security, diplomacy, and the authority of the Supreme Leader.

Larijani was seen as a stabilizing figure during Iran's uncertain leadership transition following the death of Khamenei on February 28. His death would remove one of the last figures with the credibility to manage that transition quietly.

### Basij Commander Also Claimed Killed

**[OFFICIAL - Israel; UNCONFIRMED by Iran]** The Israeli military said it killed the head of Iran's Basij paramilitary force in a targeted strike on Monday. The Israel Defense Forces (IDF) said the killing of Gholamreza Soleimani "constitutes an additional significant blow to the regime's security command-and-control."

The Basij famously mounted "human wave" attacks against Iraqi forces during the eight-year Iran-Iraq war in the 1980s. This century, however, it has focused more on domestic security. It has an estimated 450,000 members and plays a key role in suppressing anti-government demonstrations.
