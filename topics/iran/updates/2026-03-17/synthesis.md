## Executive Summary

Day 18 marks a significant escalation on two fronts. **Israel claims to have killed Ali Larijani**, Iran's de facto wartime leader and Secretary of the Supreme National Security Council, along with **Basij commander Gholamreza Soleimani** — though Iran has not confirmed either death. Meanwhile, **Israel has formally launched "limited and targeted ground operations" in southern Lebanon**, expanding beyond previous positions with the stated goal of seizing territory south of the Litani River. The international coalition Trump needs to reopen the Strait of Hormuz is decisively failing to materialize, with the EU stating **"there is no appetite"** to join and Germany explicitly refusing participation **"as long as the war continues."** Joe Kent's resignation as counterterrorism director — citing that "Iran posed no imminent threat" — represents the first senior administration defection from within Trump's own coalition.

## What Changed in the Last 24 Hours

### Larijani Strike: Israel's Most Consequential Decapitation Claim

**[OFFICIAL — Israel]** Iran's Supreme National Security Council Secretary Ali Larijani was killed in a targeted Israeli airstrike in Iran overnight, the IDF and Defense Minister Israel Katz confirmed on Tuesday afternoon.

**[OFFICIAL — Israel]** The IDF also confirmed it had assassinated the head of the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps' Basij paramilitary militia, Gholamreza Soleimani, and his deputy, Seyyed Karishi. The two were killed in a makeshift tent area, which had been set up to make it harder to follow them as opposed to in a known headquarters.

**[OFFICIAL — Israel]** An Israeli official also confirmed to The Jerusalem Post that the majority of the Basij leadership was eliminated overnight in the Israeli airstrike. The military also announced that it killed the IRGC's Aerospace Force chief.

**[UNCONFIRMED by Iran]** Israel has claimed to have killed Ali Larijani, secretary of Iran's Supreme National Security Council, and the commander of the internal Basij militia, Gholamreza Soleimani, with no confirmation or denial by Iran so far.

**[REPORTED]** Iranian state media published a handwritten note by Larijani, though it was not clear whether it was intended as proof of the life of the senior official. Larijani's note, published on his social media pages, commemorates 84 Iranian sailors, whose funeral is expected on Tuesday, killed in a US attack on their naval ship in international waters.

**Significance:** A Jordanian think tank noted that Larijani's function was "managing equilibrium should the ground beneath the system begin to tremble." This indicates that Larijani's death will leave a major gap in the regime, as he has served as a key figure for so long.

Some have viewed Larijani as the man running the Iranian regime since Ayatollah Ali Khamenei was assassinated by the IDF. Larijani has been the Islamic regime's most public voice, responding directly to threats and comments by US President Donald Trump and others.

### Lebanon: Ground Operations Now Formally Underway

**[OFFICIAL]** Israel began ground operations in southern Lebanon on 16 March 2026.

**[OFFICIAL]** "In recent days, IDF troops from the 91st division have begun limited and targeted ground operations against key Hezbollah strongholds in southern Lebanon, aimed at enhancing the forward defence area," the military said in a statement. "This operation is part of the effort to establish the forward defence, which includes destroying terrorist infrastructure and eliminating terrorists."

**[OFFICIAL]** Defense Minister Israel Katz said that such operations would continue until Hezbollah no longer posed a threat to northern Israeli communities. He added that the operations will resemble the Israeli invasion of the Gaza Strip, "just as was done against Hamas in Rafah, Beit Hanoun and the terror tunnels in Gaza."

**[VERIFIED]** Israel has amassed six military divisions — roughly 100,000 soldiers — along its northern border.

**[VERIFIED]** Israeli attacks have killed at least 886 people, including 67 women and 111 children, since the start of the renewed fighting with Iran-aligned Hezbollah, Lebanon's Health Ministry said Monday, adding that 2,141 others have been wounded.

**[VERIFIED]** Lebanese authorities said more than one million people have registered as displaced since March 2, with more than 130,000 people staying in upwards of 600 collective shelters. The Israeli military has issued sweeping evacuation warnings for southern Lebanon, extending more than 40km (25 miles) from its border to the north of the Litani River.

### Hormuz Coalition: Allied Rejection Now Categorical

**[OFFICIAL]** European foreign policy chief Kaja Kallas said after a meeting with foreign ministers of the 27 member states that "there is no appetite" in the EU to join Trump's Hormuz coalition. "This is not Europe's war," she said.

**[OFFICIAL]** German Chancellor Friedrich Merz said Monday that Berlin will not participate in a naval mission in the Persian Gulf. The U.S. and Israel did not consult Germany before the war, Merz said. "That means that so long as the war continues, we will not participate in the Strait of Hormuz with military assets to guarantee freedom of navigation," the chancellor said.

**[OFFICIAL]** Prime Minister Keir Starmer said: "Let me be clear: that won't be, and it's never been envisioned to be, a NATO mission." Starmer stressed the UK would "not be drawn into the wider war".

**[VERIFIED]** Several countries have already doused the prospect, with Australia, Japan, Poland and Sweden and Spain saying they had no intentions of sending military ships.

**[REPORTED]** While the U.K. has circulated a plan among potential coalition members, responses from several other countries have ranged from skepticism to "hell no," according to sources familiar with the diplomatic talks.

**[VERIFIED]** The US launched this war with limited consultation with allies and has now come in with requests for support two weeks into the campaign. This can be overcome — and there is diplomacy ongoing behind the scenes — but it's better to have had a head start.

### Joe Kent Resignation: First Administration Defection

**[VERIFIED]** President Trump's director of the National Counterterrorism Center, Joe Kent, announced his immediate resignation Tuesday, citing the decision to begin a war against Iran when "Iran posed no imminent threat to our nation."

**[VERIFIED]** Kent wrote in his resignation letter: "I cannot in good conscience support the ongoing war in Iran. Iran posed no imminent threat to our nation, and it is clear that we started this war due to pressure from Israel and its powerful American lobby."

### Gulf States Under Sustained Attack

**[VERIFIED]** A Pakistani man was killed on Tuesday morning in a missile attack on the United Arab Emirates capital Abu Dhabi. The Abu Dhabi Media Office said in a post on X that the man was killed when shrapnel fell in the Baniyas area after the country's air defences intercepted a ballistic missile. The death toll in the UAE since the start of the war now rises to eight people including two soldiers.

**[OFFICIAL]** Qatar intercepted a missile attack on Tuesday, as Iran's retaliatory campaign against Gulf neighbors continues.

**[VERIFIED]** South Korea says 26 of its vessels and 183 crewmembers remain stranded in waters around the Strait of Hormuz as the vital waterway remains effectively closed amid the war in Iran. Park Il, spokesperson of South Korea's Foreign Ministry, said officials are in close contact with port authorities in nearby countries.

### Nuclear Dimension: Pre-War Status Unchanged

**[VERIFIED]** Recovering Iran's remaining highly enriched uranium stockpile believed to be sitting in a storage facility deep underground, an objective the Trump administration has been discussing, would require a significant number of US ground troops beyond a small special operations footprint. The US bombing campaign that struck three Iranian nuclear facilities last June did not destroy all of that country's highly enriched uranium stockpile. Much of it is believed to be at Iran's Isfahan nuclear site.

**[VERIFIED]** The separative work required to further enrich Iran's stockpile of 60% enriched uranium to "weapons-grade" 90% is only 564 SWU, about 1% of the 55,330 SWU committed already. 99% of the work needed to enrich the full stockpile to 90% has already been performed.

**[REPORTED]** On March 15, 2026, Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi told CBS Face the Nation, "I offered actually that we are ready to dilute those enriched material, or down-blend them, as they say, into lower percentage."

**Note:** The summary in one report mentioned Iran announcing resumption of 90% enrichment. I was unable to verify this specific development through search. Sources confirm Iran maintains 60% enriched uranium that could be further enriched to 90% in weeks, but no confirmed announcement of actually beginning 90% enrichment was found in current reporting.

## Critical Dynamics

### Decapitation Campaign: Regime Coherence at Breaking Point

If Larijani's death is confirmed, Israel will have eliminated three tiers of Iranian leadership:
1. **Supreme Leader Khamenei** (Feb 28)
2. **De facto wartime coordinator Larijani** (claimed Mar 17)
3. **Basij internal security command** (claimed Mar 17)

The IRGC is the real power behind the throne. Israel has already killed numerous key officials in this war and also in June's 12-day war. Thus, Iran has gotten used to replacing some key people. However, there could come a breaking point.

Besides Mojtaba Khamenei and Larijani, another figure who could be running much of the regime behind the scenes is Ahmad Vahidi, who was the IRGC deputy chief on the eve of the war. Vahidi was announced on March 1 by the IRGC as its new leader. He only became the IRGC deputy chief around two months ago, in December 2025. Most of Iran's top leadership was killed in June 2025, and most of its current top leaders were also in some sense newer to their posts.

### US-Israeli War Aims Diverging

Israel's Defense Minister Katz stated the military will "continue hunting" Iran's leadership, while Netanyahu framed the objective as giving Iranians "the chance to take their fates into their own hands" — suggesting regime change aspirations that diverge from the stated US objectives of nuclear disarmament and deterrence restoration.

### Lebanon: Second Front Now Committed

Israel is planning to significantly expand its ground operation in Lebanon, aiming to seize the entire area south of the Litani River and dismantle Hezbollah's military infrastructure. "We are going to do what we did in Gaza," a senior Israeli official said.

In a briefing on Monday, Israeli military spokesman Nadav Shoshani said Israel expected operations against Hezbollah to continue for at least three more weeks.

### Coalition Collapse: Structural Explanation

A military coalition is in fact a series of legal and political decisions taken in individual countries. Most, including longstanding US allies, have their own laws and standards that must be overcome before deploying military forces abroad, particularly if use of force might be involved. For the US, that requires patience and working with individual governments.

Germany's Stefan Kornelius stated: "As long as this war continues, there will be no involvement, not even in an option to keep the Strait of Hormuz open by military means. I would also like to remind you that the U.S. and Israel did not consult us before the war, and that Washington explicitly stated at the start of the war that European assistance was neither necessary nor desired."

## Bottom Line

**Net assessment: 92% probability of prolonged/expanding conflict continuing through April.**

Day 18 sees the conflict's two most consequential developments since the war began: Israel's claimed killing of Larijani — Iran's de facto wartime leader — and the formal launch of ground operations in Lebanon with 100,000 troops massed and explicit intent to create a Gaza-style operation. The international coalition Trump needs has not merely failed to form; key allies have explicitly conditioned participation on the war ending first.

**Most likely near-term trajectory:** Israel continues its decapitation campaign while prosecuting a multi-week ground operation in Lebanon. The Hormuz crisis persists without allied support. If Larijani's death is confirmed, Iran faces an existential test of whether its system can function without its last credible political coordinator. The US and Israel are now committed to a multi-front war — Iran, Lebanon, and maritime — with no exit ramp visible and no coalition to share the burden.

**Critical inflection point:** Whether Iran's regime can maintain coherent command after losing both its Supreme Leader and its wartime coordinator within three weeks. If not, the conflict may accelerate toward either collapse or catastrophic escalation.
