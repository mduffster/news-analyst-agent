## Daily incremental update — US–Israel–Iran conflict  
**As of Mon, May 4, 2026 (ET)**  
**What changed since Fri, May 1, 2026 (ET)**

### 1) U.S. shifts from “blockade-only” to active **freedom-of-navigation push** in Hormuz (“Project Freedom”)
- **CENTCOM announced it will support “Project Freedom” starting May 4** to restore commercial transit through the Strait of Hormuz, specifying a major force package (guided-missile destroyers, **100+ aircraft**, unmanned platforms, and **~15,000 personnel**). **[OFFICIAL]** ([centcom.mil](https://www.centcom.mil/MEDIA/PRESS-RELEASES/Press-Release-View/Article/4476318/us-military-supports-launch-of-project-freedom-in-strait-of-hormuz/?utm_source=openai))  
- The U.S. military said **two U.S.-flagged merchant ships transited** Hormuz successfully; U.S. destroyers also transited. **[VERIFIED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/a4857f28d9b47e0170b65ced19451a25))  
- Axios reports **rules of engagement were changed**, authorizing strikes on “immediate threats” (e.g., IRGC fast boats / missile positions) against ships attempting transit. **[REPORTED]** ([axios.com](https://www.axios.com/2026/05/04/strait-hormuz-us-merchant-ships-cross))  

**What it means:** This is a material escalation in *risk* (even if framed as “humanitarian”). The U.S. is now testing Iran’s claimed control of the strait directly rather than relying primarily on economic strangulation via the port blockade. That raises the chance of **rapid tactical incidents** (warning shots, miscalculation, ship damage) that could collapse the “terminated/paused” political framing and re-open the question of whether the conflict is *functionally* back in active hostilities.

---

### 2) Iranian military threatens force; dispute over whether Iran fired/struck U.S. ships
- Iranian messaging: Iran’s military said ships must **coordinate with Iran** and warned foreign forces approaching/entering could be **targeted**. **[VERIFIED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/a4857f28d9b47e0170b65ced19451a25))  
- Iranian outlets (incl. IRGC-linked claims) said Iran **hit** or **forced back** a U.S. ship; **CENTCOM denied any U.S. Navy ships were struck**. **[VERIFIED]** (denial) / **[REPORTED]** (claims) ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/a4857f28d9b47e0170b65ced19451a25))  

**What it means:** Even if today’s contact stayed at “warnings,” the signaling suggests Iran is prepared to contest the corridor—creating a higher baseline probability of an incident cycle (attack → retaliation → counter-retaliation) centered on **Hormuz**, not the original strike campaign.

---

### 3) Maritime/energy-security incidents around the UAE add escalation pressure
- AP reports the UAE issued **three missile alerts**, and authorities said an **Iranian drone hit an oil facility** in Fujairah; AP also notes a cargo ship ablaze off the UAE coast and an explosion/fire on a South Korean-operated ship in/near the strait (cause unclear). **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/a4857f28d9b47e0170b65ced19451a25))  
- UKMTO-related reporting indicates a **tanker struck by projectiles** off Fujairah (details/attribution not yet definitive). **[REPORTED]** ([business-standard.com](https://www.business-standard.com/world-news/ship-struck-by-projectiles-in-strait-of-hormuz-says-british-military-126050400157_1.html?utm_source=openai))  

**What it means:** The conflict’s center of gravity is drifting toward **regional shipping and infrastructure risk**. That tends to (a) strengthen U.S./partner resolve to keep the lane open, but (b) also offers Iran/proxies multiple deniable ways to raise costs without a clean “Iran attacked a U.S. warship” threshold.

---

### 4) Diplomacy: Iran’s “new proposal” appears more concrete, via Pakistan mediation channel
- AP reports Iran delivered a new proposal via **Pakistani mediators**; Trump said he is reviewing it. **[VERIFIED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/9317c11e51ba8dfa527b424f931fe04b?utm_source=openai))  
- AP also summarizes Iranian-linked outlets describing elements of Iran’s position (lift sanctions, end blockade, withdraw U.S. forces/cease hostilities; and reportedly *not* addressing the nuclear file in that proposal). **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/a4857f28d9b47e0170b65ced19451a25))  

**What it means:** The diplomatic channel remains active, but the immediate military reality (Project Freedom + incidents) is now moving faster than the talks—raising the odds that “negotiations” become crisis-management rather than an orderly off-ramp.

---

## Net assessment (since May 1)
The biggest change is the U.S. **forcing a live test** of Hormuz access beginning **May 4**—a move that increases the probability of **near-term kinetic incidents** even if leaders still claim they want de-escalation. Diplomacy continues via Pakistan, but it’s now competing with an operational tempo that can produce irreversible escalatory moments.
