## Daily Update: US-Israel-Iran Conflict — May 4, 2026

### Executive Summary

**BREAKING: "Project Freedom" launches — MAJOR ESCALATION today. US destroyers entered Persian Gulf, 2 US-flagged merchant ships transited Hormuz. Iran claims it struck US frigate (denied by CENTCOM). Iran attacked UAE with missiles and drones — fire at Fujairah oil facility. South Korean cargo ship also reported hit. Oil surged 5% on naval confrontation. Hezbollah's Qassem declares "there is no ceasefire" in Lebanon. IMO reports ~2,000 vessels and 20,000 seafarers stranded.**

Day 66 of the conflict. The ceasefire is now under extreme strain as the US directly challenges Iran's control of the Strait of Hormuz. The first shots in what could become a direct naval confrontation were fired today.

---

### Critical New Developments (May 2-4)

#### "Project Freedom" Launched — US Warships Enter Gulf
**[OFFICIAL]** President Donald Trump announced the US will begin guiding ships through the Strait of Hormuz starting Monday. US Central Command said military support will include guided-missile destroyers, more than 100 aircraft and 15,000 service members, though a US official said the initiative is not an escort mission.

**[VERIFIED]** "U.S. forces are supporting Project Freedom and enforcing the naval blockade on Iranian ports," CENTCOM said. CENTCOM said in another X post Monday morning that U.S. Navy guided-missile destroyers are "currently operating in the Arabian Gulf after transiting the Strait of Hormuz in support of Project Freedom."

**[VERIFIED]** "American forces are actively assisting efforts to restore transit for commercial shipping. As a first step, 2 U.S.-flagged merchant vessels have successfully transited through the Strait of Hormuz and are safely headed on their journey," CENTCOM wrote.

**Assessment:** This is a significant escalation. The US has moved from blockade operations to actively challenging Iran's control of the strait. The deployment of destroyers into the Gulf represents the first major US naval presence inside the waterway since the war began.

---

#### Iran Claims Strike on US Frigate — CENTCOM Denies
**[DISPUTED]** Two missiles hit a US frigate in the Strait of Hormuz after it ignored warnings from Iran's navy to halt, state media quote the army as saying, with the US military saying no vessel was struck.

**[OFFICIAL]** U.S. Central Command on Monday denied claims from Iranian state media that a U.S. warship transiting the Strait of Hormuz was struck by two missiles and forced to retreat. "No U.S. Navy ships have been struck," CENTCOM, the U.S. military command covering the Middle East, said in a post from its official X account.

**[REPORTED]** A senior Iranian official told Reuters Iran had fired a warning shot and that it was unclear whether the warship had been damaged.

**Assessment:** The conflicting claims suggest a confrontation did occur, though the outcome is disputed. Iran has a track record of false claims about striking US ships. However, even a "warning shot" marks a direct military exchange during what is nominally a ceasefire.

---

#### Iran Attacks UAE — Fire at Fujairah Oil Facility
**[VERIFIED]** The United Arab Emirates says its forces are responding to an Iranian drone and missile attack, with authorities in Fujairah reporting a fire at an oil facility.

**[OFFICIAL]** The United Arab Emirates said a fire at a petroleum industrial site in Fujairah was caused by a drone attack launched from Iran, while air defense systems also responded to a missile threat and intercepted incoming projectiles. In a statement, the UAE Defense Ministry said four cruise missiles were detected, adding that three were intercepted over the country's territorial waters and a fourth fell into the sea.

**[OFFICIAL]** The UAE's Foreign Ministry accused Iran of a "terrorist attack" on a vessel affiliated with Abu Dhabi National Oil Company (Adnoc) as it transited through the Strait of Hormuz. Two drones targeted the ship but no injuries were reported, the ministry said. The ministry expressed its "utmost condemnation and denunciation" of the attack and called it a flagrant violation of UN Security Council Resolution 2817 on freedom of maritime navigation. "Targeting commercial navigation and using the Strait of Hormuz as a tool of pressure or economic blackmail constitutes acts of piracy by the Iranian Revolutionary Guard [Corps] and poses a direct threat to the stability of the region and its people and to global energy security."

**Assessment:** This is the first major Iranian strike on UAE territory since the ceasefire began April 7. An oil facility fire represents exactly the kind of Gulf infrastructure damage that has been a key risk factor. Iran is signaling it will respond forcefully to "Project Freedom."

---

#### Iran's Armed Forces Warn of Attacks on Any US Ships
**[OFFICIAL]** Maj. Gen. Ali Abdollahi, the commander of Iran's Khatam al-Anbiya Central Headquarters, said in a statement carried by state media on Monday that "any foreign armed force, especially the invading U.S. army, if they intend to approach and enter the Strait of Hormuz, will be subjected to attack." "We will maintain the security of the Strait of Hormuz with all our might and manage it powerfully," Abdollahi said.

**[OFFICIAL]** A senior Iranian official, Ebrahim Azizi, has warned that any US interference in the Strait of Hormuz would be considered a violation of the ceasefire. "Any American interference in the new maritime regime of the Strait of Hormuz will be considered a violation of the ceasefire," Azizi said on X. Azizi, the head of the Iranian parliament's National Security Commission, said: "The Strait of Hormuz and the Persian Gulf would not be managed by Trump's delusional posts!"

**Assessment:** Iran is framing Project Freedom as a ceasefire violation and explicitly threatening military action. The ceasefire is now hanging by a thread.

---

#### South Korean Ship Reported Attacked
**[REPORTED]** South Korea's foreign ministry said it was investigating the cause of an explosion and fire on a South Korean ship carrying 24 crew members in the Hormuz strait on Monday, adding it would "closely communicate with relevant countries". It was unclear whether the ship was targeted by Iranian missiles.

**Assessment:** A third-party vessel potentially struck during Project Freedom's first day raises serious questions about safety of the operation.

---

#### 14-Point Iranian Proposal Under Review
**[VERIFIED]** Iran has submitted a 14-point response to the U.S. proposal to end the conflict that began with U.S. and Israeli attacks on Iran on February 28, according to the semi-official Tasnim news agency and the state-owned media organization Press TV. Key points of the plan include a demand to resolve all issues and end the war within 30 days, instead of observing a two-month ceasefire as the U.S.

**[REPORTED]** According to two sources briefed of the proposal, it set a one-month deadline for negotiations on a deal to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, end the U.S. naval blockade and permanently end the war in Iran and in Lebanon. Per the Iranian proposal, only after such a deal is reached, another month of negotiations would be launched to try and reach a deal on the nuclear program, the two sources said.

**[VERIFIED]** Trump said his representatives "are having very positive discussions" with Iran. The Iranian Foreign Ministry said Iran is reviewing the US reply to Tehran's latest peace proposal.

**Assessment:** The 14-point proposal follows Iran's earlier 10-point plan, maintaining the sequencing approach (Hormuz first, nuclear later) that Trump has rejected. However, both sides claim talks are ongoing despite today's escalation.

---

#### Hezbollah Declares "No Ceasefire" — Rejects Talks
**[OFFICIAL]** "There is no ceasefire in Lebanon, but a continuous Israeli-American aggression," Hezbollah leader Naim Qassem said in a written statement broadcast by the Al-Manar TV channel, which is affiliated with the Iran-backed militant group.

**[OFFICIAL]** "There is no yellow line or buffer zone, and there will not be," declared Qassem on Monday, accusing Israel of trying to seize land by force. "With our faith and our exclusive choice of reclaiming and liberating our land and refusing surrender, we will certainly succeed in confronting it."

**[REPORTED]** The Hezbollah chief argued that Israel "has not implemented a single step of the agreement, violating it more than 10,000 times, killing 500 civilians, wounding hundreds, destroying thousands of homes and livelihoods, and displacing people from their villages."

**[VERIFIED]** Israel has launched multiple strikes across southern Lebanon, killing at least 10 people in further violations of the "ceasefire" declared two weeks ago. Lebanon's Ministry of Health said the latest wave of attacks on Saturday increased the total recorded over the previous 24 hours to 41. The overall death toll since March 2 is 2,659, with 8,183 injured.

**Assessment:** Hezbollah is publicly rejecting the Lebanon ceasefire framework entirely. Combined with continued Israeli strikes (41 killed in 24 hours on May 2), the Lebanon front is deteriorating rapidly.

#### Oil Prices and Economic Impacts
**[VERIFIED]** As of 8:45 a.m. Eastern Time today, oil sold for $115.01 per barrel (using Brent as the benchmark). That's $3.81 higher than yesterday morning and approximately a $55 rise over the past year.

**[VERIFIED]** Oil prices are moving higher on renewed concerns about the safety of transiting the Strait of Hormuz after Iran's navy claimed it had prevented two US ships from entering the waterway. Brent crude, the global oil benchmark, is up 3.8% to $112.3 a barrel. WTI, the US benchmark, is up a similar margin to $105.6 a barrel.

**[VERIFIED]** The average price of a gallon of US regular gas now stands at $4.45 a gallon, according to AAA data. Gas prices have increased by 35 cents from a week ago and $1.47 since the start of the war in Iran on February 28. This is a 49.3% increase since the start of the conflict.

**[REPORTED]** The statewide average for 87-octane gasoline in California has topped $6 a gallon as the Iran-war-driven global energy crunch ripples across the West Coast.

---

#### Stranded Vessels Crisis Deepens
**[VERIFIED]** The International Maritime Organization (IMO) estimates that up to 20,000 seafarers are stranded on board roughly 2,000 vessels in the Gulf near the Strait of Hormuz. These include oil and gas tankers, cargo ships, bulk carriers and cruise liners. Since the conflict began, the IMO has recorded at least 19 attacks on vessels, killing 10 seafarers and injuring eight. It has warned that many ships are running critically low on food, fuel and water.

**[VERIFIED]** "There is no precedent for the stranding of so many seafarers in the modern age," said Damien Chevallier, director of the IMO's Maritime Safety Division.

---

#### Touska Sailors Released — Confidence Building
**[REPORTED]** Mediator Pakistan meanwhile said that it had facilitated the transfer of 22 Iranian sailors from the "Touska" container ship seized by U.S. forces last month. It described the move as a "confidence-building measure." The sailors were flown to Pakistan on Sunday night, a statement from the country's foreign ministry said, and will be handed over to Iranian authorities.

**Assessment:** A rare positive diplomatic signal amid today's escalation.
