## Major Developments Since Last Report

### Mojtaba Khamenei Confirmed as New Supreme Leader

**[VERIFIED]** The succession question has been resolved. Mojtaba Khamenei was elected on 8 March 2026 to replace his father as supreme leader, and the IRGC, as well as Iran's top leaders (including Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf, Ali Larijani, and Masoud Pezeshkian) pledged their allegiance to him.

Trump's response was dismissive. Trump called the new supreme leader "lightweight", and threatened that he would not last long without his approval.

**[VERIFIED]** Casualties have mounted significantly:

- **Iran:** At least 1,444 people have been killed and 18,551 injured by US-Israeli attacks on Iran since February 28, Iran's Health Ministry says.

This includes six American service members who died on March 12 when their KC-135 refueling plane crashed over western Iraq. ### Lebanese Front Escalates into Full-Scale War

**[VERIFIED]** Hezbollah's entry into the conflict has opened a devastating second front. On 2 March 2026, during the ongoing war with Iran between Israel and the United States, Hezbollah, an Iranian-allied proxy in Lebanon, launched strikes on Israel in response to the killing of Iranian supreme leader Ali Khamenei.

The war is expanding into Lebanon, as an Israeli offensive to dismantle Hezbollah has displaced 800,000 people there, with more than 680 people killed.

Israel Katz authorized a ground invasion of Lebanon on 3 March. Since the beginning of the campaign, the IDF has carried out more than 1,100 strikes in Lebanon, during which approximately 110 Hezbollah headquarters were attacked. The IDF spokesperson stated that since the beginning of the fighting more than 350 militants have been eliminated in Lebanon, including about 15 senior commanders.

A significant political shift: The Lebanese government announced on 5 March it would "arrest and repatriate" anyone in Lebanon connected to Iran's Revolutionary Guards, leading to the departure of dozens of Iranian officers from Beirut.

### Ceasefire Prospects: Both Sides Dig In

**[OFFICIAL - Iran]** Foreign Minister Araghchi made Iran's position unambiguous today: "No, we never asked for a ceasefire, and we have never asked even for negotiation. We are ready to defend ourselves as long as it takes."

**[REPORTED]** Despite Trump's claims that Iran "wants to make a deal," President Donald Trump's administration has rebuffed efforts by Middle Eastern allies to start diplomatic negotiations aimed at ending the Iran war that started two weeks ago with a massive U.S.-Israeli air assault, according to three sources familiar with the efforts. Iran, for its part, has rejected the possibility of any ceasefire until U.S. and Israeli strikes end.

"The Guards strongly believe that if they lose control over the Strait of Hormuz, Iran will lose the war," a senior Iranian source said. "Therefore, the Guards will not accept any ceasefire, ceasefire talks, or diplomatic efforts, and Iran's political leaders will not engage in such talks despite attempts by several countries."

### Strait of Hormuz: A Tacit Arrangement Emerges

**[OFFICIAL - US]** Treasury Secretary Bessent revealed today that: the U.S. is allowing Iranian oil tankers to transit the Strait of Hormuz. "We've let that happen to supply the rest of the world," Bessent said.

On 5 March, the IRGC announced that Iran would keep the Strait of Hormuz closed only to ships from the US, Israel and their Western allies. This was confirmed again on 8 March. On 13 March, Turkish transport minister Abdulkadir Uraloğlu said that Iran approved the passage of a Turkish ship through the strait. It was also reported that two Indian-flagged gas carriers and a Saudi oil tanker with 1 million barrels for India were allowed to pass.

**[VERIFIED]** The economic impact has been severe. Oil prices have surged about 40% since the U.S. and Israel attacked Iran two weeks ago. The International Energy Agency on Wednesday announced that it would carry out its largest-ever release of oil reserves — 400 million barrels to stabilize markets.

US Energy Secretary Wright said the markets are experiencing a "short-term disruption" and acknowledged: "We're simply not ready. All of our military assets right now are focused on destroying Iran's offensive capabilities and the manufacturing industry that supplies their offensive capabilities."

### Iranian Military Degradation — But Capacity Remains

**[VERIFIED]** The rate of ballistic missile launches by Iran declined from the beginning of the war to 4 March, with analysts pointing to a depletion of Iranian missile and launcher stores as well as a strategy of rationing for a longer war as explanatory factors. On 5 March 2026, a military source told Fars News Agency that Iran had fired over 500 ballistic and naval missiles and almost 2,000 drones since 28 February.

By the tenth day of the war, Iranian missile and drone attacks had dropped by more than 90%, a reduction that was steeper than during the 2025 conflict and that was credited to effective suppression efforts by US and Israeli missions over Iran.

**[REPORTED]** Internal Iranian military strain is becoming visible: Iran's armed forces are facing acute supply shortages, rising desertions and deepening friction between the regular army (Artesh) and the Revolutionary Guards, according to informed sources. Among the most serious allegations are reports that wounded army personnel have been denied assistance by the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC), that some frontline units are operating with minimal ammunition, food and drinking water, and that attempts to mobilize reserve forces have faltered.

### Nuclear Dimension: Material Likely Intact

**[VERIFIED]** The nuclear stockpile remains a central concern. "What we believe is that Isfahan had until our last inspection a bit more than 200kg, maybe a little bit more than that, of 60 per cent uranium," IAEA chief Rafael Grossi said. Most of Iran's overall stock was kept at Isfahan and some held elsewhere may have been destroyed. "The widespread assumption is that the material is still there," ​Mr Grossi said.

In his CBS interview, Araghchi said Iran's stockpile of highly enriched uranium was now buried "under the rubble" following US and Israeli strikes.

**[REPORTED]** Trump is reportedly considering a special forces ground operation to breach the Esfahan tunnels and eliminate or seize the HEU stockpile, though he currently denies that such a decision is near.

### Congress: War Powers Effort Failed

**[VERIFIED]** The United States House of Representatives has narrowly rejected a war powers resolution that would have halted President Donald Trump's war on Iran and required congressional authorisation for any further attacks. The vote on Thursday was 219 to 212 in the House.

It is the second vote in as many days, after the Senate defeated a similar measure along party lines.

However, Sen. Tim Kaine, who co-sponsored the War Powers resolution, said the measure is "not a one and done," and that Democrats are considering other strategies including through the appropriations process. "But I can assure you it's not going to be the last."

### Protests Continue Inside Iran

**[REPORTED]** Despite the internet blackout and heavy security presence: After Mojtaba Khamenei was named the new Supreme Leader on 8 March, chants of "Death to Mojtaba" were heard from the windows of homes. On 10 March, Pahlavi urged Iranians to stay in their homes while awaiting for his "final call".

On 13 March, the IRGC threatened a crackdown bigger than the one in January. On 14 March, people chanted 'Long live the Shah' in the Chitgar neighborhood of Tehran. Nationwide strikes were conducted by businesses, stores, cafes, and workers as well as by online shops and social media influencers.
