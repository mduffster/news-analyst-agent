## What Changed in the Last 24 Hours

### Strait of Hormuz: Selective Blockade Formalized

**[OFFICIAL]** Iran's Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi stated the Strait of Hormuz "is open, but closed to our enemies, to those who carried out this cowardly aggression against us and to their allies." This represents the clearest articulation yet of Iran's selective blockade policy.

**[VERIFIED]** Araghchi said Tehran is open to holding talks with countries wanting to safely access the strait. However, he also said Iran was not seeking a ceasefire and "has no hesitation in defending itself and is prepared to continue the war for as long and as far as necessary."

**[OFFICIAL]** On March 16, an Australian government minister stated that Australia will not send ships to the straits. On the same day, Japan's Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi also ruled any current plans to send Japanese navy ships to help escort oil tankers through the strait.

**[REPORTED]** The Wall Street Journal reported on Sunday, citing unnamed US officials, that the Trump administration plans to announce as early as this week that multiple countries have agreed to form a coalition to escort ships through the Strait of Hormuz. Al Jazeera could not immediately verify the report.

**[OFFICIAL]** White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt reiterated Trump's call for China and NATO allies to join in helping reopen the Strait of Hormuz. Yet, so far, no countries have committed to sending warships to the area.

### Oil Market: Record Disruption, Prices Above $100

**[VERIFIED]** Brent crude oil futures hit a high of $106.50 per barrel around 3:45 a.m. (IST) on Monday, March 16, 2026. The current price of Brent Oil futures is $104.37, with today's trading range between $102.06 and $106.50.

**[OFFICIAL]** The International Energy Agency said Sunday: "The war in the Middle East is creating the largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market."

**[VERIFIED]** Tehran's near-blockade of the Strait of Hormuz means that some 15 million barrels of crude oil and 5 million barrels of oil products are cut off from the global market every day. In other words, 400 million barrels of crude from the IEA release would be absorbed in just 26 days.

**[OFFICIAL]** The IEA's March report states: "The war in the Middle East is creating the largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market. With crude and oil product flows through the Strait of Hormuz plunging from around 20 mb/d before the war to a trickle currently, limited capacity available to bypass the crucial waterway, and storage filling up, Gulf countries have cut total oil production by at least 10 mb/d."

### Kharg Island: Oil Infrastructure Still Spared — For Now

**[OFFICIAL]** US Central Command said Saturday the strikes on Kharg Island destroyed more than 90 military targets, including missile facilities, but preserved oil infrastructure.

**[VERIFIED]** The US continues to pummel Iranian targets across the country, with a particular focus on the shores of the Persian Gulf and Kharg Island. Trump announced Friday he had ordered strikes on military installations on the island, while sparing its oil facilities. On Saturday he told NBC the US "may hit it a few more times just for fun."

**[REPORTED]** Trump is drawn to the idea of seizing Kharg Island outright because it would constitute "an economic knockout of the regime" — essentially defunding Tehran. But the move would require boots on the ground and could trigger Iranian retaliatory strikes against oil facilities and pipelines across Gulf countries, particularly Saudi Arabia.

**[OFFICIAL]** "No one should read into anything more than what the president announced," a senior White House official said. "The president has made no decisions on Kharg Island." But, the official said, "that could change" if the effort to clear the strait drags on.

**[REPORTED]** Analysis suggests the March 13 strikes on Kharg Island "destroyed the runway, naval base, air defenses, and mine storage: exactly the targets you neutralize before an amphibious or airborne assault."

## Critical Dynamics

### Hormuz Coalition: More Rhetoric Than Reality

Despite Trump's claims of imminent coalition announcements, the concrete picture shows declining rather than growing international support:

- **Australia:** Explicitly ruled out naval participation
- **Japan:** Ruled out current plans for naval escort missions
- **EU:** Demanding clarity on US objectives and end-state before committing
- **China:** No commitment; summit timing now uncertain

Security analyst Andreas Krieg noted Trump's call for a coalition appeared to mask the absence of a broader plan: "It doesn't seem like they had a plan for the Strait of Hormuz to be closed, and it seems like a desperate move in an information campaign to calm markets." He said there was no quick military solution to reopening the strait, as all Iran needed to do was strike occasionally to keep insurers away.

### Kharg Island: The Decisive Variable

The pattern of Kharg strikes — destroying "the runway, naval base, air defenses, and mine storage: exactly the targets you neutralize before an amphibious or airborne assault" — suggests preparation for potential escalation even as the administration maintains ambiguity.

Analysts cautioned that attacks on Kharg Island's oil infrastructure would represent a major escalation. Tehran "would escalate by attacking more energy infrastructure in the region, for instance, Abqaiq in Saudi Arabia," according to Edward Fishman of the Council on Foreign Relations.

### Economic Spillover Intensifying

The IEA's characterization of this as "the largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market" is now translating into concrete state-level impacts:

- Global oil supply is projected to plunge by 8 mb/d in March
- IEA has reduced the forecast for global oil demand growth in 2026 by 210 kb/d
- Iraq's oil exports (previously ~3.4 mbpd via Basra) have stopped due to the Hormuz closure
- Plunging LPG and naphtha supplies are already forcing petrochemical plants to curb production. LPG use in cooking and heating, especially in India and East Africa, is also at risk.

### Lebanon: Second Front Escalating

Since 2 March 2026, there is an ongoing war in Lebanon between Israel and Hezbollah. The war has killed more than 820 people in Lebanon. The war has also displaced nearly 1 million people, or 14% of the country's entire population, creating a humanitarian crisis.

The announced Israeli ground invasion would represent a significant expansion of the conflict's scope and duration.
