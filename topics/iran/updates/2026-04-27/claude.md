### Executive Summary

**KEY DEVELOPMENTS: Iran proposes Hormuz-for-blockade swap, deferring nuclear talks; US-Iran talks collapse as Trump cancels Pakistan trip; Araghchi meets Putin in St. Petersburg; Deadliest day in Lebanon since ceasefire kills 14; IDF soldier killed by Hezbollah drone; Brent crude at $108; May 1 War Powers deadline 4 days away.**

The diplomatic situation deteriorated over the weekend despite a new Iranian proposal to decouple the Strait of Hormuz crisis from nuclear negotiations. Trump today meets with his national security team to discuss the offer, but administration signals suggest skepticism. Meanwhile, Lebanon's ceasefire is fraying rapidly.

### What Changed Since Yesterday

#### Iran Proposes Hormuz-for-Blockade Swap, Delays Nuclear Talks

**[REPORTED]** Iran has given the US a new proposal focusing on solving the crisis over the strait and the US blockade first. As part of this, the ceasefire would be extended for a long period or the parties would agree on a permanent end to the war. According to the proposal, the nuclear negotiations would only start at a later stage, after the strait was open and the blockade lifted.

**[VERIFIED]** One source said Araghchi made it clear to the Pakistani, Egyptian, Turkish and Qatari mediators over the weekend that there's no consensus inside the Iranian leadership about how to address the US demands.

**[OFFICIAL]** The White House has received the proposal, but it's unclear whether the US is willing to explore it. "These are sensitive diplomatic discussions and the US will not negotiate through the press. As the president has said, the United States holds the cards and will only make a deal that puts the American people first, never allowing Iran to have a nuclear weapon," White House spokesperson Olivia Wales told Axios.

**Assessment:** This proposal represents a significant shift in Iranian strategy — essentially offering to separate the immediate crisis from the core nuclear dispute. However, lifting the blockade and ending the war would remove President Trump's leverage in any future talks to remove Iran's stockpile of enriched uranium and convince Tehran to suspend enrichment — two primary war objectives for Trump. The proposal is unlikely to succeed in its current form.

#### Trump Cancels Pakistan Trip, Talks Collapse Over Weekend

**[OFFICIAL]** The US and Iran failed to kick off a second round of peace talks this weekend despite a last-minute push by Pakistan, delivering a blow to Islamabad's efforts to broker a settlement to end the two-month-old war. After days of anticipation, President Donald Trump on Saturday called off a planned trip by his special envoys Jared Kushner and Steve Witkoff to Islamabad for round two of peace negotiations with Iran, saying the Islamic Republic had "offered a lot, but not enough" to merit new discussions.

**[OFFICIAL]** Trump said Sunday that any possible Iran talks can take place over the phone. "If they want to talk, they can come to us, or they can call us. You know, there is a telephone," the president told Fox News, adding, "we're not sending people to travel 18 hours to meet."

**[REPORTED]** Trump told reporters: "They gave us a paper that should have been better and - interestingly - immediately when I cancelled it, within 10 minutes, we got a new paper that was much better."

**Assessment:** Trump's cancellation and dismissive tone suggests the administration is in no hurry to negotiate from a position it perceives as strong. The "18-hour travel" excuse rings hollow given the stakes.

#### Araghchi Meets Putin in St. Petersburg

**[VERIFIED]** Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi and Russian President Vladimir Putin met in Moscow today. The pair discussed Moscow's commitment to its strategic relationship with Tehran, Russian state news agency TASS and Iranian media reported.

**[OFFICIAL]** Putin told Araghchi: "For our part, we will do everything that serves your interests, the interests of all the people of the region, so that peace can be achieved as soon as possible." The president also hailed "how courageously and heroically the people of Iran are fighting for their independence and sovereignty," and declared: "Russia, just like Iran, intends to continue our strategic relationship."

**[REPORTED]** Putin said he had received a message from Iranian Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei, according to TASS. Khamenei has not been seen or heard from in public since becoming Iran's leader, and only written messages have been released in his name.

**[VERIFIED]** Ahead of the meeting, Araghchi told reporters that the US, the world's greatest "superpower", has achieved none of its war goals, and this is why it is requesting negotiations.

**[VERIFIED]** Kremlin spokesman Dmitry Peskov told reporters that it would be "difficult to overestimate" the importance of the visit "in light of the evolving situation around Iran and in the Middle East."

**Assessment:** Russia is positioning itself as Iran's primary international backer while offering to mediate. Russia has offered to mediate to try to help restore calm to the Middle East following US and Israeli strikes on Iran, which Moscow has condemned. It has also repeatedly offered to store Iran's enriched uranium as a way of defusing tensions, an offer the United States has not taken up.

#### Lebanon: Deadliest Day Since Ceasefire — 14 Killed, IDF Soldier Dies in Hezbollah Drone Attack

**[VERIFIED]** Lebanon's health ministry said Israeli strikes on the country's south on Sunday killed 14 people, the deadliest day since a ceasefire in the Israel-Hezbollah war came into force over a week ago.

**[VERIFIED]** Lebanon's health ministry said the dead on Sunday included two women and two children, adding that 37 other people were wounded. Israeli strikes have killed at least 36 people since the truce began, according to an AFP tally of health ministry figures.

**[VERIFIED]** An Israeli soldier was killed and six others were wounded in a Hezbollah explosive drone strike in southern Lebanon on Sunday, as cross-border violence continued throughout the day, despite a fragile ceasefire that was extended last week.

**[VERIFIED]** Sgt. Idan Fooks is the third soldier to be killed in southern Lebanon during the ceasefire, and the first in a direct Hezbollah attack. The other two troops were killed by bombs previously planted by the terror group.

**[OFFICIAL]** Israel has issued new forced evacuation notices for areas in southern Lebanon, ordering residents of seven towns that lie beyond its so-called "buffer zone" to leave, ramping up the conflict with Hezbollah despite a US-brokered ceasefire.

**[VERIFIED]** Hezbollah said it attacked Israeli troops inside Lebanon as well as the rescue force that came to evacuate them, targeting a newly established Israeli artillery position in the town of Biyyada with a swarm of drones.

**Assessment:** The "ceasefire" in Lebanon exists in name only. Both sides are escalating daily, with Israel issuing evacuation orders beyond its declared security zone and Hezbollah killing Israeli soldiers. Netanyahu told his weekly cabinet meeting: "Hezbollah's violations are, in practice, dismantling the ceasefire."

#### Oil Spikes to $108 as Talks Collapse

**[VERIFIED]** Brent rose to 108.11 USD/Bbl on April 27, 2026, up 2.64% from the previous day. Over the past month, Brent's price has risen 0.67%, and is up 66.86% compared to the same time last year.

**[VERIFIED]** The Iran conflict has now entered its ninth week, triggering what the IEA has described as the largest energy supply shock on record, while intensifying inflationary pressures and weighing on the global growth outlook.

**[REPORTED]** Goldman Sachs now expects oil prices to stay higher for longer, raising its Brent forecast to $90 a barrel by late 2026 from $80 previously, as disruptions in the Persian Gulf prove more persistent than earlier assumed. The bank wrote in a note published Monday that delayed normalization in Gulf exports, now expected only by end-June, alongside a slower production recovery is tightening supply sharply.

**[REPORTED]** On April 26, Citigroup raised its 2026 Brent crude oil price forecast to $150. Citing the deadlock in US-Iran negotiations and the resulting persistent blockade of the Strait of Hormuz, Citi believes the situation in the Middle East has shifted from a short-term risk to a structural variable that could continuously disrupt global supply.

**[VERIFIED]** US gas prices edged up another penny to $4.11 for a gallon of regular, according to the latest reading from AAA. The rise marked the fifth straight day of price increases, following a two-week stretch of falling prices earlier this month that had taken prices down to $4.02. But prices are still below the recent peak of $4.17 that had been reached on April 9.

#### State Department Quietly Published Legal Defense of War

**[VERIFIED]** State Department Legal Adviser Reed Rubinstein argued the US campaign against Iran was lawful self-defense and part of a conflict that had already been underway for years, not the start of a new war. The statement, titled "Operation Epic Fury and International Law," was quietly posted on the State Department's website on Tuesday but was not pushed through the department's usual media channels. In the memo, Rubinstein said the United States was acting both in its own self-defense and in the collective self-defense of Israel, citing what he called decades of Iranian aggression, proxy attacks, missile strikes, and Tehran's nuclear ambitions.

**[VERIFIED]** The State Department document states: "Epic Fury is only the latest round of an ongoing international armed conflict with Iran. As the United States has explained in multiple letters to the UN Security Council, including most recently on March 10, the United States is engaged in this conflict at the request of and in the collective self-defense of its Israeli ally, as well as in the exercise of the United States' own inherent right of self-defense."

**Assessment:** This is a genuinely fresh development — the administration is now trying to shape the legal and political terrain for what comes next. If diplomacy stalls again, this document could become one of the key arguments Washington uses to defend a longer campaign against Tehran.

#### May 1 War Powers Deadline Now 4 Days Away

**[VERIFIED]** A post-Vietnam law puts a 60-day clock on the use of military force without congressional authorization. The war in Iran — for which the Trump administration sought no approval — hits that 60-day mark May 1, according to the text of the law, the War Powers Resolution, but it's not at all clear what will happen next.

**[VERIFIED]** Some believe the 60-day clock started from the date hostilities began (which would make the deadline April 29), while others cite the text of the law to argue it's 60 calendar days from the date the White House officially notified Congress (which would put the deadline at May 1). But many Republican lawmakers believe that the ceasefire period does not count toward the 60-day deadline.

**[OFFICIAL]** Vice President JD Vance said in January, before the Iran war, that the War Powers Resolution would not affect how Trump leads the country. "The War Powers Act is fundamentally a fake and unconstitutional law," Vance said. Despite multiple administrations holding that view, the law has never been used to end a military action and courts have shied away from getting involved.

**[OFFICIAL]** Some Republicans, such as Sen. Susan Collins, have indicated that they would join with Democrats to vote in favor of resolutions ordering an end to the fighting. But it would take just a few Republicans in both the House and the Senate crossing the aisle for resolutions ordering an end to the war to pass and be sent to Trump's desk. Collins, who leads the powerful Senate Appropriations Committee, said: "I've been very clear from the beginning that I consider the 60-day trigger in the War Powers Act to be [severe] and that the president needs to request authorization from Congress to continue hostilities beyond that point."

**[REPORTED]** While the deadline comes on May 1, the president may unilaterally certify in writing to Congress that the continued use of force is necessary to ensure the orderly withdrawal of US forces from the region — adding 30 days to the president's power to wage Operation Epic Fury.

### Key Emerging Issues

1. **Trump Situation Room meeting today** — President Donald Trump plans to discuss options for the war going forward, including whether the US will resume its bombing campaign, with top national security officials today. This is the critical near-term decision point.

2. **State Department legal memo** — Administration laying groundwork to continue war past May 1 without congressional authorization. The "ongoing armed conflict" framing is legally contested but politically useful.

3. **Iran's internal divisions becoming public** — Araghchi explicitly told mediators there's "no consensus" in Tehran on how to address US demands. This could either enable a deal (moderates win) or prevent one (hardliners block).

4. **Lebanon ceasefire effectively dead** — Despite three-week "extension," yesterday was deadliest day since truce began. Israel expanding operations beyond declared buffer zone. This front may collapse first.

5. **Citi's $150 oil forecast** — Signals major institutional view that Hormuz crisis is structural, not temporary. Political pressure on Trump will intensify as prices rise further.

6. **Russia-Iran coordination deepening** — Putin meeting and offer to store enriched uranium positions Moscow as key player. Could complicate any US deal or provide Iran with offramp.
