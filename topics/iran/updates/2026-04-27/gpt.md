### 1) Pakistan-mediated track: Iran has now **put a concrete “interim” proposal on the table** focused on Hormuz/blockade first, nuclear later
- Iran has conveyed (via Pakistan) a proposal to **end its “chokehold” on the Strait of Hormuz** in exchange for the U.S. **lifting its naval blockade** and **ending the war / moving to a long-term truce**, while **postponing nuclear negotiations to a later stage**. **[REPORTED]** (AP attributes to two regional officials speaking anonymously) ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/374d81d1aac6d8f19c21e1d1e10ab103?utm_source=openai))  
- Axios separately reports the same basic structure (Hormuz reopening + end-war framework now; nuclear later), citing a U.S. official and other sources. **[REPORTED]** ([axios.com](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/27/iran-us-hormuz-strait-nuclear-talks-proposal-pakistan?utm_source=openai))  

**What it means:** This is the first *specific* sequencing proposal I’ve seen in days: it tests whether Washington values **near-term maritime/economic stabilization** enough to trade away its **blockade leverage** before locking in nuclear constraints. It also suggests Tehran is trying to **decouple** the “shipping war” from the nuclear file—something the U.S. has historically resisted when it believes pressure is working. Net: this modestly improves the odds of a **process breakthrough** (even if only on a partial maritime arrangement), but also clarifies the **core bargaining gap**: U.S. leverage now vs. nuclear concessions now. **[ANALYSIS]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/374d81d1aac6d8f19c21e1d1e10ab103?utm_source=openai))
