## Daily incremental update — US–Israel–Iran conflict  
**As of Fri, April 10, 2026 (ET)**  
**Window scanned:** new reporting/statements **since Thu, April 9, 2026 (ET)**

### What changed since the last report

1) **Hormuz is still functionally “not reopened” at scale; the toll dispute is now explicit and central.**  
- Axios reports Trump is **demanding Iran stop “tolls”** and cites reporting that Iran is seeking a **$1/barrel toll (paid in cryptocurrency)**. **[REPORTED]** ([axios.com](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/09/iran-us-strait-of-hormuz-khamenei?utm_source=openai))  
- USNI reports only a **small number of transits** immediately after the ceasefire announcement (single digits per day) and frames the strait as still effectively as open/closed as before in practical terms; it also highlights that this is the **first time Iran has openly said** it placed sea mines (per a risk analyst cited by USNI). **[REPORTED]** ([news.usni.org](https://news.usni.org/2026/04/09/vessels-trickle-past-iran-as-questions-remain-over-strait-of-hormuz-status?utm_source=openai))  
**What it means:** The ceasefire’s key deliverable (“COMPLETE, IMMEDIATE, and SAFE OPENING”) is drifting from *political compliance* into a **commercial/operational reality test**: if traffic remains a trickle and toll/mines remain contested, Washington can argue Iran isn’t complying—even without a dramatic kinetic incident.

2) **Israel’s Lebanon track just shifted: Netanyahu authorized direct talks with Lebanon—while reiterating continued strikes—creating a narrower off-ramp but not a de-escalation yet.**  
- AP reports Netanyahu authorized **direct negotiations with Lebanon** aimed at disarming Hezbollah and establishing relations, while also stating Israel will **keep striking Hezbollah** until northern security is restored. **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/7760f88f183ed2a13a721057e31f3ce7?utm_source=openai))  
- Axios frames this as coming **after U.S. pressure** and notes Iran is warning it might abandon peace talks or keep Hormuz closed if Lebanon is treated as outside the ceasefire. **[REPORTED]** ([axios.com](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/09/israel-lebanon-negotiations-ceasefire?utm_source=openai))  
**What it means:** This is the first concrete procedural “escape hatch” for the biggest ceasefire spoiler (Lebanon). But because Israel is simultaneously signaling **continued operations**, Iran/Hezbollah can still treat strikes as ceasefire violations and retaliate—so the talks may reduce escalation risk only if paired with **near-term restraint or a sequencing deal** (e.g., pause-for-talks).

3) **The Islamabad talks look more real (named attendees) and may be working off a “modified” Iranian proposal—suggesting bargaining space is opening, but the scope dispute (Lebanon) remains the tripwire.**  
- Le Monde and Al Jazeera both describe delegations arriving/being prepared, with the U.S. side led by **VP JD Vance** and joined by **Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner**. **[REPORTED]** ([lemonde.fr](https://www.lemonde.fr/en/international/article/2026/04/10/how-pakistan-played-a-pivotal-role-in-brokering-the-iran-us-ceasefire_6752276_4.html?utm_source=openai))  
- Critical Threats (ISW) reports U.S. officials described a **“new/modified”** Iranian proposal as the basis for talks and cites reporting that Iran has **softened** some demands (while underscoring disagreement over whether **Lebanon** is included). **[REPORTED]** ([criticalthreats.org](https://www.criticalthreats.org/analysis/iran-update-evening-special-report-april-9-2026?utm_source=openai))  
**What it means:** Compared with yesterday, the diplomacy channel looks slightly more capable of producing **implementable terms** (verification/traffic rules/sanctions sequencing). However, the negotiations are now more exposed to **single-incident spoilers** (a major Lebanon strike/Hezbollah salvo, or a mine/tanker incident) because each side is already building a narrative of “they violated first.”

4) **European/UN messaging is rising, but still “supporting cast”—focused on Lebanon de-escalation and reopening Hormuz.**  
- AP reports European leaders urging negotiation to shore up the shaky ceasefire, quell Lebanon fighting, and reopen Hormuz. **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/5c49b81c89bc694207db8e07ceba70ea?utm_source=openai))  
- UN messaging similarly frames the ceasefire as a hope for reopening Hormuz (no new enforcement mechanism indicated). **[OFFICIAL]** ([un.org](https://www.un.org/en/iran-ceasefire-raises-hopes-reopening-key-strait-hormuz?utm_source=openai))  
**What it means:** Increased diplomatic chorus may help create “face-saving” space for compromises (e.g., tolls rebranded as fees/escrow/inspection charges), but none of this yet substitutes for **maritime security guarantees** or a **Lebanon track freeze**.

### Bottom line (incremental)
- Since yesterday, the biggest shift is that **Hormuz remains commercially constrained and politically disputed (tolls + mines + low traffic)**, while **Lebanon gets a potential negotiating channel** that could lower the chance of immediate blow-up—*if* it produces restraint quickly. **[REPORTED]** ([axios.com](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/09/iran-us-strait-of-hormuz-khamenei?utm_source=openai))  
- Net effect today: **slightly better diplomatic structure**, but **compliance arguments harden** around Hormuz mechanics.
