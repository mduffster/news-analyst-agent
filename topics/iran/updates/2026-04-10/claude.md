### Key Developments Since April 9

#### Iran's Pre-Condition for Islamabad Talks

**[OFFICIAL]** According to PressTV, Iran has threatened to cancel the talks if Israel continues to strike Beirut and its southern Dahiyeh suburb, which is considered a Hezbollah stronghold. "The Americans were forced to make the Zionist regime halt its assaults on Beirut," the Iranian official said.

**[REPORTED]** US Vice President JD Vance has warned Iran not to "play" the United States as he departed for Islamabad for negotiations. Speaking to reporters before his trip to Pakistan, Vance said President Donald Trump had set "pretty clear guidelines" for how the talks should be conducted. He added that he was "looking forward" to the negotiations and expressed optimism that discussions with Tehran would be "positive".

**Assessment:** This is a critical escalation of Iranian demands. The talks may begin Saturday, but Iran is conditioning their continuation on Israeli restraint in Lebanon—something Washington has explicitly refused to commit to. The talks could collapse within hours of starting.

#### Lebanon: Israel and Hezbollah Continue Exchanging Fire

**[VERIFIED]** Al Jazeera's team on the ground reported that Israeli forces blew up homes in the town of Hanine in southern Lebanon. The Lebanese group Hezbollah responded by firing rockets towards settlements in northern Israel, including Kiryat Shmona, Metula and Misgav Am. In a statement posted on Telegram, fighters said "these attacks would continue until the Israeli-American aggression against the country and the people stopped."

**[VERIFIED]** Hezbollah also said it targeted Israel's Ashdod naval base with missiles, two days after deadly Israeli air attacks on Beirut left more than 300 people dead. The attack was in response to Israel's "violation of the ceasefire and its repeated attacks on Beirut."

**[VERIFIED]** Lebanon's Health Ministry said at least 303 people were killed and more than 1,000 wounded on Wednesday in Israeli strikes in central Beirut and other areas of Lebanon. But Israel continued its bombardment overnight and into Thursday, saying it killed Ali Yusuf Harshi, an aide to Hezbollah leader Naim Qassem.

**[VERIFIED]** Since the ongoing Israel-Lebanon conflict began on March 2, Israel has issued evacuation orders for about 15 percent of Lebanese territory, displacing more than 1.2 million people, according to the United Nations. Israeli attacks have killed at least 1,888 people and wounded more than 6,000 others, according to Lebanese health authorities.

**[OFFICIAL]** A senior Lebanese official told Reuters that Lebanon intends to join a meeting next week in Washington with US and Israeli representatives to discuss and announce a ceasefire. Beirut considers it a precondition for further talks to reach a broader deal with Israel. But a senior Lebanese source told Anadolu that the planned talks in Washington will be preliminary: "The meeting at the US State Department next week is preparatory, not a negotiation."

**[OFFICIAL]** Lebanon's cabinet instructed security forces to restrict weapons in Beirut exclusively to state institutions. "The army and security forces are requested to immediately begin reinforcing the full imposition of state authority over Beirut Governorate and to monopolize weapons in the hands of legitimate authorities alone," Prime Minister Nawaf Salam said at the end of a cabinet meeting.

**[OFFICIAL]** Hezbollah lawmaker Ali Fayyad stated that the group rejects direct negotiations with Israel and said that the Lebanese government should demand a ceasefire as a precondition before further steps.

**Assessment:** Two parallel diplomatic tracks are now emerging—US-Iran in Islamabad and Israel-Lebanon in Washington. But they're deeply interlinked. Iran is conditioning its participation on Lebanon, while Netanyahu insists on negotiations "under fire." Hezbollah rejects direct talks. The fragility is extreme.

#### Strait of Hormuz: Still Blocked Despite Ceasefire

**[VERIFIED]** Shipping remains at a standstill in the Strait of Hormuz despite the ceasefire agreement between the United States and Iran, dampening hopes for a resolution to one of the worst global energy disruptions in history.

**[VERIFIED]** More than 600 vessels, including 325 tankers, are still stranded in the Gulf due to the blockage of the strait. "While some vessel movement has resumed, traffic remains very limited, compliant shipowners are likely to stay cautious, and safe transit capacity is expected to remain constrained at maximum 10–15 passages a day if the ceasefire holds."

**[OFFICIAL]** "So let's be clear: the Strait of Hormuz is not open," Sultan Ahmed Al Jaber, the CEO of ADNOC, said in a social media post on Thursday. "Access is being restricted, conditioned and controlled. Iran has made clear – through both its statements and actions – that passage is subject to permission, conditions and political leverage. That is not freedom of navigation. That is coercion."

**[VERIFIED]** The Iranian navy released a map late Wednesday indicating it may have mined the strait and outlining the designated shipping lanes vessels should use to transit safely. It directs outbound ships leaving the Persian Gulf along a route just south of Larak Island, while inbound vessels must follow a route north of the island — both closer to Iran's mainland than the route often taken before the war.

**[OFFICIAL]** US President Donald Trump said on Thursday that Iran was doing a "very poor job" of allowing oil to go through the Strait of Hormuz. "That is not the agreement we have!" Trump said in a post on Truth Social.

**Assessment:** The Hormuz situation is deteriorating, not improving. Iran is maintaining de facto control through mine warnings, approval requirements, and limited transits. At 5-7 ships per day vs. 100+ pre-war, this is a 95% reduction. Trump's frustration is growing.

#### Saudi Arabia Energy Infrastructure Damaged

**[VERIFIED]** Adding to supply concerns, Saudi Arabia said attacks on its oil facilities have reduced production capacity by around 600,000 barrels per day and cut throughput on the East-West Pipeline by approximately 700,000 bpd.

**[REPORTED]** The Saudi Press Agency reported that throughput along the East-West Pipeline has dropped by about 700,000 bpd. The pipeline, which Reuters reported was attacked by Iran hours after a ceasefire to pause the war with the US and Israel was agreed, is currently the only Saudi export route available for crude oil.

**Assessment:** This is a significant development. The East-West Pipeline was Saudi Arabia's main bypass route around Hormuz. Its damage means even non-Hormuz exports are now compromised, tightening global supply further.

#### Oil Markets

**[VERIFIED]** Brent rose to $95.95 USD/Bbl on April 10, 2026, up 0.03% from the previous day.

**[VERIFIED]** West Texas Intermediate crude futures for May delivery gained more than 3% to close at $97.87 per barrel on Thursday. International benchmark Brent crude futures for June delivery rose more than 1% to settle at $95.92.

**[VERIFIED]** On the week, the US oil benchmark is down about 12%.

**Assessment:** Prices have stabilized after the ceasefire announcement but remain near $100. The market is pricing in significant uncertainty over whether Hormuz will actually reopen.

#### Humanitarian Crisis Deepening

**[VERIFIED]** "The latest bloodshed adds to a staggering 600 children either killed or injured in Lebanon since 2 March," UNICEF said.

**[VERIFIED]** Human Rights Watch said Israeli attacks have crippled important infrastructure in the south, including bridges over the Litani River. "Between March 12 and April 8, Israeli forces systematically destroyed or severely damaged all main bridges connecting areas south of the Litani River to the rest of the country," the group said.

**[VERIFIED]** "What we're witnessing is not just a displacement crisis, it is rapidly becoming a food security crisis," said WFP's Lebanon country director, speaking from Beirut on Friday.
