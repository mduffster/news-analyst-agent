## Daily incremental update — US–Israel–Iran conflict  
**As of Fri, April 3, 2026 (ET)**  
**Window scanned:** new reporting/official statements **since Thu, April 2, 2026 (ET)**

### What changed since the last report (new since Apr 2)

1) **Hormuz reopening effort moved into the UN Security Council — but got “defanged” into a defensive-only authorization**
- Bahrain **watered down** its draft UNSC resolution after **China and Russia opposed language** that could be read as authorizing force (“all necessary means”). The revised text reportedly authorizes states to use **“all defensive means necessary and commensurate”** in the **Strait of Hormuz and adjacent waters** for **at least six months**, including via multinational naval partnerships (with advance notification to the UNSC). **[VERIFIED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/f2a2fafe3e1691b9f0be5e7d691a90d0))  
- This is a meaningful procedural shift: it signals the GCC/partners are trying to create **legal-political cover for escorting/defensive operations**, while avoiding language that could be construed as a mandate for **offensive** action inside Iranian waters/territory. **[VERIFIED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/f2a2fafe3e1691b9f0be5e7d691a90d0))  

**What it means:** The “day-after Hormuz” track is becoming **institutionalized** (UK-led coalition talk → UNSC action), but the **narrow defensive framing** likely **reduces escalation risk** while also limiting its ability to “solve” the closure quickly. Expect more emphasis on **convoys, escorts, mines/counter-mine defense, and incident-response** rather than a forcible opening.

2) **Potential step-change escalation risk: Iranian media claims a U.S. pilot ejected over southwest Iran**
- An affiliate of Iranian state TV claimed a **U.S. fighter pilot ejected** over southwestern Iran; U.S. officials **did not immediately confirm** in AP reporting. **[UNCONFIRMED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/a6365c6123cc8a696474f576d4ce7668))  

**What it means:** If confirmed, this is a **major escalation hinge** because downed-aircrew recovery/capture scenarios can rapidly expand rules of engagement and trigger high-tempo rescue/strike cycles. For now, treat as **unverified** until there’s DoD/CENTCOM confirmation.

3) **Iran widened civilian-impact pressure on Gulf states: refinery fire + desalination damage; shrapnel injuries in UAE**
- AP reporting describes Iranian fire across the region that **set Kuwait’s Mina al-Ahmadi refinery on fire** and caused **material damage to a desalination plant**; separate AP “Latest” notes **shrapnel injuries in the UAE**. **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/a6365c6123cc8a696474f576d4ce7668?utm_source=openai))  

**What it means:** This reinforces a worrying trajectory: Iran (and/or its aligned fires) are increasingly stressing **critical civilian infrastructure** (water, refining). That increases pressure on Gulf governments to seek stronger **defensive coalitions**, and it raises the risk of **retaliatory targeting** and broader economic disruption.

---

4) **Israel issued a notable claim of targeting Iran’s oil-enabling command structure**
- The IDF posted that it eliminated **Jamshid Eshaqi**, described as **commander of the “Oil Headquarters”** enabling armed forces activity via oil revenue; the IDF also described **20+ strikes** in Tehran over 24 hours and additional missile launch detections (including from Yemen). **[OFFICIAL]** ([idf.il](https://www.idf.il/en/mini-sites/iran-israel-war-2026/iran-israel-war-2026-live-updates-1/april-2-2026-iran-israel-war-2026-live-updates/))  

**What it means:** This suggests Israel is explicitly prioritizing **war-sustaining economic nodes** (oil logistics/command) rather than only launchers/air defense—potentially complementing (or substituting for) U.S. rhetorical threats about energy infrastructure. It also signals **continued high strike tempo** rather than tapering.

---

### Bottom line (incremental)
Today’s delta is **more institutional diplomacy around Hormuz (UN track)** but **worsening kinetic/civilian-impact signals** (Kuwait refinery/desalination damage; UAE injuries) and a **high-stakes but unconfirmed** claim of a **downed U.S. pilot**. Net effect: near-term still looks like **sustained air/sea conflict with expanding regional blowback**, while diplomatic efforts focus on **defensive navigation** rather than a quick reopening.
