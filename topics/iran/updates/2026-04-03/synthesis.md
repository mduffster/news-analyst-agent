## Executive Summary

1. **[REPORTED] US F-15E down over Iran — crew status unknown, search underway.** A channel affiliated with Iranian state television claimed Friday that a U.S. fighter pilot ejected from their aircraft over southwestern Iran. It was not clear what may have happened to the plane, including whether Iran was claiming it was shot down or there was another issue. If the claim is confirmed, it could lead to yet another dramatic escalation in the war, nearing the end of its fifth week. Photos appear genuine and are certainly consistent with parts of an F-15E, including one of the vertical tails, and parts of the wing and tail.

Hegseth told US Army Chief of Staff Gen. Randy George to retire immediately. Hegseth's move comes a day after President Donald Trump's address to the nation on the Iran war. In the speech, Trump signaled the US will intensify strikes on Iran, after earlier suggesting the US could be done with the war within two to three weeks.

3. **[VERIFIED] Iran strikes Kuwait critical infrastructure again.** Iran's missiles set alight a refinery and damaged a desalination plant in Kuwait on Friday as Israeli and U.S. strikes kept hitting Iran. This was the third time the refinery has been hit, and Kuwait and much of the Gulf are highly dependent on desalinated water. An Indian national was killed on March 30 after a Kuwaiti power and desalination plant was hit.

4. **[VERIFIED] Spot oil prices hit 2008 highs at $141.** While futures settled around $109, the spot price for physical oil deliveries has surged dramatically. The crude price continues its sharp upward trajectory as of April 3, 2026, with both major benchmarks trading in the $105–$115 per barrel range. Brent crude is hovering near $109–$111, while WTI has surged to similar levels. This marks one of the most significant short-term rallies in recent years, with WTI jumping over 11% in a single session and Brent gaining nearly 8%.

5. **[VERIFIED] UN Security Council Hormuz vote delayed — China opposes force authorization.** The UN Security Council has postponed a vote scheduled for Friday on authorizing the use of "defensive" force to protect shipping in the Strait of Hormuz from Iranian attacks. The 15-member body was set to vote Friday morning on a draft resolution brought by Bahrain, but by Thursday night the schedule shifted. The reason given was that the United Nations observes Good Friday as a public holiday. No new date has been given for voting on the draft.

6. **[REPORTED] Former FM Zarif publishes peace proposal in Foreign Affairs.** Mohammad Javad Zarif, who served as foreign minister from 2013-2021, claimed in an op-ed for American journal Foreign Affairs that Tehran had the "upper hand" in the conflict against the US and Israel, but argued Iran needed to stop the war to prevent the loss of more civilian lives. "Iran should use its upper hand not to keep fighting but to declare victory and make a deal that both ends this conflict and prevents the next one," Zarif said. "It should offer to place limits on its nuclear program and to reopen the Strait of Hormuz in exchange for an end to all sanctions."

7. **[VERIFIED] UK air defense deployment to Kuwait discussed.** On Friday, Crown Prince Sheikh Sabah Khaled and UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer discussed the deployment of a British ground-based air defence system in Kuwait.

## What Changed Since April 2 (Day 34)

### US Aircraft Downed — Potential Hostage Crisis

**[REPORTED]** A local channel of Iranian state television said Friday that a U.S. fighter pilot ejected from their aircraft over southwestern Iran. The U.S. did not respond immediately to requests for comment. The anchor on the channel read the following announcement: "If you capture the enemy pilot or pilots alive and hand them over to the police, you will receive a precious prize." An on-screen crawl separately urged the public to "shoot them if you see them."

**[REPORTED]** Investigations by Tasnim reporters in the provinces show that the Americans are using several Black Hawk helicopters, a Hercules 130, and reconnaissance drones to search for the fighter pilot. Axios also confirmed that Iran has shot down a U.S. fighter jet, per Iranian media and a source familiar with the incident, and a search and rescue effort is underway to locate the two crew.

**[REPORTED]** The photos appear genuine and are certainly consistent with parts of an F-15E, including one of the vertical tails, and parts of the wing and tail. Specifically, the tail seen here would indicate an aircraft assigned to the 494th Fighter Squadron at RAF Lakenheath in England, based on its red band.

**[CLAIMED]** Iranian state media claims this was an F-35 shot down by a new IRGC air defense system, but debris analysis contradicts this.

**Assessment:** This is the most significant US combat loss of the war. The F-15E Strike Eagle is a two-seat aircraft, meaning up to two crew members may be on the ground in Iranian territory. A capture scenario would introduce hostage crisis dynamics and could dramatically escalate the conflict. The ongoing search and rescue operation — with helicopters operating deep inside Iranian territory — itself carries significant risk.

### Pentagon Leadership Upheaval

**[VERIFIED]** Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth has asked Army Chief of Staff Gen. Randy George and two other military leaders to leave their posts, two Defense officials confirmed to Axios. George is the Army's most senior general and is being removed during the Iran war. Gen. David Hodne and Maj. Gen. William Green Jr. have been removed from their positions.

**[VERIFIED]** George found out in a phone call from Hegseth on Thursday while he was in a meeting. He later spoke to his staff in person about the announcement, and his staff was "very stoic" when receiving the news. As the Army chief, George has worked closely with Army Secretary Dan Driscoll — a senior official close to the White House whom Hegseth has perceived as a threat. The abrupt and public nature of George's immediate retirement left little room for officials to argue against removing one of the joint chiefs amid ongoing conflict with Iran.

**[REPORTED]** The New York Times said the removal was related to clashes between George and Hegseth over the latter's decision to single out and block the promotion of four army officers on a list of 29 personnel. Most of the officers on the list are white men, while two blocked by Hegseth are Black, and the other two are women. Hegseth's decision led senior military officers to question whether racial or gender bias was at play.

**[REPORTED]** Democratic Senator Chris Murphy, who is a member of the Senate Foreign Relations Committee, offered his view: "It's likely that experienced generals are telling Hegseth his Iran war plans are unworkable, disastrous, and deadly."

**[VERIFIED]** Over the course of the year, the ax fell first on Coast Guard Commandant Adm. Linda Fagan, chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff Gen. CQ Brown, Chief of Naval Operations Adm. Lisa Franchetti, Air Force Vice Chief of Staff Gen. James Slife, Defense Intelligence Agency head Lieutenant General Jeffrey Kruse, National Security Agency and Cyber Command head Gen. Timothy Haugh and others.

**Assessment:** Firing the Army's top general during active wartime is extraordinary and nearly unprecedented. With the Army responsible for deploying air defense systems and with the 82nd Airborne potentially preparing for ground operations, the timing raises serious questions about command stability and internal dissent over war strategy.

### Iran Continues Infrastructure Attacks on Gulf States

**[VERIFIED]** The attack came hours after Kuwait's Al-Ahmadi oil refinery was also targeted in early morning drone strikes. State news agency KUNA said the attack caused fires in a "number of operational units," and no employees were injured. Emergency and firefighting teams were sent. This was the third time the refinery has been hit and that people across the country are on "high alert".

**[VERIFIED]** Kuwait says an Iranian attack hit a power and water desalination plant earlier today, causing material damage to parts of the facility. The Ministry of Electricity, Water and Renewable Energy says that emergency and technical teams are responding under contingency plans to maintain operations and secure the site.

**[VERIFIED]** Emergency teams contained the impact swiftly, with nationwide electricity and water operations remaining stable and secure. Desalination infrastructure remains a critical vulnerability, as Kuwait relies on seawater for about 90 per cent of its drinking supply.

**[CLAIMED]** Israel's "unconventional and illegitimate attack on Kuwait's water desalination centres is a sign of the vileness and baseness of the Zionist occupiers," the IRGC said in a statement, blaming Israel rather than accepting responsibility.

**Assessment:** Iran is systematically targeting Gulf states' critical infrastructure — power, water, and oil refining. The targeting of desalination facilities is particularly threatening given the region's near-total reliance on desalinated water. This pressure campaign appears designed to fracture the coalition supporting US operations.

### UN Security Council Resolution Faces Obstacles

**[VERIFIED]** The United Nations Security Council is to vote on a Bahraini resolution to protect commercial shipping in and around the Strait of Hormuz, but veto-wielding China made clear its opposition to authorizing any use of force. Two diplomats said the meeting of the Council's 15 members and the vote were set for Saturday morning, rather than Friday as earlier planned. Friday is a UN holiday. Oil prices have surged since the United States and Israel struck Iran at the end of February, unleashing a conflict that has run for more than a month and effectively closed the key shipping artery.

**[VERIFIED]** The draft resolution has been molded in a bid to rally several countries that have appeared skeptical, including Russia, China and France. Revised wording no longer explicitly invokes Chapter 7 of the UN Charter, which allows the Security Council to authorize armed force to restore peace. The latest version also emphasizes the defensive nature of any intervention — a stipulation that seems to have alleviated French concerns.

**[OFFICIAL]** In remarks to the Security Council on Thursday morning, China's U.N. envoy Fu Cong opposed authorizing force. Such a move would be "legitimizing the unlawful and indiscriminate use of force, which would inevitably lead to further escalation of the situation and lead to serious consequences."

**[VERIFIED]** UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres warned on April 2 that the situation was nearing a breaking point. "We are on the edge of a wider war that could engulf the entire region," he said. "When the strait is strangled, the world's most vulnerable cannot breathe."

**Assessment:** The resolution faces significant headwinds. Even with "defensive-only" language, Russian and Chinese opposition likely means the resolution will either fail or pass without meaningful authorization. This leaves military action to ad hoc coalitions operating without UN mandate.

### Zarif Peace Proposal — First Senior Iranian Call for Deal

**[REPORTED]** Former Iranian Foreign Minister Mohammad Javad Zarif — a diplomat with long experience negotiating with the West who remains close to a pragmatic wing of Iran's leadership — wrote on Friday that the time has come to end the suffering. "Prolonged hostility will cause a greater loss of precious lives and irreplaceable resources without actually altering the existing stalemate," Zarif wrote in Foreign Affairs magazine.

**[REPORTED]** Zarif's proposal includes a series of steps aimed at de-escalating tensions. He suggested that Iran commit to never pursuing nuclear weapons and reduce its stockpile of enriched uranium to below 3.67%, the level set under the 2015 agreement. The plan also calls for the creation of a regional uranium enrichment facility involving China and Russia. In return, Zarif proposed that the U.S. and its allies lift all sanctions on Iran. Additional measures include reopening the Strait of Hormuz and the signing of a nonaggression pact between Iran and the U.S.

**[REPORTED]** Zarif is seen as a relative moderate within the Islamic republic elite, but has no official post in the current government. However this is one of the first times during this conflict that a high profile figure in Iran has called for a deal and an end to the war, with top military and political officials urging daily for fighting to continue until the US is defeated.

**[REPORTED]** While he has no official position in Iran's government, he helped get reformist President Masoud Pezeshkian elected and would likely not have published such a piece without at least some authorization from senior leaders. Immediately after the piece came out, Zarif wrote he had been "torn" about it — a sign he may already face pressure at home.

**Assessment:** This is the first significant public peace proposal from a senior Iranian figure. While Zarif has no official role, his proximity to President Pezeshkian and the timing suggest this may be a trial balloon from moderates. However, his criticism of Kushner and Witkoff as "completely illiterate on both geopolitics and nuclear technicalities" may undercut its reception in Washington.

### Oil Markets: Physical Supply Crisis

**[VERIFIED]** The primary driver behind the current oil price spike is the escalating Middle East conflict involving Iran, which has intensified fears of supply disruption. The Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint for global oil flows, is effectively constrained, threatening over 10% of global supply. Recent U.S. military actions and ongoing uncertainty about conflict resolution have significantly increased the geopolitical risk premium embedded in the crude price.

**[VERIFIED]** Reports from multiple analysts indicate spot/physical Brent prices have reached $141 — the highest since the 2008 financial crisis — while futures trade around $109. This $32+ gap between spot and futures signals acute physical supply shortages not captured in futures markets.

**Assessment:** The divergence between spot and futures prices indicates a physical supply emergency. Real-world oil buyers are paying crisis-level premiums. If spot prices remain elevated, downstream economic effects will accelerate.

### UK Directly Supporting Gulf Defense

**[VERIFIED]** On Friday, Crown Prince Sheikh Sabah Khaled and UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer discussed the deployment of a British ground-based air defence system in Kuwait.

**Assessment:** This represents direct allied military support to a Gulf state under Iranian attack — a significant escalation of UK involvement beyond the diplomatic coordination seen at yesterday's 40-nation summit.
