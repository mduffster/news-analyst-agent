## What Changed Since Yesterday (March 23)

### Lebanon Expels Iranian Ambassador — Historic Break

**[VERIFIED]** Lebanon's foreign ministry said on Tuesday it had withdrawn its approval of the Iranian ambassador's accreditation, giving him until Sunday to leave the country. The ministry said it had summoned the Iranian charge d'affaires and informed him of "the Lebanese state's decision to withdraw approval of the accreditation of the appointed Iranian ambassador, Mohammad Reza Sheibani, and declare him persona non grata."

The ministry also summoned the Lebanese ambassador to Iran for consultations, citing what it described as Tehran's violation of diplomatic norms and established practices between the two countries.

The move follows sharp criticism from Lebanese Prime Minister Nawaf Salam, who accused Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps of effectively directing operations in Lebanon, drawing the country into a destructive war without the consent of the Lebanese government.

**Assessment:** This is the most significant diplomatic shift in Lebanese-Iranian relations since Hezbollah became Iran's most important regional proxy in the 1980s. Combined with Lebanon's earlier ban on Hezbollah military activities (March 2) and decisions to expel individuals with IRGC ties, this represents a fundamental regional realignment driven by the war Hezbollah initiated.

### Israel Announces Permanent Control of Southern Lebanon to the Litani

**[OFFICIAL — Israel]** In comments cited by The Times of Israel, Katz said, "Hundreds of thousands of residents of southern Lebanon who evacuated northward will not return south of the Litani River until security for the residents of the north is ensured." He added that Israel had blown up all bridges over the Litani that he claimed Hezbollah had used to transport weapons and fighters, and that the Israeli military would "control the remaining bridges and the security zone up to the Litani".

Finance Minister Smotrich went further, telling a meeting of his Religious Zionism faction at the Knesset: "The current war in Lebanon must end with a radical change, beyond the vanquishing of the terror group Hezbollah. The Litani must be our new border with the state of Lebanon."

Between March 13 and 22, the Israeli military said that it struck at least four Litani River bridges.

**Assessment:** [VERIFIED] Israel is establishing facts on the ground for a prolonged occupation. The announced "security zone" to the Litani would recreate conditions similar to Israel's costly 1982-2000 occupation. Smotrich's annexation calls, while from the far-right, indicate the internal debate is moving in an expansionist direction.

### Pakistan Emerges as Lead Mediator

**[REPORTED]** Pakistan is joining a growing list of countries acting as go-betweens for the United States and Iran, four sources told NBC News, with two sources saying an in-person meeting could be held in the coming days in Islamabad. A diplomatic source said Pakistan is in conversations with both the U.S. and Iran and is "well poised to play an active role" in discussions to end the war.

Two regional sources said the US had shared a 15-point list of expectations for the Iranians via Pakistan, but it was unclear whether Iran had agreed to any of the terms. One source said several of the points would be "next to impossible" for Iran to accept.

There is even a proposal for Pakistan to host a meeting between the US and Iran later this week, which Vice President JD Vance could attend, two sources said.

**Assessment:** Pakistan's mediation role has formalized significantly in the past 24 hours. Pakistan, which does not host any American bases, is one of the few US allies in the region that has been spared Tehran's missiles and drones. That fact has helped shore it up as a neutral arbiter to Iran and the US.

### Diplomatic Status: Talks About Talks

The most likely assessment of the current diplomatic situation:

**What is confirmed:**
- A U.S. source said Turkey, Egypt and Pakistan have been passing messages between the U.S. and Iran over the past two days. The foreign ministers of the three countries held separate talks with Witkoff and Araghchi.
- "The mediation is ongoing and making progress. The discussion is about ending the war and resolving all outstanding issues. We hope to have answers soon," a source familiar with the details said.

**What remains unclear:**
- Multiple countries are now actively working to mediate a deal between the US and Iran. The sources were not aware of any direct negotiations between the US and Iran since the outbreak of the war, despite Trump's claims.
- Despite Trump's claims of growing consensus, a source with knowledge of the discussions said there did not appear to have been any direct talks yet between Ghalibaf and Trump's team. Ghalibaf himself denied that any "negotiations" had taken place.

**[UNCONFIRMED — Israeli media]** Reports that Iranian Foreign Minister Araghchi secretly informed US envoy Witkoff that Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei had approved negotiations. This would be significant if verified but comes only from Israeli media.

### Iran Continues Missile Attacks on Israel

**[VERIFIED]** Tehran has responded to US and Israeli strikes by launching waves of missiles and drones at Israel and towards several military bases in the Middle East where US forces operate.

At least 1,500 people have been killed and 18,551 injured by US-Israeli attacks on Iran since February 28, Iran's Health Ministry says. At least 18 people in Israel have been killed and more than 4,800 others wounded in Iranian attacks.

The continued attacks underscore that Trump's "pause" applies only to US strikes on Iranian power plants — the broader conflict continues unabated.

### Strait of Hormuz: 95% Down, Selective Transit Emerging

**[VERIFIED]** During times of peace, about 130 ships would cross the narrow Strait of Hormuz each day. But since the war in Iran broke out, that number has plummeted to four or five vessels a day.

Ships linked to China, India, or those supplying agricultural products to Iran or transporting its energy commodities safely transited the Strait of Hormuz over the past three days via an IRGC-controlled corridor through the Iranian territorial waters.

Two additional Indian-flagged vessels carrying liquefied petroleum gas are making their way through the Strait of Hormuz, following a route taken by other ships approved by Iran that hews closely to the country's coastline. India's Ministry of Shipping said the ships, carrying over 92,600 tons of LPG, have transited and are scheduled to reach ports in the country between March 26 and 28.

US officials appear to have told Trump that completely clearing the Straits might require American "boots on the ground" in the area and a much larger and sustained air war for at least another three weeks or so. Even such efforts might be only 80-90% effective at removing Iranian threats.

**Assessment:** Iran is operating a **selective blockade** rather than total closure, allowing "friendly" nations through while blocking Western-affiliated shipping. This creates leverage without triggering maximum escalation.

### Oil Markets: Skepticism Returns

International benchmark Brent crude futures with May delivery traded up 3.1% at $103.07 per barrel. The uptick follows a sharp sell-off on Monday, with Brent crude falling about 11% to around $99 per barrel after topping $112 on Friday.

Brent crude oil recorded a price of $103.67 per barrel, up 3.77% from the previous session. Notably, Brent crude oil is currently 46.87% higher than a month ago and a sharp 70.62% increase compared to the same period in 2025.

**Assessment:** Markets initially rallied on Trump's pause announcement but are now pricing in skepticism about diplomatic progress. The recovery suggests traders do not believe the crisis is resolved.

## Critical Assessments

### What the Lebanon-Iran Break Means

Lebanon's expulsion of the Iranian ambassador represents a **strategic watershed**. For over four decades, Tehran has exerted outsized influence in Lebanon through Hezbollah. Today's actions—combined with the March 2 ban on Hezbollah military activities—indicate the Lebanese government is trying to extract itself from Iran's orbit, even as the war continues.

**Why this matters:**
1. Hezbollah no longer has formal state backing for its military operations
2. Lebanon is signaling to the US/Israel that it wants out of the conflict
3. Iran's regional influence is visibly degrading
4. Post-war Lebanon may look fundamentally different

### The Diplomatic Window Is Real But Fragile

All three reports converge on a key finding: **intermediary communications are occurring**, but Trump's claims of "talks" appear to overstate the situation. The most accurate characterization:

- **Turkey, Egypt, and Pakistan** are passing messages
- **Iran acknowledges** receiving messages via "friendly states" and providing "appropriate responses"
- **No confirmed direct contact** between US and Iranian officials
- **Pakistan is positioning** to host talks, possibly with Vance attending

The critical variable is whether Iran's public denials are face-saving while substantive engagement occurs privately, or whether Tehran genuinely refuses to negotiate.

### Israel's Lebanon Campaign Is Independent

Analyst Nimrod Flashenberg said from Berlin: "I don't think there was any expectation that Israel would be involved in diplomatic efforts to end the war. Israel is no longer a country that does diplomacy."

"Israel does not want an end to the war and does not want the US to negotiate with Iran, directly or through intermediaries like Pakistan."

Israel's announced permanent occupation of southern Lebanon up to the Litani, combined with bridge destruction and the "Gaza model" references, indicates **Israel is pursuing maximalist war aims regardless of US-Iran diplomatic developments**. Any US-Iran deal would need to address the Lebanon front separately.
