### What changed since the last report (new since Mar 25)

1) **Israel claims it killed the IRGC Navy commander (and separately the navy intelligence chief) tied to Hormuz pressure**
- Israel said it killed **Alireza Tangsiri**, described as the **head of the IRGC Navy**, in an overnight strike in **Bandar Abbas**; Israel framed him as responsible for operations constraining shipping through the **Strait of Hormuz**. **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/f27e9560d1929732185da658e33d7fdb))  
- AP’s live “latest” also says Israel’s military claimed it killed **Behnam Rezaei**, described as an **IRGC Navy maritime intelligence** figure. **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/f27e9560d1929732185da658e33d7fdb))  
- **Iran had not immediately acknowledged** Tangsiri’s death in the AP report. **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/f27e9560d1929732185da658e33d7fdb))  

**What it means:** This is a **leadership-targeting escalation** aimed at the maritime choke point, likely intended to (a) disrupt IRGC command-and-control around interdictions and (b) signal that Israel will keep hunting senior operators. Practically, it’s unclear this quickly restores throughput: maritime interdiction/harassment can be **decentralized**, and the immediate effect may be **retaliatory pressure** rather than relaxation.

2) **GCC alleges Iran is effectively running a “paid safe-passage” scheme in Hormuz**
- The GCC Secretary-General **Jasem Mohamed al‑Budaiwi** accused Iran of **charging for safe passage** through the Strait, while AP notes Iran is letting only a **“trickle”** of ships through and restricting others it deems linked to the US/Israel effort. **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/f27e9560d1929732185da658e33d7fdb))  

**What it means:** This reframes Hormuz from “closed/open” into a **permissioned corridor with monetized coercion**, which (if it persists) keeps oil/shipping risk premia elevated even without a total blockade. It also increases the odds GCC states push for **more overt coalition enforcement** (escorts, interdiction of IRGC assets, expanded strikes), pulling the conflict toward **regional entanglement**.

3) **Pakistan publicly acknowledges it is relaying US–Iran messages; Islamabad does *not* confirm direct talks**
- Pakistan’s top diplomat **Ishaq Dar** said **indirect US–Iran talks are ongoing via messages relayed by Pakistan**. **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/f27e9560d1929732185da658e33d7fdb))  
- Pakistan’s Foreign Ministry said it is working to bring the sides to the table but **did not confirm** whether **direct talks in Islamabad** would occur “later this week,” promising details “in due course.” **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/f27e9560d1929732185da658e33d7fdb))  

**What it means:** This is a modest but real upgrade in **process credibility** (a named senior Pakistani figure openly owning the channel), but it also **walks back** the earlier “imminent in-person” vibe: expect **message traffic** to continue even if a meeting slips.

4) **Trump hardens public messaging as oil jumps; AP reports updated casualty figures via Al Jazeera**
- Trump publicly urged Iran to “**get serious soon**” on negotiations and again claimed Iran is “begging” for a deal. **[OFFICIAL]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/f27e9560d1929732185da658e33d7fdb))  
- AP reports **Brent crude** rose above **$100** and markets swung on reduced ceasefire optimism. **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/f27e9560d1929732185da658e33d7fdb))  
- An Iranian deputy health minister told **Al Jazeera** the Iran death toll reached **at least 1,937** with **24,800 wounded** (AP notes Iran hasn’t issued official tallies in days). **[REPORTED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/f27e9560d1929732185da658e33d7fdb))  

**What it means:** The politics/markets loop is tightening: higher oil prices raise urgency for some form of **shipping-risk reduction**, but today’s headline operational move (targeting IRGC navy leadership) points toward **coercion first**, deal later.
