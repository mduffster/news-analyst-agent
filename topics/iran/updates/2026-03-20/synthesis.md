## What Changed in the Last 24 Hours

### Kharg Island Occupation Under Active Consideration

**[REPORTED — Multiple Sources]** An operation to take over Kharg Island, which sits 15 miles offshore and processes 90% of Iran's crude oil exports, could put U.S. troops more directly in the line of fire. Thus, such an operation would only be launched after the U.S. military further degrades Iran's military capacity around the Strait of Hormuz.

"We need about a month to weaken the Iranians more with strikes, take the island and then get them by the balls and use it for negotiations," a source with knowledge of White House thinking said.

President Trump is weighing a seizure of Iran's critical oil depot on Kharg Island in a bid to force Tehran's hand and open the Strait of Hormuz. "I think that U.S. forces — which I think are very capable of seizing Kharg Island – could be at risk of coming under fire here. It is within the range of certain kinds of weapon systems in Iran," said Clayton Seigle, an energy expert at CSIS.

**Assessment:** This is the most significant development since the energy war escalation. A Kharg Island operation would represent the first direct US ground forces on Iranian territory, crossing a threshold that could fundamentally alter the conflict's character.

### Iran Continues Gulf Strikes Despite Israeli Energy Pause

**[VERIFIED]** Kuwait says its Mina Al-Ahmadi refinery again hit in Iranian drone attacks, starting fire.

Countries across the Middle East intercepted drones and missiles early Friday morning, including Iran, Israel, Saudi Arabia, the United Arab Emirates, Bahrain and Kuwait.

On 20 March, Saudi Arabia intercepted a missile over the northern region of Al Jouf.

**Assessment:** Iran is not accepting Trump's conditional framework from yesterday. Despite Netanyahu's commitment to halt energy strikes, Iran continues attacks on Gulf infrastructure—suggesting Tehran views the energy war as leverage.

### Netanyahu Claims War May End Soon

**[OFFICIAL — Israel]** Netanyahu spoke to the media, saying that Israel was helping the U.S. open the Strait of Hormuz. Netanyahu also said that Iran had lost the ability to enrich uranium and make ballistic missiles. The prime minister said the war may end sooner than people think.

**Assessment:** Netanyahu's claim contradicts the operational reality of Iran's continued strikes and the Pentagon's $200 billion supplemental request. His statement likely aims to manage domestic expectations rather than reflect actual war-termination prospects.

### Oil Prices Retreat from Yesterday's Peak but Remain Elevated

**[VERIFIED]** The current price of Brent Oil futures is 110.95, with a previous close of 107.38. Today's trading range for Brent Oil futures is between 109.21 and 119.11. The 52-week price range for Brent Oil futures spans from 58.40 to 119.50.

Oil prices dipped early Friday after Netanyahu said Israel will halt strikes on Iran's key energy sites. But Goldman Sachs has warned that energy prices will stay elevated and that a barrel of oil may cost more than $100 all the way through 2027.

### Congressional Opposition to $200 Billion Grows

**[VERIFIED]** Plenty more Republicans told CNN they are increasingly anxious about whether the US is being dragged into an "endless war" that Trump himself ran against. Several said they would only consider the Iran funding request if the White House better explains its plans—including the possibility of thousands of US troops being sent to the Middle East. "What are we doing? We're talking about boots on the ground. We're talking about that kind of extended activity. Now we're in a whole 'nother zip code," said Rep. Chip Roy.

GOP Rep. Thomas Massie of Kentucky added: "It begs the question, how long do they plan to be there? What are the goals? Is this the first $200 billion?"

Even House Speaker Mike Johnson, in brief comments to CNN on Thursday, declined to endorse the figure. Johnson said instead that he'd look at the details when they are offered. Sen. Ruben Gallego, an Arizona Democrat, posted what has been the position of most Democrats for weeks: "The answer is a simple no."

Opinion polls show that the war is not popular, with only about one in four Americans supporting it.

### Marine Reinforcements En Route to Gulf

**[VERIFIED]** The United States is deploying roughly 2,500 Marines aboard up to three amphibious warships from the Indo-Pacific toward the Middle East as fighting with Iran enters a more uncertain phase. The move adds a mobile expeditionary force to the more than 50,000 U.S. troops already in the region.

Once they arrive, the marines could be used in several ways—but their most likely mission would be helping to reopen the strait, perhaps by seizing small islands near the shipping lanes and setting up air-defence systems.

### UN Pushes for Humanitarian Corridor for Stranded Seafarers

**[VERIFIED]** The head of the UN's International Maritime Organization wants to work with GCC countries on a "safe-passage framework" to evacuate 20,000 seafarers from the Gulf. Speaking to AGBI, secretary-general Arsenio Dominguez said he will begin talks with GCC countries from Monday after Ramadan. Calling for a coordinated, internationally recognised approach, the UN shipping regulator chief said urgent work was needed to ensure safe transit.

By the IMO's estimates, roughly 20,000 seafarers across about 3,200 vessels remain stuck in the Gulf, dealing with growing security risks, operational bottlenecks, and mounting fatigue as the conflict drags on.

### European Nations Signal Hormuz Readiness with Major Caveats

**[VERIFIED]** In a joint statement, the United Kingdom, France, Germany, Italy, the Netherlands and Japan said they are prepared to join "appropriate efforts" to ensure safe passage through the Strait of Hormuz.

**[OFFICIAL — Italy]** Defence sources in Rome have made it clear that any Italian participation in a mission with other countries to safeguard commercial shipping through the Strait of Hormuz could only take place under the auspices of the UN. Defence Minister Guido Crosetto clarified that "Europe is perceived as an ally of the United States" and "a European fleet would be perceived as a NATO fleet, and therefore as a fleet entering the war alongside the United States and Israel."

**Assessment:** The European statement is diplomatic signaling rather than operational commitment. Italy's insistence on a UN mandate (which Russia and China would likely veto) effectively conditions participation on a scenario that may not materialize.

### Lebanon Death Toll Crosses 1,000

**[VERIFIED]** The number of people killed in Lebanon since Israel renewed widespread attacks on the country now stands at 1,001, according to Lebanon's Health Ministry. Among those killed are at least 118 children. More than 2,584 people have also been wounded.

A fifth of the population of Lebanon has been uprooted from their homes in just two weeks, according to an official with the United Nations Refugee Agency, leaving families struggling to find shelter during the conflict between Israel and Hezbollah.

### Iranian Leadership Vacuum Persists

**[VERIFIED]** As noted in the prior reports, Iran has not named replacements for the vast majority of senior officials killed by Israeli strikes since the conflict began on February 28. No successors have been named for more than a dozen senior positions, including: the chief of the military's General Staff; the head of the National Security Council; the intelligence minister; and the head of the Basij.

**Assessment:** The leadership vacuum complicates both military coordination and any potential ceasefire negotiations. Who has authority to commit Iran to a deal remains unclear.

## Critical Dynamics

### Kharg Island: The New Center of Gravity

The Kharg Island deliberations represent the most significant escalation consideration since the energy war began. Kharg Island generates $78 billion a year in energy revenue, with irreplaceable deep water berths no other Iranian port can replicate. Seizure would constitute an "economic knockout" but would place US forces within range of Iranian mainland weapons systems.

Kharg sits roughly 400 miles from the Strait, meaning seizing it alone may not restore shipping traffic through the chokepoint. Taking the island could trigger retaliatory Iranian strikes against oil facilities and pipelines across Gulf countries, particularly Saudi Arabia.

### The Funding Constraint Is Real

Democrats are not expected to support such a Pentagon funding boost for a war they oppose, leading Republicans to eye fulfilling the request through the special budget reconciliation process, which bypasses the need for Democratic support to clear the 60-vote threshold in the Senate. But to do that, Republicans must get near-unanimous support in a slim House GOP majority—a tall task.

The cost of the war in Iran continues to rapidly climb as the conflict nears the end of its third week, with the bombing campaign costing more than $1 billion a day, according to some accounts. Adding $200 billion more to the military's budget would represent a major expenditure, considering the entire military budget for fiscal 2026 was $1 trillion.

### The $200 Billion Signal: Mission Expansion, Not Conclusion

Early indications suggest that the war will be the most expensive for the US since the long conflicts in Iraq and Afghanistan. Administration officials told lawmakers that the first six days of the Iran war had cost more than $11 billion.

The U.S. has so far struck more than 7,000 targets across Iran, Hegseth said, while signaling that the operations would only increase in the days to come. "Today, will be the largest strike package yet, just like yesterday was."
