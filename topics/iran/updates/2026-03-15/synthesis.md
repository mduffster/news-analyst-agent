Both reports converge on the core assessment: **the war has entered a pattern that is devastating but not decisive.** The US and Israel are systematically destroying Iran's military infrastructure, but the regime hasn't collapsed. Iran's retaliatory capacity is heavily degraded but not eliminated — enough to keep Gulf states under fire, Hormuz closed, and oil above $100.

**On the end-state:** Both reports identify a fundamental contradiction between US rhetoric (Wright's "few weeks" timeline) and operational reality (Israel's three-more-weeks plan, Iran's rejection of negotiations). The synthesis view is that the most likely mechanism for ending the high-intensity phase is **unilateral declaration rather than negotiation** — Trump declares objectives achieved, scales back, and pivots to Hormuz escort operations. Both sides claim victory. This aligns with GPT's "prolonged regional entanglement" trajectory.

**Critical escalation risk:** Claude's report emphasizes Kharg Island as the single most dangerous decision point. Trump's "just for fun" comment about striking Kharg again, combined with Araghchi's threat to retaliate against all regional oil facilities if Iran's energy infrastructure is hit, creates a potential escalation ladder into truly catastrophic territory (global recession, $150+ oil). GPT's framework captures this as the mechanism by which "weeks become months."
