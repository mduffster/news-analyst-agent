### Analysis

The war has settled into a pattern that is devastating but not yet decisive. The US and Israel are systematically destroying Iran's military infrastructure, but the regime hasn't collapsed. Iran's retaliatory capacity is >90% degraded but not eliminated — enough to keep Gulf states under fire, Hormuz closed, and oil above $100. Lebanon is a full second front with nearly 800 dead. The global economy is absorbing the largest oil disruption in history.

The most likely near-term trajectory: the next 2-3 weeks see continued intense strikes (Israel's plan), followed by a gradual US pivot toward declaring objectives "substantially achieved." The mechanism for ending the war is not negotiation — Araghchi has shut that door. It's exhaustion and unilateral declaration. Trump declares victory, scales back strikes, attempts to reopen Hormuz with naval escorts. Iran claims survival as victory. Both sides claim they won. The low-level conflict continues indefinitely — periodic strikes, proxy warfare, economic warfare — but the high-intensity phase winds down by early-to-mid April.

**The risk that prevents this: Kharg Island's oil infrastructure.** Trump threatened it. Iran said attacking it would trigger strikes on all regional oil facilities. If Trump hits Iranian oil, Iran hits Saudi/UAE oil, and the global economy enters recession. That's the escalation ladder that could break the pattern and push this into truly catastrophic territory. Trump's "just for fun" comment about Kharg suggests he doesn't fully appreciate — or doesn't care about — the consequences. That single decision point may matter more than everything else combined.
