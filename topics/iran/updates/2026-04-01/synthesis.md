Iranian officials have rejected those claims, saying no such request was made. This represents a hardening from yesterday's report that he might end the war without Hormuz resolution.

2. **[OFFICIAL] Trump to address nation tonight at 9 PM ET.** President Trump is set to address the nation on the Iran war at 9 p.m. Eastern time on Wednesday night, with White House press secretary Karoline Leavitt saying he would be providing "an important update." The news comes after Trump shared an updated timeline for the end of the war on Tuesday, telling reporters in the Oval Office that he expects it to be over within "two weeks, maybe three."

The 21-member crew of the tanker, contracted by state-owned QatarEnergy, were evacuated and no casualties were reported. Kuwait's civil aviation authority said Wednesday that the Gulf state's international airport had come under an Iranian drone attack that led to "a large fire" at fuel tanks.

4. **[VERIFIED] Israel kills senior Hezbollah commander in Beirut; seven dead in overnight strikes.** At least seven people have been killed in Israeli attacks on Beirut's southern suburbs and a nearby town, according to Lebanon's Ministry of Public Health. An Israeli raid hit Beirut's Jnah area, killing at least five people and injuring 21. A separate strike late on Tuesday hit Khaldeh, just south of the capital, killing two people and injuring three.

5. **[VERIFIED] Oil drops sharply — Brent falls to ~$105.** At 8:15 a.m. Eastern Time today, the price of oil sits at $104.86 per barrel, using Brent as the benchmark. That's a decrease of $5.83 since yesterday morning and roughly $30 more than at this time last year. Markets are reacting to potential de-escalation signals.

6. **[VERIFIED] American journalist kidnapped in Iraq by suspected Iran-backed militia.** An American journalist was kidnapped in Iraq on Tuesday by suspected Iranian-backed militants, according to the State Department and the country's Interior Ministry. An individual believed to have been involved in the kidnapping had been captured and was in Iraqi custody. This person had ties to the Iranian-aligned militia group Kataib Hezbollah.

## What Changed Since March 31 (Day 32)

### Trump's Contradictory Signals Continue

**[OFFICIAL]** Trump's Hormuz demand represents a significant reversal from yesterday's WSJ report that he was willing to end the war without the strait reopening. On Truth Social, Trump wrote: "We will consider when Hormuz Strait is open, free, and clear. Until then, we are blasting Iran into oblivion."

**[OFFICIAL]** In response, Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) said the strait was "fully" under its control.

**[REPORTED]** In an interview with Al Jazeera, Iranian Foreign Minister Araghchi acknowledged receiving direct messages from U.S. Mideast envoy Steve Witkoff. He insisted, however, that there were no direct negotiations and said Iran has no faith that talks with the U.S. could yield any results, saying "the trust level is at zero."

**[VERIFIED]** The U.S. has presented Iran with a 15-point plan aimed at bringing about a ceasefire, which includes a demand for the strait to be reopened. Iran's own five-point response includes it retaining sovereignty over the waterway.

**Assessment:** Trump's policy messaging is now dangerously incoherent. Within 24 hours, he signaled willingness to end without Hormuz reopening, then demanded Hormuz as a precondition for ceasefire. Tonight's address may clarify direction — or deepen confusion.

### Iran Expands Gulf Attacks

**[VERIFIED]** A cruise missile struck an oil tanker off the coast of Qatar early Wednesday after Iran launched three missiles toward the country. Air defences intercepted two of the incoming missiles, while the third hit the vessel in Qatar's northern territorial waters. The tanker, identified as the Aqua 1, was operating under contract to QatarEnergy. All 21 crew members onboard were evacuated safely, and no casualties were reported.

**[VERIFIED]** A fully-loaded Kuwaiti oil tanker came under attack off Dubai the day before, one of more than 20 ships attacked by Iran during the war.

**[VERIFIED]** In the United Arab Emirates, a person was killed when a drone was intercepted and debris hit him while he was working on a farm in Fujairah.

**[VERIFIED]** Bahrain sounded two alerts for incoming missiles, while Kuwait's state-run KUNA news agency said a drone hit a fuel tank at Kuwait International Airport, sparking a large fire.

**Assessment:** Iran is widening its target set across the Gulf. The Qatar tanker strike is significant — Iran is now attacking vessels in the territorial waters of GCC states that have tried to remain neutral. This makes maritime normalization harder even if Hormuz nominally reopens.

### Lebanon: Senior Hezbollah Commander Killed, Fierce Clashes Continue

**[VERIFIED]** The Israeli military says it aimed to kill a senior Hezbollah commander as it launched its latest attacks on Beirut's suburbs. The IDF confirmed killing Hajj Yusuf Ismail Hashem, commander of Hezbollah's Southern Front, in a naval strike.

**[VERIFIED]** Hezbollah on Wednesday claimed cross-border attacks against Israel and said its fighters were engaged in "fierce clashes" with soldiers in the Lebanese town of Shamaa, about 5km (3 miles) from the border.

**[VERIFIED]** At least 50 people have been killed in Israeli attacks on Lebanon in the past 24 hours, the country's health ministry said. Yesterday, at least 1,268 people had been killed in Israeli strikes in Lebanon since March 2, while today this figure jumped to 1,318.

**[OFFICIAL]** Israeli Defense minister Israel Katz states that the military will control a "security zone" up to the Litani River in southern Lebanon until the threat of Hezbollah is removed.

**[VERIFIED]** At least 10 Israeli soldiers have been reported killed since the latest round of fighting with Hezbollah flared up. Three United Nations peacekeepers from the UN Interim Force in Lebanon (UNIFIL) were also killed in southern Lebanon this week.

**Assessment:** The killing of Hezbollah's Southern Front commander represents a significant tactical blow, but fierce clashes continue. Israel's explicit commitment to permanent occupation up to the Litani creates a separate, potentially prolonged conflict track that may continue regardless of any US-Iran resolution.

### Houthis Now Actively Engaged — Third Barrage at Israel

**[VERIFIED]** On 28 March 2026, the Houthis in Yemen joined the 2026 Iran war by launching ballistic missiles against Israel. The Houthis said that they had launched ballistic missiles targeting "sensitive" military sites in Israel, and vowed to continue the attacks "until the aggression on all resistance fronts stops."

**[VERIFIED]** Iran-backed Houthi rebels in Yemen launched ballistic missiles at southern Israel, as the US and Israel continue to strike Tehran.

**[REPORTED]** The Houthis did not rule out the closure of the Bab el-Mandeb—which connects the Red Sea and the Gulf of Aden—warning that such option is "likely."

**[REPORTED]** In early March 2026, daily transits fell to the low 20s on some days — a drop of more than half as major carriers shifted routes almost overnight. The missile launch may prove a one-off act of defiance, but the risk is that it signals a new phase of Houthi involvement. If attacks on shipping resume in earnest, the Suez Canal could see further collapse in traffic.

**Assessment:** The Houthis have transitioned from threats to action with at least three missile barrages at Israel. The critical question is whether they resume Red Sea shipping attacks, which would create a "dual chokepoint" crisis on top of Hormuz.

### Trump Threatens NATO Withdrawal

**[OFFICIAL]** President Donald Trump says he is strongly considering pulling the United States out of NATO, the military alliance that has been a cornerstone of the international order since the end of World War II, after other members failed to join his war on Iran.

**[OFFICIAL]** UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer responded: "I have to act in our national interests. This is not our war."

**Assessment:** This opens a potentially significant secondary crisis. NATO allies have provided defensive support but refused to join offensive operations. Trump's frustration may be tactical pressure or genuine policy shift — either way, it adds alliance strain atop the regional crisis.

### American Journalist Kidnapped in Iraq

**[VERIFIED]** American journalist Shelly Kittleson was abducted in Baghdad as Iraqi forces pursue suspects.

**[VERIFIED]** The State Department said a suspect taken into custody by Iraqi authorities had ties to the pro-Iranian militia Kataib Hezbollah.

**[REPORTED]** Trump administration sources confirmed to Al-Monitor that they were aware of the threat against her and had advised her against traveling to Iraq.

**Assessment:** This kidnapping by an Iran-aligned militia adds a hostage dimension to the conflict, complicating any de-escalation negotiations.

### Oil Prices Drop Sharply on De-escalation Hopes

**[VERIFIED]** Oil fell below $100 per barrel and Asian shares were sharply higher today after U.S. stocks soared to their best day in almost a year on renewed hopes that the Iran war could soon end. South Korea's Kospi recovered its losses from earlier this week, surging 8.4%.
