## Daily incremental update — US–Israel–Iran conflict  
**As of Thu, May 7, 2026 (ET)**  
**What changed since Wed, May 6, 2026 (ET)**

### 1) Iran moved to *institutionalize* control of Hormuz transits (vetting + “taxing”)
- Iran has **created a government agency to vet and tax vessels** seeking to pass through the Strait of Hormuz, per AP (citing a shipping data company), while Tehran says it’s **reviewing the latest U.S. proposals**. **[VERIFIED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/fdc6d2ae9396377919c967746fa9996b?utm_source=openai))  

**What it means:** This is an escalation in *governance*, not just harassment: Iran appears to be trying to convert wartime leverage into a standing “permissioned” transit regime. That **hardens the bargaining problem** with the U.S. blockade still in place—because even if escort operations remain paused, commercial confidence won’t return if shipowners believe they face (a) Iranian “fees/permissions” risk and (b) U.S. sanctions/interdiction risk simultaneously.

---

### 2) Deal track: “one-page memo / interim framework” is now being framed more explicitly as temporary and incomplete
- Axios reporting indicates the White House is **waiting on Iran’s response** to a **one-page MOU** framework, with Iran saying it is still **reviewing** and hasn’t responded to Pakistani mediators. **[REPORTED]** ([axios.com](https://www.axios.com/2026/05/06/trump-iran-war-deal-framework-response?utm_source=openai))  
- Reuters-described reporting (via a Reuters pickup) characterizes the effort as a **temporary agreement to halt fighting** while leaving major disputes unresolved, and tied to stabilizing Hormuz shipping. **[REPORTED]** ([iranintl.com](https://www.iranintl.com/en/202605071506?utm_source=openai))  

**What it means:** The likely “best case” near-term outcome is drifting toward an **interim stopgap** (a “don’t shoot / don’t seize / don’t escalate” memo) rather than a durable settlement. That can reduce immediate kinetic risk, but it also **raises relapse risk** because key structural issues (sanctions, enrichment/nuclear verification, maritime rules, proxies) remain unsettled.

---

### 3) Lebanon lane: Israel struck Beirut (first reported strike there since the ceasefire announcement), claiming a Hezbollah commander target
- Multiple outlets report **an Israeli strike in Beirut’s southern suburbs** (Dahieh/Ghobeiri area), described as the **first such Beirut strike since the ceasefire announcement**, with Israel saying it killed a Hezbollah commander. **[REPORTED]** ([cfr.org](https://www.cfr.org/articles/israel-strikes-beirut-for-the-first-time-since-ceasefire-announcement?utm_source=openai))  

**What it means:** This is a clear **spoiler risk** to the “pause for talks” window: even if U.S.–Iran naval contact is reduced, **Israel–Hezbollah escalation can force linkage** (Iran demanding a Lebanon component; Israel striking to pre-empt; Hezbollah responding). It increases the chance that any U.S.–Iran “one-page” deal must either **explicitly include Lebanon** or collapses quickly under events.

---

### 4) Maritime risk signal: concrete casualties from the CMA CGM ship attack continue to surface
- AP’s live update stream continued reporting on the **CMA CGM San Antonio** being damaged during a Hormuz transit, with injuries reported. **[VERIFIED]** ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/b70f88f5aeef24e3c3e139c52f79c6fe?utm_source=openai))  
- Separate national reporting (Philippines) says **7 Filipino seafarers were injured**, with some in ICU. **[REPORTED]** ([gmanetwork.com](https://www.gmanetwork.com/news/pinoyabroad/dispatch/986790/filipino-seafarers-drone-attack-ship-hormuz-strait/story/?utm_source=openai))  

**What it means:** The “attack with injured crew” is the kind of incident that **freezes commercial behavior** regardless of diplomatic headlines. It reinforces that the market’s earlier optimism is vulnerable to **single-incident reversals** in insurance/charter decisions.
