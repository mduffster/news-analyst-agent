## Daily Update: US-Israel-Iran Conflict — May 7, 2026 (Day 69)

### Executive Summary

**Iran's response to the US 14-point MOU is expected today, but Tehran says it has "not yet reached a final conclusion." The first confirmed in-person meeting between President Pezeshkian and Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei signals internal consultations are ongoing at the highest levels. Meanwhile, Israel broke the Beirut ceasefire with its first strike on the Lebanese capital since mid-April, killing Radwan Force commander Malek Ballout. Oil prices stabilized around $100-102/barrel after Wednesday's dramatic crash. Iran has formalized its control over the Strait of Hormuz through a new "Persian Gulf Strait Authority" requiring transit permits — a move analysts see as Tehran's attempt to cement wartime gains.**

**Bottom line:** Today is the anticipated deadline for Iran's response, but signals from Tehran suggest deliberations continue. The Pezeshkian-Khamenei meeting indicates a serious internal process is underway, but the outcome remains uncertain. Israel's Beirut strike raises questions about whether Netanyahu is attempting to complicate US-Iran negotiations.

### Critical New Developments (May 7)

#### Pezeshkian Meets Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei — First Confirmed In-Person
**[OFFICIAL]** Iran's president said he held a two-and-a-half-hour meeting with Mojtaba Khamenei, according to local media, marking the first reported in-person meeting between a top Iranian official and the country's new supreme leader. President Masoud Pezeshkian referenced the need to "strengthen unity, trust, and solidarity within the country's leadership structure" as he described a recent meeting with Khamenei.

**[REPORTED]** Khamenei has not been seen publicly since he was inaugurated in March after US-Israeli strikes killed his father and predecessor, the late Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, leading to speculation about his health and whereabouts.

**Assessment:** This is a significant development. The fact that Pezeshkian emphasized "unity" and "solidarity" suggests the meeting was about aligning civilian and religious authority on the US proposal. Combined with yesterday's reports of Pezeshkian clashing with the IRGC, this meeting may represent an attempt to resolve internal disagreements before responding to Washington.

---

#### Iran Still Reviewing US Proposal, Response Expected Today
**[OFFICIAL]** Iran is still reviewing "messages" from the United States via Pakistani mediation and has not yet finalized its response to a US proposal to end the war, Iranian Foreign Ministry spokesperson Esmaeil Baghaei has said. "Iran has not yet reached a final conclusion, and no response has been given to the American side," Tasnim News Agency cited Baghaei as saying. Iran's Foreign Ministry is still reviewing "messages with the mediation of the Pakistani side."

**[REPORTED]** US officials said the back-and-forth with Iran is slow because every message has to travel to and from Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei, who remains in hiding for security reasons.

**[OFFICIAL]** Pakistan is hopeful its mediation efforts between the United States and Iran will soon reach a breakthrough after Iran said it was reviewing the Trump administration's latest proposal. "Our hope and expectation is for an agreement sooner rather than later," Pakistani Foreign Ministry spokesperson Tahir Andrabi told NPR on Thursday.

**Assessment:** The 48-hour window from Wednesday is now closing, but Iran has not delivered a response. This delay could signal continued internal debate, or Tehran may be using the time to negotiate better terms. The Pezeshkian-Khamenei meeting suggests high-level coordination is underway.

#### Trump Sets One-Week Deadline, Then Says "Never a Deadline"
**[OFFICIAL]** US President Donald Trump told Fox News on Wednesday that Iran has one week to respond to Washington's proposed peace deal.

**[OFFICIAL]** Asked directly if there was a deadline for Iran, Trump said there was "never a deadline." "Never a deadline. It'll happen, it'll happen, but never a deadline," Trump said.

**Assessment:** Trump's contradictory messaging continues — setting a one-week deadline in one interview, then saying "never a deadline" hours later. This creates ambiguity about how long Washington will wait.

#### Israel Strikes Beirut, Kills Radwan Force Commander
**[VERIFIED]** Israel said on Thursday it had killed a commander of Hezbollah's elite Radwan force in an airstrike on Beirut a day earlier, the first Israeli attack on the Lebanese capital since a ceasefire agreed last month. The Israeli military said the commander was killed when Israel struck the southern suburbs of Beirut. There was no immediate confirmation from Hezbollah. Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu and Defence Minister Israel Katz announced the attack in a joint statement on Wednesday.

**[REPORTED]** The source close to Hezbollah told AFP on condition of anonymity that "Malek Ballout, the operations commander in the Radwan force" was killed.

**[VERIFIED]** At least 11 other people were killed in strikes across the south and east, according to the Lebanese health ministry. At least 11 people were killed in earlier Israeli strikes on southern and eastern Lebanon.

**[OFFICIAL]** Trump told Axios last week he had asked Netanyahu to conduct only "surgical" strikes in Lebanon. Iran has demanded in talks with the US that Israel halt strikes in Lebanon.

**Assessment:** The timing is notable — Israel struck Beirut just as the US and Iran appear closest to a deal. Given that Iran has demanded a halt to Israeli operations in Lebanon as part of any agreement, this strike could complicate negotiations. Netanyahu may be signaling that Israel maintains freedom of action regardless of US-Iran talks.

#### IDF Claims 220+ Hezbollah Fighters Killed Since Ceasefire
**[OFFICIAL]** The Israel Defense Forces said in a Thursday statement that it had killed more than 220 Hezbollah fighters and commanders over the past few weeks. The IDF said 85 Hezbollah fighters were killed in the past week.

**[VERIFIED]** Since the war began on 2 March, Israeli strikes have killed more than 2,700 people in Lebanon. The Israeli military says it has lost 17 soldiers and a contractor in southern Lebanon.

**Assessment:** Despite the "ceasefire" announced April 17, combat in southern Lebanon has continued intensively. The 220 fighters killed since the truce began underscores that the agreement has not stopped significant military operations.

#### Iran Formalizes Hormuz Control with "Persian Gulf Strait Authority"
**[VERIFIED]** Iran has laid out a set of new rules for ships seeking to cross the Strait of Hormuz, according to a document seen by CNN. Titled "Vessel Information Declaration," the document is an application form issued by Iran's Persian Gulf Strait Authority (PGSA) that must be completed by all transiting vessels to ensure their safe passage.

**[VERIFIED]** Iran has launched an official "Persian Gulf Strait Authority" with its own formal email address, providing owners with a verifiable single window for arranging transit with the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps, a U.S.-designated foreign terrorist organization. According to Islamic of Iran Public Broadcasting, vessels wanting to transit the Strait of Hormuz will receive an email from the official address "[email protected]," which will include information about Iran's transit regulations.

**[REPORTED]** Iran's establishment of the "Persian Gulf Strait Authority" seeks to permanently alter the status of the strait. "Practically, what Iranians are saying is that this is not a temporary new maritime regime; they are talking about a permanent change in the status of the strait, and they say they are not going to give up on that," said Al Jazeera's Resul Serdar Atas, reporting from Tehran.

**Assessment:** Iran is institutionalizing its control over Hormuz even as negotiations proceed. This creates a fait accompli that may complicate any deal — Iran is signaling it won't give up wartime gains regardless of negotiations.

**[VERIFIED]** By 8:45 a.m. Eastern Time today, oil had reached $100.45 per barrel, measured using the Brent benchmark. That's $6.07 less than it cost yesterday morning and about $38.90 above its price a year earlier.

Wholesale gas prices also dropped by 5%, and heating oil, a proxy for jet fuel, fell 6%. Even with Wednesday's drops, the price of oil is still up more than 65% since the start of the year.

**Assessment:** Oil prices have stabilized around $100-102/barrel after Wednesday's ~$16 crash. Markets appear to be in "wait and see" mode pending Iran's response. Prices remain about $16 below pre-escalation levels (May 5: $116.55) but well above pre-war levels (~$62).

#### Netanyahu Insists on "Full Coordination" with US
**[OFFICIAL]** Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu and his team held several calls with Trump and his advisers on Wednesday to discuss the U.S.-Iran negotiations. "There is full coordination between us, there are no surprises. We share common goals, and the most important goal is the removal of the enriched material from Iran — all the enriched material — and the dismantling of Iran's enrichment capabilities," Netanyahu said in a statement. Netanyahu said Trump believes he can achieve that "one way or another," and added that he had ordered the IDF to be ready if no deal is reached and the war resumes.

**[REPORTED]** An Israeli official told Army Radio on Wednesday that Jerusalem had not been aware that Trump was nearing an agreement with Iran. "We were preparing for an escalation," the official said. Netanyahu's office rushed to deny that report, issuing a statement attributed to a senior Israeli official insisting that the premier was not caught off guard and is in constant contact with Trump.

**Assessment:** Despite Netanyahu's public statements about "full coordination," the leaked comments that Israel "was preparing for an escalation" suggest some surprise at the diplomatic turn. Netanyahu's subsequent Beirut strike may have been designed to reassert Israel's autonomy from US diplomatic plans.

#### ~1,600 Ships Still Stranded in Strait of Hormuz
**[VERIFIED]** About 1,600 ships are still stuck in the Strait of Hormuz, with shipping companies facing an expensive and risky situation, looking for windows of opportunity to leave the waterway for more than two months. President Donald Trump's operation to "guide" ships through the strait lasted just 48 hours. Only two ships were guided through. Now, on their own again, companies are unwilling to bear the risk of transit.

**[VERIFIED]** On 6 May 2026, 7 vessels transited the Strait of Hormuz — 5 inbound and 2 outbound. Inbound traffic was dominated by Iranian-feeder general cargo, with India- and Comoros-flagged tonnage accounting for three of the seven crossings.

**Assessment:** Commercial transit remains at a trickle despite the pause in Project Freedom. Insurance costs and uncertainty keep vessels anchored. Even a deal would take weeks to normalize shipping flows.

### Key Emerging Issues

1. **Pezeshkian-Khamenei meeting as decision point** — The first confirmed meeting between civilian government and supreme leader since war began suggests internal alignment ahead of responding to US proposal. The emphasis on "unity" indicates efforts to bridge Pezeshkian-IRGC divide.

2. **Israel's Beirut strike timing** — First strike on Lebanese capital since mid-April came at peak of US-Iran deal momentum. Iran has demanded halt to Lebanon operations as condition for agreement. Netanyahu may be signaling independence from US diplomatic track.

3. **Iran's PGSA institutionalizes Hormuz control** — The formal permit system represents Iran's attempt to make wartime gains permanent regardless of negotiations. Any deal will need to address this new reality.

4. **MOU may not include key demands** — The sources said the memorandum did not mention several key demands Washington has made in the past, which Iran has rejected, such as the restrictions on Iran's missile program and an end to its support for proxy militias in the Middle East. The sources also made no mention of Iran's existing stockpile of more than 400 kg (900 pounds) of near-weapons-grade uranium — one of Washington's central concerns. This suggests a very narrow deal focused on ending hostilities, with difficult issues deferred.

5. **Market pricing in uncertainty** — Oil price stabilization around $100-102/barrel suggests markets are waiting for Iranian response before making major moves. A positive response could push prices lower; rejection or breakdown could send them back toward $115+.
