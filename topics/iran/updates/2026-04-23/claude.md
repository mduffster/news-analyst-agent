### What Changed Since Yesterday

#### Trump Escalates Mine Warfare Posture

**[OFFICIAL]** "I have ordered the United States Navy to shoot and kill any boat, small boats though they may be (Their naval ships are ALL, 159 of them, at the bottom of the sea!), that is putting mines in the waters of the Strait of Hormuz. There is to be no hesitation," Trump wrote in a post on social media.

**[OFFICIAL]** Trump said that the United States has been working to clear mines from the strait. "Additionally, our mine 'sweepers' are clearing the Strait right now. I am hereby ordering that activity to continue, but at a tripled up level!"

**[OFFICIAL]** "We have total control over the Strait of Hormuz," Trump wrote in another Truth Social post. "No ship can enter or leave without the approval of the United States Navy. It is 'Sealed up Tight,' until such time as Iran is able to make a DEAL!!!"

**[VERIFIED]** Pentagon officials briefed lawmakers this week on an intelligence assessment that found it could take up to six months to fully clear the Strait of Hormuz of mines after the war with Iran ends.

**Assessment:** Trump's explicit authorization of lethal force against minelaying boats represents a significant escalation in rules of engagement. While previous orders allowed for mine destruction, this is the clearest authorization to date for direct attacks on Iranian personnel engaged in mining operations.

#### US Expands Maritime Interdiction to Indian Ocean

**[OFFICIAL]** The U.S. military on Thursday seized another tanker associated with the smuggling of Iranian oil. The Defense Department released video footage of U.S. forces on the deck of the Guinea-flagged oil tanker Majestic X, which was seized in the Indian Ocean. "We will continue global maritime enforcement to disrupt illicit networks and interdict vessels providing material support to Iran, wherever they operate," a Pentagon statement said. Ship-tracking data showed the Majestic X in the Indian Ocean between Sri Lanka and Indonesia.

**[VERIFIED]** The development marked the second tanker boarding outside the Middle East in recent days as the U.S. blockade on Iran expands into global waters.

**[VERIFIED]** The U.S. has imposed a retaliatory naval blockade on Iranian ports in the region. U.S. Central Command said overnight that it has so far directed 31 ships to turn around or return to port as part of the blockade.

**Assessment:** The seizure of tankers in the Indian Ocean — far from the Strait of Hormuz — demonstrates a significant expansion of US operations. This global interdiction campaign suggests the US is attempting to strangle Iranian oil revenue regardless of geography.

---

#### Strait Traffic Near Standstill After Ship Seizures

**[VERIFIED]** With the US-Israel and Iran war dragging into its eighth week close to 1,000 ships and 20,000 seafarers remain trapped in the Arabian Gulf region. While the US has indefinitely extended its ceasefire with Iran earlier this week the conflict would appear to be at something of a stalemate. The US navy continues to blockade Iranian ports and Iran is maintaining its closure of the Strait of Hormuz.

**[VERIFIED]** Two of the ships the MSC Francesca and Epaminondas were yet to reappear on AIS tracking. The following day it became clear that the reason these two vessels hadn't turned their AIS transponders back on was because they hadn't attempted transit of the strait until 22 April. In quick succession warnings came of attacks on the MSC Francesca, Epaminondas, and a third container ship Euphoria by fast attack boats from the IRGC Navy. The IRGC Navy seized both the MSC Francesca and the Epaminondas... The Euphoria was able to escape and made it to UAE waters.

**Assessment:** Yesterday's IRGC seizure of two ships attempting to run the strait has effectively shut down any remaining commercial traffic. The risk-reward calculus for shipping companies has become untenable.

#### Oil Prices Surge Past $103

**[VERIFIED]** Global crude oil markets have entered a volatile phase, with benchmark prices climbing above the psychologically significant $100-per-barrel mark. The rally has been led by Brent Crude, which rose to $103.38 per barrel, gaining 1.44% in the latest session. Its US counterpart, WTI Crude, also advanced to $94.46, up 1.67%. This marks the fourth consecutive session of gains.

**[VERIFIED]** The ongoing disruption is contributing to severe supply concerns, with estimates of demand destruction nearing 4 to 5 million barrels per day, or about 5 percent of global supply, with Asia most affected.

**Assessment:** Oil has risen $7+ from yesterday's $96 levels. This is the fourth straight day of gains since the ceasefire extension failed to improve the Hormuz situation. Prices are now 53% above pre-war levels.

#### Pentagon Leadership Instability

**[VERIFIED]** Hegseth had a conversation with Trump and informed Phelan he needed to resign or be fired. "President Trump and Secretary Hegseth agreed new leadership at the Navy is needed," one of the sources, a senior administration official, told CNN.

**[VERIFIED]** Multiple sources told CNN there was tension for months between Phelan and Hegseth, who believed Phelan was moving too slowly on implementing shipbuilding reforms and was also irked by Phelan's direct communication with Trump, which Hegseth viewed as an attempt to bypass him. It was during a meeting between Trump and Hegseth on shipbuilding at the White House Wednesday that the issues came to a head.

**[VERIFIED]** The U.S. Navy has a new acting leader after the departure of John Phelan, with Under Secretary Hung Cao stepping into the role, the Pentagon announced Wednesday. No reason was given for Phelan's exit.

**[VERIFIED]** There's the overall scope of the Pentagon purge. Just three weeks before Phelan's ouster, Hegseth also fired his Army chief of staff, Gen. Randy George, the Army's top officer, even as the war in Iran raged on.

**Assessment:** Firing the Navy Secretary during an active naval blockade operation is extraordinary. This is the 34th senior defense official removed under the Trump administration, creating significant leadership instability during wartime operations.

#### Iran Positions and Death Toll

**[OFFICIAL]** Iran's President Masoud Pezeshkian said Tehran seeks "dialogue and agreement", but that "breach of commitments, blockade and threats" are hindering negotiations. Iran's parliament speaker said his country would not reopen the Strait of Hormuz as long as the US naval blockade remained in place, calling the latter a "blatant violation" of the two countries' ceasefire.

**[OFFICIAL]** Trump has not set a deadline by which Iran must submit a peace proposal, the White House said on Wednesday.

**[VERIFIED]** Iran's forensics chief said nearly 3,400 people had been killed in the country since U.S.-Israeli strikes began Feb. 28. More than 2,200 people have been killed in Lebanon, 32 have been killed in Gulf states, and 23 have died in Israel.

**[REPORTED]** The maritime analytics firm Kpler estimated the flow of crude from Iran to China to be 985,000 barrels per day in the first half of April. Since then, this flow has not been interrupted. Meanwhile, at Jask, an Iranian oil export terminal which bypasses the Strait of Hormuz, there is an all-time high of 5.8 million barrels in storage.

**Assessment:** Iran continues to receive some oil revenue through the Jask terminal, which bypasses Hormuz. However, the blockade is constraining overall revenue significantly. The casualty toll approaching 6,000 across the region underscores the human cost of this conflict.

#### Israel-Lebanon Talks Begin Today

**[VERIFIED]** Israeli and Lebanese representatives are expected to convene again at the State Department on Thursday for the second round of historic direct talks.

**[OFFICIAL]** Lebanese President Joseph Aoun said Ambassador Nada Hamadeh Mouawad will represent Lebanon, tasked with formally requesting an extension of the truce and raising objections to Israeli activity in southern Lebanon. Aoun underscored that Lebanon's position allows "no concessions or compromises" unless they contribute to safeguarding sovereignty and national interests.

**[REPORTED]** Israeli strikes in southern Lebanon on Wednesday killed at least five people, including Lebanese journalist Amal Khalil. Lebanese officials said Khalil and another journalist took shelter in a house after a nearby vehicle was targeted, but the building was then struck as well.

**[VERIFIED]** The talks at the State Department come a week after the US announced a 10-day cessation of hostilities aimed at building conditions for "lasting peace". The ceasefire can be extended by "mutual agreement" between Lebanon and Israel if progress is made during talks and if Lebanon "effectively demonstrates its ability to assert its sovereignty".

**[REPORTED]** "Iran is insisting on maintaining its presence and backing Hezbollah in Lebanon. Hezbollah seems to be happy to continue to play their role with Iran."

**Assessment:** The Israel-Lebanon talks take place against a backdrop of continued violations. The killing of a journalist yesterday complicates the diplomatic environment, but both sides have indicated willingness to extend the ceasefire.
